# Research Plan

## Study Planning Details

**Project Title:** VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research - Information Architecture and User Flow Optimization

**Product / Feature Area:** VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features

**What decision will this research inform?** This research will inform the product team's decision on which navigation improvements to prioritize for the mobile app redesign scheduled for Q1 2026. Findings will directly influence decisions about main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes. The research will also guide decisions about whether to implement progressive disclosure, redesign the dashboard layout, or focus on improving discoverability of core features first.

**Primary research goal:** Identify the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that cause 45% of veterans to abandon mobile app tasks, and understand the root causes behind the current 4.2/10 user satisfaction rating.

## Methodology Selection

**Selected Methods:**
* ✅ **Card Sorting** - To understand veteran mental models for organizing app features and content
* ✅ **Usability Testing** - To observe navigation behavior and identify friction points in task completion  
* ✅ **Competitive Analysis** - To benchmark against other government mobile apps and healthcare platforms
* ⚪ User Interviews - May include brief post-task interviews during usability sessions
* ⚪ Survey Research - May follow as phase 2 for quantitative validation of findings
* ⚪ Concept Testing - Not applicable for current navigation research

**Target Participants:**
* Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
* Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
* Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
* Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
* Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs

**Number of Participants:**
* **Card Sorting:** 8 participants (45-minute hybrid sessions)
* **Mobile Usability Testing:** 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

**Timeline Preference:** ⚪ Rush (2-3 weeks) ✅ **Standard (4-6 weeks)** ⚪ Extended (7+ weeks)

*Rationale: Standard timeline allows for proper recruitment of active mobile app users with diverse accessibility needs, adequate time for card sorting analysis, and coordination with mobile development team schedules for implementation planning.*
