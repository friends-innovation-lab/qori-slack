# Research Summary

Source: Disability Rights Technology Coalition
Study Period: March 2024 - June 2024

Participants: 156 government service users with disabilities

Investigation into accessibility barriers preventing disabled citizens from successfully using government mobile applications and digital services.

# Key Findings:

**Screen Reader Users (n=47):**

- 71% reported unlabeled buttons as primary barrier
- 64% experienced form validation issues not announced
- 58% struggled with data table navigation
- Average task completion time: 340% longer than sighted users

**Motor Impairment Users (n=39):**

- 83% had difficulty with small touch targets
- 76% needed alternative to gesture-based navigation
- 62% required longer timeout periods for form completion

**Cognitive Accessibility Needs (n=70):**

- 89% preferred linear, step-by-step processes
- 74% needed clear progress indicators
- 68% required plain language instructions

**Recommendations for Government Apps:**

1. Comprehensive Accessibility Testing: Include disability community in all testing phases
2. Accessible Design Patterns: Use established, tested accessibility patterns
3. Multiple Input Methods: Support various ways to complete tasks
4. Clear Communication: Provide helpful error messages and guidance
