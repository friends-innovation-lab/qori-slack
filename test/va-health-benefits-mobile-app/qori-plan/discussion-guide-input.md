### What are you researching? *
```
VA Health and Benefits mobile app navigation and information architecture. Testing whether veterans can efficiently complete common tasks like prescription refills, appointment scheduling, and benefits status checks. Current app has 4.2/10 satisfaction rating with 45% task abandonment. Need to identify navigation pain points and mental models for Q1 2026 redesign.
```

### What specific questions do you need answered? *
```
- How do veterans mentally organize health vs benefits information?
- Where do veterans expect to find prescription refills and appointment scheduling?
- What causes veterans to abandon tasks and use search instead?
- How do screen reader users navigate the current menu structure?
- Which navigation pattern works better: bottom tabs or hamburger menu?
- What accessibility barriers block task completion?
```

### Who are your participants? *
```
Veterans who use the VA mobile app 2-3 times monthly. Mix of iOS and Android users. Ages 25-75+. At least 50% using assistive technology (VoiceOver, TalkBack, large text). Urban, suburban, and rural locations. Range of tech comfort levels from basic to advanced.
```

### Research method *
```
Usability Testing
```

### Session length (minutes) *
```
60 minutes
```

```
https://mobile.va.gov/app-testing/navigation-study
```
