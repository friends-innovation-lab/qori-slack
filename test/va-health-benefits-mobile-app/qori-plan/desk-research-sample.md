## Source 1: VA Internal Research (2024)

**Veteran Digital Service Usage Patterns**
Report Period: January 2024 - December 2024
Sample Size: 2.3M unique veteran users

### Mobile Usage Statistics
- 78% of VA interactions now occur on mobile devices (up from 52% in 2022)
- Average session duration: 4.2 minutes (mobile) vs 8.7 minutes (desktop)
- Task abandonment rate: 42% mobile vs 28% desktop
- Most common mobile tasks: Prescription refills (89%), appointment viewing (76%), payment checking (61%)

### Demographic Insights
- Veterans 65+: Fastest growing mobile user segment (156% increase)
- Rural veterans: 23% more likely to use mobile-only access
- Veterans with service-connected disabilities: 67% rely primarily on mobile access

### Pain Points Identified
- Navigation Confusion: 34% of support calls related to "can't find feature"
- Form Completion Issues: 28% abandonment rate on multi-step processes
- Search Functionality: Only 12% success rate for finding specific information

## Source 2: Mobile Accessibility Research

**Mobile Accessibility Barriers in Government Services**

### Key Findings
- Screen reader users spend 3x longer completing equivalent tasks
- 45% of accessibility issues stem from unlabeled buttons and form fields
- Touch target sizes below 44x44 pixels cause 60% of motor-impairment errors
- Veterans using assistive technology abandon tasks at 2x the rate of other users

### Recommendations
- All interactive elements must have descriptive labels
- Minimum touch target size: 48x48 pixels
- Consistent navigation placement across all screens
- Support for iOS VoiceOver and Android TalkBack required

## Source 3: Section 508 Compliance Guidelines

### Mobile Application Requirements
- All buttons must have accessible names
- Focus order must be logical and predictable
- Color cannot be the only means of conveying information
- Text must be resizable up to 200% without loss of functionality
- All functionality available via assistive technology
