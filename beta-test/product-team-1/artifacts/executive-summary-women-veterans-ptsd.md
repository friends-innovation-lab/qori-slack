# 🧾 Executive Summary – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study

---

## 🎯 Objective  
This study explored how women Veterans with PTSD discover and navigate mental health resources through VA.gov. The goal was to identify pain points, trust gaps, and opportunities to design for trauma-informed, gender-specific care access online.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights  
1. **Gender-specific content is hard to find** – Women felt PTSD pages lacked relevant representation and language.  
2. **Pages were too dense to process** – Participants struggled to scan or take action during moments of distress.  
3. **Support pathways felt unclear or unsafe** – Unlabeled contact info led to hesitation and abandonment.

---

## 👤 Who We Spoke To  
- 6 Participants  
- All women Veterans, diagnosed or self-identifying with PTSD  
- Age range: 32–58  
- Mixed geography: urban, rural, suburban  
- Mobile-primary (3), desktop (2), tablet (1)

---

## 🛠️ Top Recommendations  
- Add clear links to women-specific PTSD resources on landing pages  
- Redesign content for skimmability with bulleted summaries  
- Label contact numbers clearly with trauma-aware language

---

## 🧠 Why It Matters  
Without clear, inclusive pathways, women Veterans may disengage before reaching care. This undermines VA’s equity goals and puts vulnerable users at risk. Small changes in content structure and tone can significantly improve access and trust.
