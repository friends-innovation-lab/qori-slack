## 🎯 Research Objectives

1. Observe and document how veterans naturally interact with VA services in their real-world environment
2. Identify pain points, workarounds, and unmet needs in veterans' current VA experience workflows
3. Understand the context and environmental factors that influence veterans' decision-making when accessing VA services
4. Capture authentic user behaviors and mental models that may not emerge in artificial testing environments

## 🎯 Main Research Activities

*Adapt these activities to your specific research method.*

### Activity 1: Natural Task Observation

**Setup:**
Ask the participant to continue with their normal activities or start a task they would typically do in this environment.

**Instructions:**
"I'd like you to go about your normal routine or work on something you'd typically be doing right now. Please act as naturally as possible - I'm here to observe and learn from how you normally do things. Feel free to think aloud if it feels natural, but don't worry about explaining everything."

**What to Capture:**
- Environmental factors that influence their behavior
- Interruptions and how they handle them
- Tools and resources they reach for
- Moments of hesitation or difficulty
- Workarounds or shortcuts they use

---

### Activity 2: Contextual Interview During Tasks

**Setup:**
While they work, ask gentle questions about what you're observing without disrupting their natural flow.

**Instructions:**
"As you're working, I might ask some questions about what you're doing or why you're doing it that way. Feel free to keep working while we talk - I want to understand your process in real-time."

**What to Capture:**
- Reasoning behind their choices and actions
- Pain points or frustrations as they occur
- Mental models and assumptions
- Relationships between different parts of their process
- Emotional reactions to various steps or outcomes

## 💭 Post-Session Interview

*Ask these after completing all main activities.*

1. **Overall Impressions**
   "Now that we've gone through your typical process, what stands out to you most about how you currently handle VA-related tasks?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What surprised you as you walked me through this?"

2. **Pain Points**
   "What are the biggest frustrations or roadblocks you encounter in this process?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "Can you tell me about a time when this was particularly challenging?"

3. **Expectations**
   "When you first started using VA services, how did you expect the process to work versus how it actually works?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What would an ideal experience look like to you?"

4. **Mental Model**
   "How do you think about or organize all the different VA services and processes in your mind?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What connections do you see between different VA services?"

5. **Improvements**
   "If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your VA experience, what would it be?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What would that solve for you day-to-day?"

6. **Comparison**
   "How does navigating VA services compare to other government services or healthcare systems you've used?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What do those other systems do better or worse?"

7. **Likelihood to Use**
   "On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to recommend VA services to another veteran? What would make it a 10?"

8. **Anything Else**
   "Is there anything else you'd like to share about your VA experience that I didn't ask about?"
