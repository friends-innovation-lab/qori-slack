## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about improving the Veterans Affairs mobile app's benefits features to better serve Veterans' needs when accessing and managing their benefits on mobile devices. The findings will help Veterans more easily check benefit status, submit claims, and complete benefits-related tasks through their smartphones, reducing the need for phone calls or office visits. For VA, this research will guide mobile app enhancements that can decrease call center volume, improve Veteran satisfaction scores, and increase digital service adoption rates.

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Expand the flagship mobile app

**Explanation:** The Benefits MObile app directly aligns with expanding VA's mobile capabilities to provide Veterans with convenient access to their benefits information on-the-go.

**Additional Priorities:**
- **Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience:** A mobile benefits app can deliver tailored, personalized content and notifications specific to each Veteran's benefits status and eligibility
- **Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims:** Mobile access to benefits information can streamline the claims process by allowing Veterans to easily check status, upload documents, and manage their disability claims from their mobile devices

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The Benefits MObile app serves as a critical digital touchpoint throughout the veteran's entire lifecycle, providing on-the-go access to benefits information, claims status, and essential services from transition through retirement.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Benefits MObile app enables veterans to quickly check disability compensation payments, view benefit letters for medical appointments, and access their benefits information while managing healthcare needs and financial stability during recovery or ongoing treatment.
- **Putting down roots:** Benefits MObile app helps veterans navigate education benefits like GI Bill payments, check home loan eligibility status, and access benefit verification letters needed for employment, housing applications, and establishing credit in their new civilian communities.
- **Retiring:** Benefits MObile app allows aging veterans to monitor their benefits payments, access important documents for estate planning, and stay connected to their earned benefits while managing the transition from working life to full retirement.

**Critical Pain Points:** 
- Limited functionality compared to the full VA.gov website, forcing veterans to switch between mobile and desktop platforms to complete complex tasks
- Inconsistent app performance and frequent login issues that prevent veterans from accessing time-sensitive benefits information when they need it most
- Lack of real-time notifications for critical updates like claim status changes or payment delays, leaving veterans without timely information about their benefits

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Evaluate the user experience and effectiveness of the Benefits MObile app to identify specific pain points and opportunities for improvement that will increase user engagement by 25% and reduce task completion time by 30% within the next quarter.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct user interviews with 15-20 current Benefits MObile app users to understand their workflow patterns, identify friction points during benefits enrollment and claims processes, and gather specific feedback on navigation and feature usability
- Analyze user behavior data from the Benefits MObile app to determine which features are underutilized and understand the primary reasons users abandon tasks before completion, particularly during benefits selection and account management
- Generate a prioritized list of actionable design and functionality recommendations for the Benefits MObile app development team, including wireframes for critical user journey improvements and a roadmap for implementing changes that will directly impact user satisfaction scores

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** User interviews provide deep qualitative insights into veterans' lived experiences with benefits management, uncovering pain points, mental models, and contextual factors that quantitative methods cannot capture. This approach is essential for understanding the complex emotional and practical challenges veterans face when navigating benefits through mobile interfaces.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** Semi-structured 60-minute remote interviews conducted via VA-approved video conferencing platforms, allowing for natural conversation flow while ensuring consistent coverage of key topics across all participants.
- **Key Activities:** Journey mapping exercises where participants walk through their current benefits management process, task-based scenarios using existing benefits tools, card sorting activities to understand information architecture preferences, and retrospective probing about past benefits experiences and mobile app usage patterns.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Behavioral observation of current workflow patterns, emotional response tracking during benefits-related discussions, identification of workaround strategies veterans currently employ, assessment of mobile-specific needs and constraints, and validation of proposed feature concepts through reaction testing.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Requires Collaboration Cycle checkpoint after research plan completion and before recruitment begins, with estimated 2-week timeline for OCTO review and approval of interview guides and screening criteria.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Interview guides structured to avoid re-traumatization by focusing on system interactions rather than underlying conditions, providing participants control over session pacing and topic depth, and ensuring researchers are trained in recognizing signs of distress.
- **Accessibility:** Sessions accommodate screen reader users through verbal description protocols, provide alternative communication methods for participants with hearing impairments, offer flexible scheduling for various time zones and work schedules, and ensure interview materials are available in multiple formats upon request.

## ⚠️ Risk Management

> [!WARNING]
> **High-Risk Areas Requiring Immediate Mitigation**

> [!CAUTION] 
> **Medium-Risk Areas to Monitor Closely**

Here are the top 4 risks for user interview research on the Benefits Mobile app:

**Recruitment Challenges:** Difficulty finding eligible participants who actively use benefits services and are willing to discuss sensitive financial/health information through the Benefits Mobile app
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with HR departments, benefits administrators, and employee resource groups to access target users; offer higher incentives for participation; provide clear privacy assurances about how benefits-related data will be protected

**Execution Issues:** Participants may be reluctant to share honest feedback about benefits experiences due to privacy concerns or fear of impacting their actual benefits coverage
- **Likelihood:** Medium  
- **Mitigation:** Conduct interviews through anonymous sessions; use hypothetical scenarios and task-based testing rather than asking about personal benefits details; clearly separate research from actual benefits administration

**Timeline Risks:** Benefits enrollment periods and company fiscal calendars may limit participant availability or create urgency that compresses research timeline for Benefits Mobile app improvements
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Schedule research well in advance of open enrollment periods; identify backup research windows; prepare rapid synthesis methods to deliver insights quickly during critical benefits decision periods

**Quality Concerns:** Participants may not accurately recall their Benefits Mobile app usage patterns or may confuse experiences across multiple benefits platforms and vendors
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Use screen recording during live app usage rather than relying on recall; ask participants to demonstrate specific tasks within the Benefits Mobile app during interviews; triangulate findings with app analytics data to validate user-reported behaviors
