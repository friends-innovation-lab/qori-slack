## Interview Guide

### Opening (3-5 min)

> *Build rapport and set context*

- Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching claims status navigation and wanted to understand the design constraints better.

---

### Section: Design & Technical Constraints (35 min)

> *Understand limitations that shape design decisions*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | Walk me through the current design system components available for claims status displays. | • What's missing that you wish you had? • Which components cause the most friction? • How flexible are these components? |
| 2 | What are the biggest technical constraints you face when designing claims status interfaces? | • How do these constraints impact user experience? • What workarounds have you had to create? • Which constraints are hardest to design around? |
| 3 | Describe the data limitations that affect how you can present claims information. | • What claims data do you wish you had access to? • How real-time does the status information need to be? • What causes delays in status updates? |
| 4 | How do accessibility requirements shape your design decisions for claims status? | • Which accessibility standards are most challenging? • What assistive technologies do you design for? • How do you handle complex status information for screen readers? |
| 5 | What platform or device constraints impact the claims status experience? | • How does mobile vs desktop affect your approach? • Are there browser limitations you work around? • What about offline scenarios? |
| 6 | Tell me about any regulatory or compliance constraints that influence your designs. | • What information are you required to show? • What can't you show or change? • How do legal requirements conflict with good UX? |
| 7 | What are the biggest integration challenges with backend systems? | • Which systems are hardest to work with? • How do system limitations show up in the interface? • What would ideal system integration look like? |

**Listen for:** Specific technical blockers, workarounds they've created, tension between user needs and system capabilities, areas where they feel most constrained
