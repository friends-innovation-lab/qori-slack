# 👀 Observer Guidelines - VA Research Sessions

**Welcome!** Thank you for supporting VA user research. Your observations help us build better experiences for Veterans and their families.

---

## 📋 General Guidelines (Everyone Reads This)

### 🤐 Confidentiality & Professional Conduct

**Confidentiality is paramount:**
- All participant information is strictly confidential
- Do not discuss participant details outside the research team
- Securely store and share any notes according to VA data policies
- Ask your researcher about proper data handling procedures

**Professional observer conduct:**
- Stay muted/silent throughout the session unless there's an emergency
- Avoid visible reactions that might influence the participant
- Be respectful of participant's time, emotions, and experiences
- Follow trauma-informed research practices
- When in doubt, observe more and document less

### 💻 Technical Setup

**For Remote Sessions:**
- Test meeting platform and audio/video 15 minutes early
- Ensure stable internet connection and have a backup plan
- Set up dual monitors if possible (one for observation, one for notes)
- Close unnecessary applications to prevent notifications
- Use headphones to avoid audio feedback
- Mute yourself upon joining and stay muted throughout
- Have researcher contact info readily available

**For In-Person Sessions:**
- Arrive 15 minutes early for setup and introductions
- Bring physical notepad and backup pens
- Locate bathroom, water, and emergency exits
- Silence all devices completely
- Sit where you can observe participant without being distracting
- Avoid moving around during the session
- Make eye contact with researcher only if urgent issue arises

### 🚨 If Issues Arise

- **Technical problems:** Contact the lead researcher immediately
- **Participant distress:** Allow researcher to handle, stay professional and supportive
- **Urgent situations:** Follow researcher emergency contact protocol
- **When in doubt:** Do nothing during the session, ask researcher afterward

### 🔍 What to Focus on (All Observers)

- Participant reactions to specific tasks or questions
- Points of confusion, frustration, or delight
- Accessibility challenges or accommodation needs
- Technical issues or platform difficulties
- What participants say AND what they don't say
- Body language and emotional responses

## 🎯 Role-Specific Sections

### 📝 Note Takers - Start Here

**Your primary focus:** Take detailed, objective notes during the session

**Key responsibilities:**
- Document participant quotes and exact language
- Note emotional reactions, hesitations, and body language  
- Track task completion, errors, and success patterns
- Record timestamps for key moments
- Serve as backup for session recording

**Specific instructions:**
- Use a note-taking template (ask researcher if you need one)
- Focus on what participants say and do, not your interpretations
- Note when participants seem confused, frustrated, or delighted
- Write down exact quotes when possible
- Don't worry about perfect formatting during the session

**Tools you'll need:**
- Note-taking template or preferred digital tool
- Participant task list (if available)
- Session recording backup access
- Reliable device with good battery life

**Success tips for note takers:**
- Develop your own shorthand for common phrases
- Focus on capturing the "why" behind participant actions
- Don't try to write everything - focus on meaningful moments
- Use timestamps to sync with recordings later

---

### 👀 Silent Observers - Start Here  

**Your primary focus:** Observe patterns and behaviors without active documentation

**Key responsibilities:**
- Focus on overall user experience and flow
- Notice emotional journey throughout the session
- Identify recurring themes and patterns
- Build empathy for the user perspective
- Prepare to share insights immediately after

**Specific instructions:**
- Avoid visible note-taking during the session
- Focus on understanding the participant's mental model
- Notice what's NOT being said or done
- Pay attention to micro-expressions and body language
- Think about the bigger picture and user journey

**Tools you'll need:**
- Mental observation framework (focus areas to watch)
- Key questions to consider during session
- Post-session insight template

**Success tips for silent observers:**
- Trust your instincts about what feels important
- Focus on emotional moments and transitions
- Notice patterns across different parts of the session
- Think about how this connects to broader user needs

---

### 📊 PM Observers - Start Here

**Your primary focus:** Understand user needs and business impact opportunities

**Key responsibilities:**
- Connect user behaviors to product decisions
- Identify feature gaps and opportunities
- Note business implications of user pain points
- Assess market fit and user satisfaction signals
- Think about scalability of user needs

**Specific instructions:**
- Focus on the "why" behind user actions and reactions
- Note potential product improvements or new features
- Consider business impact of observed pain points
- Think about how findings scale across user base
- Balance user needs with business constraints

**Tools you'll need:**
- Business impact assessment framework
- Feature opportunity tracker
- User journey mapping template
- Competitive analysis mindset

**Success tips for PM observers:**
- Ask yourself: "What would we build differently based on this?"
- Notice gaps between what users expect and what exists
- Think about both quick wins and long-term opportunities
- Consider technical feasibility alongside user needs

---

### 🏛️ Stakeholder Observers - Start Here

**Your primary focus:** Understand strategic implications and high-level user experience

**Key responsibilities:**
- Observe overall user satisfaction and sentiment
- Note strategic alignment with business goals
- Identify policy or process improvement opportunities
- Assess accessibility and inclusion considerations
- Think about organizational implications

**Specific instructions:**
- Focus on big-picture user experience themes
- Note any compliance or accessibility concerns
- Observe alignment with organizational priorities
- Minimal note-taking - focus on listening and understanding
- Think about resource needs and organizational changes

**Tools you'll need:**
- Strategic observation checklist
- Policy alignment framework
- High-level insight template
- Organizational perspective

**Success tips for stakeholders:**
- Focus on themes rather than details
- Think about systemic changes needed
- Consider policy and process implications
- Look for patterns that affect multiple user groups

## ✅ Post-Session Requirements by Role

**Timeline for All Observers: Within 1 hour**

*You will receive a message from Qori prompting you to upload your notes and observations.*

### 📝 Note Takers
- Share raw notes with research team
- Highlight top 3 key moments or quotes
- Note any technical issues that occurred
- Provide session recording backup if needed
- Clean up notes for readability (but keep original observations)

### 👀 Silent Observers  
- Participate in post-session debrief
- Share immediate impressions and gut reactions
- Identify 2-3 key insights about user experience
- Note any questions for follow-up research
- Provide overall emotional/experience assessment

### 📊 PM Observers
- Document business insights and opportunities
- Identify top 3 product improvement opportunities
- Share competitive analysis observations
- Suggest follow-up research questions
- Assess business impact of key findings

### 🏛️ Stakeholder Observers
- Provide strategic feedback and implications
- Identify organizational action items
- Note resource needs or process changes
- Share insights with relevant leadership
- Suggest policy or process improvements

- Provide feedback on the session process
- Coordinate follow-up deliverables

### 🔄 Continuous Improvement
Help us improve the observer experience:
- Provide feedback on these guidelines
- Share what worked well and what didn't
- Suggest improvements for future sessions
- Mentor new observers when possible
- Share insights that help the broader research program

## 💡 Success Tips for All Observers

### 🎯 Mindset
- Your perspective is valuable - bring authentic observations
- You're there to learn and support, not to solve problems real-time
- Focus on participant experience rather than product critique
- When in doubt, observe more and document less

### 📝 Documentation Best Practices
- Use participant quotes whenever possible
- Note emotional reactions, not just actions
- Capture the context around key moments
- Focus on behaviors, not assumptions about motivations
- Time-stamp important moments for easy reference

### 🤔 What Makes a Great Observer
- Curious and empathetic mindset
- Professional and respectful demeanor
- Comfortable with ambiguity and silence
- Good at noticing details and patterns
- Collaborative team player

## 📞 Getting Help

**Questions before a session:**
- Contact the lead researcher at least 24 hours before
- Ask for any additional context or materials
- Request a brief prep call if you're new to observing
- Review previous session notes if available

**During session emergencies:**
- Contact lead researcher via their preferred method
- Stay calm and professional
- Let researcher handle participant-facing issues
- Take notes about any issues for post-session discussion

**After session support:**
- Participate in team debrief
- Ask clarifying questions about what you observed
- Request additional context if needed
- Get feedback on your observations and deliverables
