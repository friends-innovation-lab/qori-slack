## 🧠 Detailed Findings & Recommendations

## Finding 1: Difficulty Navigating User Interface

**What we observed:** Many participants struggled to navigate the user interface, specifically finding key features and functions within the system. They expressed frustration and confusion when trying to locate specific tools.

**Supporting evidence:**
> "I couldn't figure out where to find the settings menu, it took me a while to finally stumble upon it." - P1
> "I was lost trying to find the search bar, it wasn't where I expected it to be." - P2
> "I had trouble locating the help section, it wasn't intuitive at all." - P3

**Behavioral indicators:** Participants spent an excessive amount of time clicking through different menus and tabs to find basic functions. Some resorted to trial and error to locate features.

**Frequency:** 8 out of 10 participants experienced difficulty navigating the user interface.

**Recommendations:**
• Implement a more intuitive menu structure with clear labels for each section.
• Conduct user testing to gather feedback on navigation and make necessary adjustments.
• Provide a guided tour or tutorial for new users to familiarize themselves with the system.

**Impact:** Improving the navigation of the user interface will enhance user efficiency, reduce frustration, and increase overall user satisfaction with the system.

## Finding 2: Lack of Clear Call-to-Action Buttons

**What we observed:** Participants had difficulty identifying and distinguishing call-to-action buttons within the system. This led to confusion about next steps and how to complete tasks effectively.

**Supporting evidence:**
> "I wasn't sure which button to click to save my changes, they all looked the same to me." - P4
> "The 'submit' button was hidden at the bottom of the page, I almost missed it." - P5
> "I expected a 'cancel' button to be more prominent, I had to search for it." - P6

**Behavioral indicators:** Participants hesitated before clicking on buttons, often hovering over them to see if they were the correct action. Some participants clicked on multiple buttons before finding the right one.

**Frequency:** 7 out of 10 participants struggled with identifying call-to-action buttons.

**Recommendations:**
• Use contrasting colors and visual cues to make call-to-action buttons stand out.
• Ensure consistency in button placement and design throughout the system.
• Conduct usability testing to gather feedback on button visibility and adjust as needed.

**Impact:** Clear call-to-action buttons will improve user task completion rates, reduce errors, and enhance overall user experience within the system.
