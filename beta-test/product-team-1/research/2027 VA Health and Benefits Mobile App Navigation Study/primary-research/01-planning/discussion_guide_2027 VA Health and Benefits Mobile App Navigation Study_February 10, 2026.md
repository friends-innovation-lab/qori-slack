## 🎯 Research Objectives

1. Identify navigation pain points that cause veterans to abandon common tasks like prescription refills and appointment scheduling
2. Understand veterans' mental models for organizing health versus benefits information within the app
3. Evaluate accessibility barriers that prevent assistive technology users from completing core tasks
4. Compare effectiveness of different navigation patterns (bottom tabs vs hamburger menu) for veteran users

## 🎯 Main Research Activities

*For each task, read the scenario and instruction verbatim. Observe without helping unless participant is completely stuck.*

### Task 1: Prescription Refill

**Time:** 12 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You're at home and realize you're running low on your blood pressure medication. You want to request a refill using the mobile app.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please show me how you would go about refilling your prescription. Remember to think aloud as you navigate."

**Success Criteria:**
- Locates prescription/pharmacy section
- Finds refill functionality
- Completes refill request process

**What to Observe:**
- Initial navigation path and expectations
- Use of search vs menu navigation
- Hesitation points or backtracking
- Screen reader announcement clarity (for AT users)

**Probing Questions:**
- "Where did you expect to find prescription refills?"
- "What would you do if you couldn't find what you were looking for?"

### Task 2: Schedule Medical Appointment

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Scenario:**

## 💭 Post-Session Interview

*Ask these after completing all main activities.*

1. **Overall Impressions**
   "Now that you've tried navigating through the app, what's your overall impression of how health and benefits information is organized?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "Did anything feel out of place or harder to find than expected?"

2. **Pain Points**
   "What was the most frustrating or confusing part of trying to complete these tasks?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "Can you walk me through what made that particularly difficult?"

3. **Expectations**
   "When you first opened the app, where did you expect to find prescription refills and appointment scheduling?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "How did that match up with where they actually were?"

4. **Mental Model**
   "In your mind, how do you think about the difference between your health information and your benefits information?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "Should those be completely separate or is there overlap?"

5. **Improvements**
   "If you could redesign the main navigation to make it work better for you, what would you change?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What would make it easier to get to the things you use most often?"

6. **Comparison**
   "How does this compare to how you currently handle these tasks - whether through other apps, websites, or phone calls?"
   - *Follow-up if needed:* "What works better in your current process that this app could learn from?"

7. **Likelihood to Use**
   "On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to use this app for managing your VA health and benefits? What would make it a 10?"

8. **Anything Else**
   "Is there anything else about navigating the app or finding information that I didn't ask about but you think is important?"

## 🚨 Emergency Procedures

**Technical Issues:**
- Have backup Zoom/Teams meeting link ready
- Screen share troubleshooting: Check browser permissions, try different browser, use phone screen share as last resort
- Keep participant's phone number for direct contact if connection fails
- Have mobile hotspot available for internet backup

**Participant Distress:**
- Pause session immediately and check comfort level
- Remind participant they can stop at any time without penalty
- Emphasize there are no wrong answers - we're testing the app, not them
- **For veterans experiencing crisis:** VA Crisis Line: 988, Press 1 (24/7 support)
- **For veterans needing VA support:** 1-800-MyVA411 (1-800-698-2411)

**Data/Privacy Concerns:**
- Stop recording immediately if participant requests
- Reassure about data anonymization and secure storage
- Review consent form sections if needed
- Provide researcher contact info for follow-up questions
- Remind about compensation processing timeline

**Session Disruption:**
- If participant needs to step away, pause recording and wait
- For military/family emergencies, reschedule immediately without questions
- Document any contextual factors that may affect data interpretation
