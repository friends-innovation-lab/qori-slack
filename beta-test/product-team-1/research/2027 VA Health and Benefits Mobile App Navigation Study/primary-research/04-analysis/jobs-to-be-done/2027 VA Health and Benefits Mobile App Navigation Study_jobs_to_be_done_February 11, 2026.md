## Job 1: Stay Confidently Informed About My VA Benefits Progress

> **When** I'm waiting for updates on my disability claim or benefit status,  
> **I want to** understand exactly where things stand and what's happening next,  
> **So I can** feel secure about my financial future and know the VA is working on my case.

**What this job is really about:**

| Functional Need | Emotional Need | Social Need |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| Get clear, current status information without excessive navigation | Feel confident and reassured, not anxious or confused | Be seen as someone whose claim matters and is being processed |

**What they do today:**

> "Why is claims under Benefits and not on the main screen? I check that every day." — PT002

> "I had to tap like five times to get to my claim status." — PT002

Current workarounds: Daily manual navigation through multiple menu levels, calling VA representatives for clarification

**Why current solutions fail:**

| Pain Point | Evidence |
|------------|----------|
| Claims status buried in navigation despite daily checking | PT002: "I had to tap like five times to get to my claim status" |

| Search function fails for common queries | PT002: "The search doesn't find what I'm looking for. I typed 'claim' and got nothing useful" |

**Success looks like:** Immediate visibility of claim status with plain language explanations that reduce anxiety and provide clear next steps

**Design opportunity:** Add customizable home screen widgets for claims status with plain language status descriptions and proactive notifications for status changes

## Job 2: Access My VA Information Without Barriers or Friction

> **When** I need to manage my healthcare, check payments, or communicate with the VA,  
> **I want to** complete these essential tasks efficiently regardless of my physical or cognitive abilities,  
> **So I can** maintain my independence and get the care and benefits I've earned.

**What this job is really about:**

| Functional Need | Emotional Need | Social Need |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| Navigate and complete tasks using assistive technology or with physical limitations | Feel capable and independent, not frustrated or blocked | Be treated with dignity and have equal access to services |

**What they do today:**

> "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text. That's not helpful at all." — PT001

> "I need bigger buttons. These are hard to tap with my fingers." — PT003

Current workarounds: Trial-and-error navigation, asking for help from family members, abandoning tasks

**Why current solutions fail:**

| Pain Point | Evidence |
|------------|----------|
| Unlabeled buttons block screen reader users | PT001: "I shouldn't have to guess what buttons do. That's basic accessibility" |
| Inconsistent text scaling reduces effectiveness | PT003: "Text is still too small in some places even with the large text setting" |
| Touch targets too small for users with motor difficulties | PT003: Multiple miss-taps observed, "I need bigger buttons" |
| Critical interface elements hidden at bottom of screens | PT001: Nearly missed edit button, took 6 minutes with extensive navigation |

**Success looks like:** Seamless task completion using assistive technology or accessibility features without workarounds or assistance

**Design opportunity:** Implement comprehensive accessibility audit with consistent button labeling, proper text scaling, larger touch targets, and better element placement

> Session timeout interrupted message composition, had to start over — PT003

**Why current solutions fail:**

| Pain Point | Evidence |
|------------|----------|
