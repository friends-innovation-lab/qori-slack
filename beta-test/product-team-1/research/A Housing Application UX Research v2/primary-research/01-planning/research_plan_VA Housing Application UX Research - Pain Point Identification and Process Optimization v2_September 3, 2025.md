# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Housing Application UX Research - Pain Point Identification and Process Optimization v2

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal - facilitates online application, eligibility screening, document upload, and status tracking for Veterans seeking housing assistance. This tool streamlines the application process, ensuring timely access to critical benefits within the VA ecosystem."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** Primary Team: VA Digital Experience Team
Collaborating Office: Office of Veterans Experience (VEO)
Support: VA Housing Benefits Administration
Technical Partner: VA.gov Platform Team
Accessibility Consultant: Section 508 Compliance Office  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Jordan Martinez, Senior UX Researcher  
**🗓️ Date:** September 3, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the decision on redesigning the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal, focusing on the online application workflow, eligibility screening, document upload system, and application status tracking. This decision aims to simplify the application process for Veterans, reducing support calls and improving accessibility compliance. The redesign will streamline the user experience, making it easier for Veterans to apply for housing benefits.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims

**Explanation:** This priority directly aligns with the goal of improving the online application workflow for VA housing benefits, as it focuses on expediting disability claims processing for veterans.

**Additional Priorities (if applicable):**
- **Solidify the VA.gov platform:** Ensure a stable and reliable platform for veterans to access and apply for housing benefits.
- **Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience:** Tailor the online application process to provide a more personalized and user-friendly experience for veterans.

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal serves as a crucial tool for Veterans to apply for housing benefits, screen their eligibility, upload required documents, and track their application status seamlessly within their journey of accessing VA services.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Completing the online application for housing benefits
- **Putting down roots:** Screening eligibility criteria for housing benefits
- **Retiring:** Uploading necessary documents for the application
- **Aging:** Tracking the status of their housing benefits application

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Veterans may face challenges in navigating the online application process, understanding eligibility requirements, and uploading documents efficiently. Improving user guidance and support features could enhance the overall user experience.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and prioritize the top 3-5 usability barriers contributing to the 34% application abandonment rate for veterans applying for housing benefits online, uncovering root causes to inform targeted improvements.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Pinpoint pain points in the online application workflow to streamline the process for veterans.
- Uncover challenges with eligibility screening to enhance user understanding and completion rates.
- Evaluate the document upload system for efficiency and user-friendliness to reduce barriers to submission.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, interviews, and competitive analysis are optimal for identifying usability barriers and root causes behind high application abandonment rates among veterans.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Utilize 1-hour user interviews and 45-minute usability testing sessions
- Activities include task-based scenarios, think-aloud protocols, and competitive analysis
- Specific evaluation protocols for VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal components mentioned

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Ensure adherence to Collaboration Cycle requirements and approval timeline
- Incorporate trauma-informed research considerations and accessibility accommodations for participants

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** Primary: Veterans who have attempted to apply for VA housing benefits within the last 12 months (completed or abandoned)
Secondary: Veterans currently receiving disability compensation (30%+ rating preferred)
Inclusion criteria: Basic computer/smartphone proficiency, willing to share screen during sessions
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, professional UX/web designers
Accessibility focus: Minimum 40% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented accessibility needs  

**🧍 Participant Count:** User Interviews: 5 participants (1-hour sessions)
Usability Testing: 5 participants (45-minute task-based sessions)

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
**Demographic targets (aim for when possible):**
Based on User Interviews: 5 participants (1-hour sessions)
Usability Testing: 5 participants (45-minute task-based sessions)

**Primary targets:**
- Service era: 2-3 participants from Vietnam War era, 2 participants from Gulf War era
- Income: 2 participants with annual income below $30,000, 3 participants with annual income above $30,000
- Racial/ethnic diversity: 2-3 participants from minority backgrounds
- Gender diversity: 3 male participants, 2 female participants
- Age diversity: 2 participants aged 25-40, 3 participants aged 41-60
- Education diversity: 3 participants with high school education, 2 participants with college education
- Accessibility needs: 2 participants using assistive technologies, 3 participants with documented accessibility needs

**Secondary targets:**
- VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal usage patterns: 3 frequent users, 2 infrequent users
- Training background: 3 participants with formal training, 2 self-taught users

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding qualified participants who meet the inclusion criteria
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA disability organizations and support groups to directly reach out to potential participants and offer incentives for participation.

**Execution issues:** Potential issues with implementing usability testing, interviews, and competitive analysis
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct a pilot test of the research methodology with internal team members to identify and address any potential issues before engaging with actual participants.

**Timeline risks:** Delays in the research process that impact the standard timeline
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Establish clear milestones and deadlines for each phase of the research project, with regular check-ins to monitor progress and address any delays promptly.

**Quality concerns:** Risks related to data validity and stakeholder satisfaction
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Implement a rigorous data validation process, including cross-referencing participant responses and conducting follow-up interviews for clarification. Regularly update stakeholders on progress and involve them in decision-making to ensure satisfaction.
