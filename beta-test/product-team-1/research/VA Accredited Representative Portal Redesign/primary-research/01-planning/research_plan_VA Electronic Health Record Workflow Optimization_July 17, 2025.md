# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Electronic Health Record Workflow Optimization

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools - Digitized record-keeping and streamlined processes for improved patient care and efficiency. Essential tools within the VA healthcare system, ensuring accurate and timely care for Veterans."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** eterans Health Administration (VHA) Office of Health Informatics  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson  
**🗓️ Date:** July 17, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the decision to redesign the VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools to reduce documentation time and improve patient care efficiency for VA clinical staff. This decision will result in faster access to accurate patient information for Veterans, leading to improved quality of care and streamlined workflow processes within the VA healthcare system.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Improve the Veteran login experience

**Explanation:** Enhancing the login experience for Veterans within the VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools will streamline access to crucial health information.

**Additional Priorities (if applicable):**
- **Solidify the VA.gov platform:** Integrating the EHR system and clinical workflow tools into the VA.gov platform will provide a seamless experience for Veterans.
- **Expand the flagship mobile app:** Developing mobile access to the EHR system will enhance Veterans' ability to manage their health information on-the-go.

**Project-Specific Priorities:**
- Implement a user-friendly interface for Veterans to access their health records
- Streamline the clinical workflow tools to improve efficiency for VA employees
- Enhance security measures for protecting sensitive health data

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**Detected Audience:** Veterans
**Product Area:** VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools are integrated into the Veterans' healthcare journey to ensure seamless access to their medical records and streamline the clinical workflow.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Accessing personal health records for appointments and medication management
- **Putting down roots:** Updating medical history and tracking progress over time
- **Aging:** Managing chronic conditions and coordinating care with healthcare providers

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Difficulty navigating the EHR system, lack of user-friendly interfaces, and challenges in communicating with healthcare providers effectively.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify specific workflow inefficiencies and usability barriers within the VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System that hinder clinical staff during patient encounters and documentation tasks.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Pinpoint areas within the EHR system where navigation and data entry processes can be streamlined for improved efficiency.
- Evaluate the clarity and effectiveness of user interface design elements to enhance user experience and task completion.
- Identify opportunities to optimize the integration of clinical workflow tools within the EHR system to support seamless documentation and patient care.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 
"Usability testing is optimal for identifying specific workflow inefficiencies and usability barriers in the VA EHR system, providing actionable insights for improving user experience."

**Implementation Approach:**
- Conducting individual usability testing sessions with participants to observe their interactions with the EHR system in real-time.
- Utilizing task-based scenarios to assess navigation, data entry, and overall user satisfaction.
- Implementing structured post-task interviews to gather qualitative feedback on pain points and suggestions for improvement in the VA EHR system. 

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Ensuring all research activities align with VA Collaboration Cycle requirements and obtaining necessary approvals within the designated timeline.
- Incorporating trauma-informed research practices to create a safe and supportive environment for participants sharing their experiences.
- Providing accessibility accommodations such as screen readers or enlarged text for participants with visual impairments during the usability testing sessions.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** VA clinical staff including nurses, physicians, and medical assistants who actively use the EHR system for patient documentation and have at least 6 months of experience with the current system.  

**🧍 Participant Count:** 10-14 participants

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
- For the detected audience of Veterans, aim for:
  - 3-4 participants from recent veterans and different service periods
  - At least 3 participants with household income $49,000 or less
  - 2-3 participants identifying as Black, Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, Asian, or Native American
  - At least 1 participant identifying as other than male
  - 2-3 participants under 35 and over 55
  - At least 1 participant without a degree
  - 1 participant with accessibility needs

- For the detected audience of VA Employees, aim for:
  - 4 participants from different job functions (clinical, administrative, technical support)
  - 2-3 new employees (under 2 years) and experienced staff (5+ years)
  - At least 2 participants from lower GS levels (1-12) and higher levels (13+)
  - 2-3 participants from different VA facilities or regional offices
  - 2 participants from different VA administrations (VHA, VBA, NCA)
  - 2-3 participants with varying levels of technology comfort

- For the detected audience of External Stakeholders, aim for:
  - 3 participants from VSO representatives, private attorneys, and claims agents
  - 2-3 new practitioners (under 2 years) and experienced professionals (5+ years)
  - At least 1 participant with small (under 50 cases) and large (100+ cases) caseloads
  - 2 participants from urban and rural practitioners
  - 2-3 participants from solo practitioners and large organizations
  - 2-3 participants with varying levels of technology comfort

- Secondary targets:
  - Aim for a mix of frequent and infrequent users of VA Electronic Health Record (EHR) System and Clinical Workflow Tools
  - Include participants with a mix of formal VA training and self-taught users

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:**
- **Risk:** Difficulty in finding qualified VA clinical staff for the usability study due to their busy schedules and limited availability.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement flexible scheduling options for participants, including evening or weekend sessions, to accommodate their availability.

**Execution issues:**
- **Risk:** Potential usability implementation problems with the EHR system that may hinder the research process.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough pre-testing of the usability study protocols with a small group of participants to identify and address any potential system-related issues before the main study.

**Timeline risks:**
- **Risk:** Unforeseen delays in the research process that may impact the standard timeline.
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Regularly monitor and track progress against the project timeline, and proactively address any obstacles or bottlenecks to ensure timely completion of the research.

**Quality concerns:**
- **Risk:** Data validity issues or low stakeholder satisfaction with the research findings.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation processes, such as double-checking data entry and conducting follow-up interviews with participants to ensure accuracy. Additionally, regularly communicate with stakeholders throughout the research process to gather feedback and address any concerns promptly.
