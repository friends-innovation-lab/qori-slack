# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Accredited Representative Portal Redesign

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "Accredited Representative Portal and Digital Tools - Provides VA-accredited representatives with efficient access to essential information for assisting Veterans. Streamlines the claims process and ensures accurate representation within the VA ecosystem."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA) Digital Services  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson  
**🗓️ Date:** July 17, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the decision to redesign the Accredited Representative Portal and Digital Tools to improve claim submission workflows and reduce processing time. This change will result in faster processing times for Veterans and improved efficiency for the VA. By updating the document upload system and status tracking features, Veterans will experience quicker resolution of their claims, leading to a more streamlined experience overall.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify specific pain points and inefficiencies in the current VA Accredited Representative Portal and Digital Tools to inform targeted improvements for a more user-friendly experience.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most common challenges faced by VA Accredited Representatives when navigating the portal system.
- Uncover opportunities to simplify the process of submitting and managing veteran benefit claims through the digital tools.
- Identify key features or functionalities that are confusing or not intuitive for users during usability testing.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 
["Interviews","usability"] are optimal for this research as they allow for direct feedback from VA Accredited Representatives on their experiences with the current portal system, uncovering specific pain points and inefficiencies.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Conduct 1-hour semi-structured interviews with each participant to gather detailed insights on their interactions with the portal system.
- Use usability testing sessions to observe representatives navigating the portal and identify specific areas of improvement.
- Develop a standardized checklist for evaluating the usability of key features within the portal system.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Submit collaboration cycle proposal for approval 4 weeks prior to research initiation.
- Incorporate trauma-informed practices into interview protocols and provide resources for emotional support.
- Offer accessibility accommodations such as screen readers and alternative input devices for participants with disabilities.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** VA Accredited Representatives (attorneys, VSO representatives, and claims agents) who actively submit benefit claims for veterans and have used the current portal system within the past 6 months.  

**🧍 Participant Count:** 8-12 participants

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
**Demographic targets:**
Based on 8-12 participants, calculate realistic and proportional numbers:
- Service era diversity: Include participants from recent veterans and different service periods when possible (at least 2-3 participants from recent veterans)
- Income diversity: Aim for participants with household income $49,000 or less (target ~30% of sample, so 2-4 participants)
- Racial/ethnic diversity: Include participants identifying as Black, Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, Asian, or Native American when possible (at least 1 participant from each category)
- Gender diversity: Include participants identifying as other than male when possible (at least 2 participants)
- Age diversity: Include both under 35 and over 55 when possible (at least 1 participant from each age group)
- Education diversity: Include participants without degrees when possible (at least 2 participants)
- Accessibility needs: Include assistive technology users if relevant to the study (at least 1 participant)

**Secondary targets:**
- Geographic diversity: Rural and urban participants when possible (at least 1 participant from each)
- A Accredited Representative Portal and Digital Tools usage patterns: Mix of high and low usage levels when possible (at least 2 participants with high usage and 1 with low usage)
- Technology comfort levels: Mix of high and low comfort when possible (at least 2 participants with high comfort and 1 with low comfort)

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:**
- **Specific risk description:** Difficulty in finding qualified VA Accredited Representatives who meet the criteria for participation in the research.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Utilize multiple recruitment channels, such as VA databases, professional organizations, and referrals from existing participants to ensure a diverse pool of qualified representatives.

**Execution issues:**
- **Specific risk description:** Technical difficulties during the implementation of ["interviews","usability"] methods, leading to disruptions in data collection.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough testing of all interview and usability tools prior to the start of the research to identify and address any potential technical issues proactively.

**Timeline risks:**
- **Specific risk description:** Unforeseen delays in the research process impacting the standard timeline for project completion.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones and deadlines, regularly monitor progress, and proactively address any issues that may arise to ensure timely completion.

**Quality concerns:**
- **Specific risk description:** Data validity issues due to inaccurate or incomplete information provided by participants, as well as dissatisfaction from stakeholders with the research outcomes.
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous validation processes, such as cross-referencing participant responses with existing data sources, and regularly communicate with stakeholders to gather feedback and make necessary adjustments to the research approach.
