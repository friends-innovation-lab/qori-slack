# 📁 Fact Sheet: When to Keep Artifacts Inside a Research Study Folder

## 🎯 Purpose
In some cases, it makes sense to store findings or summaries *within* the specific research study folder.

## ✅ Use This Approach When…

| Scenario                                  | Why It Works                                    |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Single-use or isolated study              | The artifacts won’t be reused across projects   |
| Sensitive data or limited circulation     | Keeping artifacts closer to context builds trust|
| Lean teams or small repos                 | Minimizes extra folders and complexity          |
| Artifacts are still in development        | Draft briefs or rough synth lives alongside raw data|

## 📎 Common Artifacts You Might Keep Inside
- Internal findings summaries
- Draft quotes and observation highlights
- Annotated transcripts with team comments

## 🧠 Pro Tip
When work is ready to *share* or *link broadly*, move it to `artifacts/`. Until then, use the study folder as a safe workspace.

**Rule of Thumb:**  
`research/` = source  
`artifacts/` = signal
