## Transcript (Excerpted and Lightly Edited for Clarity)

**Moderator:** Thanks for joining us today. To get started, can you tell me a little bit about your experience using VA.gov?

**Participant:** Yeah, I’ve used it a few times. Mostly to refill prescriptions or check appointments. I didn’t really think of it for mental health stuff, to be honest.

---

**Moderator:** If you were looking for support with PTSD, what would you do?

**Participant:** I’d probably Google it. Like, “PTSD help VA” or something. I wouldn’t start on the homepage, it’s kind of overwhelming.

---

**Moderator:** Can you walk me through what you’d do right now if you wanted help?

**Participant:** Sure. (navigates site) Okay, I’m typing in “PTSD”... hmm. This page just says stuff about symptoms, not how to get help. Oh wait, there’s a link... but this just goes to another overview page. Where do I actually talk to someone?

---

**Moderator:** Is there anything about the page that stands out to you?

**Participant:** It doesn’t feel like it’s for me. I don’t see anything about women. It just feels like a generic info sheet. There’s too much text too. If I’m having a bad day, I’m not reading all this.

---

**Moderator:** How would you feel about reaching out from here?

**Participant:** I don’t know. The phone number doesn’t say who’s going to answer. Is it a hotline? A caseworker? I’ve tried calling the wrong number before and ended up in this endless loop.

---

**Moderator:** What would help?

**Participant:** Honestly, just a clear “here’s who to talk to” and maybe something that says “if you’re a woman Veteran, click here.” Something that feels like it’s actually made for people like me.

---

**Moderator:** That’s really helpful. Thank you. Just to close out—anything else you’d like us to know?

**Participant:** Just that I’m glad someone’s asking. This stuff is hard to talk about, but it matters.
