# 🧠 Affinity Map – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study

Compiled from interviews with 6 participants (P01–P06)

---

## 💬 Emotions & First Impressions

| Quote | Insight |
|-------|---------|
| “I felt lost right away.” | Initial entry points lack clarity and focus |
| “It looked official but not helpful.” | Users distrust overly formal language |
| “I didn’t see anything that said ‘for women.’” | Visibility of gender-specific content is low |

---

## 🔍 Navigation Patterns

| Observation | Insight |
|-------------|---------|
| Most participants used Google, not VA.gov directly | SEO and content indexing matter more than homepage UX |
| Users scanned for visual cues (buttons, banners) | Content hierarchy needs better visual anchors |
| PTSD help pages were dense and text-heavy | Cognitive load is too high during emotional states |

---

## 🚧 Friction Points

| Barrier | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Too many links without clear outcomes | “Where am I supposed to click first?” |
| No clear escalation path | “What happens after I call this number?” |
| Lack of trauma-aware tone | “It felt cold, like it didn’t care I was in crisis.” |

---

## ✅ Desired Features

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| PTSD checklist or decision tree | Reduces confusion and helps self-triage |
| Gender-specific pathways or banners | Builds trust and relevance |
| Shorter, skimmable content blocks | Supports overwhelmed users during difficult moments |
| Clear next steps and contact ownership | Reduces anxiety about unknown outcomes |
