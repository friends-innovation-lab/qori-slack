# 📝 Session Notes – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study
**Notetaker:** Michelle Tran  
**Date:** July 9, 2025  
**Participant ID:** P02  
**Session Type:** Remote moderated interview + task walkthrough  
**Platform:** Zoom  
**Recording:** Yes (audio + screen)

---

## 👤 Participant Summary
- Army Veteran, served 2005–2011
- Diagnosed PTSD
- Currently receiving some VA care
- Primarily uses mobile devices
- Comfortable using technology but “doesn’t like websites with too many steps”

---

## 🔍 Key Themes

### PTSD Support Discovery
- Began on Google, searched “PTSD help VA women”
- Landed on general PTSD overview page—not specific to women
- Participant quote: _“It didn’t feel like it was talking to me. Like, it’s written for someone else.”_

### Navigation Observations
- Didn’t notice the “Women Veterans Health” section in top nav
- Took three different page hops before reaching a service directory
- Was confused by the difference between urgent help and ongoing care

### Trust & Emotional Safety
- Repeatedly emphasized importance of tone and representation:
  _“I didn’t see any images or stories of women who looked like me.”_
- Said she would hesitate to call a generic number because she’s “done that before and it was a dead end”

---

## 💬 Verbatim Highlights
> “I’ve been to VA.gov before, but I never thought to look here for mental health stuff.”
>  
> “If I’m already anxious, I’m not reading three pages of links.”
>  
> “A banner that just says ‘get help now’ isn’t enough if you don’t know what kind of help it is.”

---

## ✅ Opportunities / Recommendations
- Add gender-specific PTSD care links on the main PTSD landing page
- Use inclusive imagery and language for women Veterans
- Clarify urgent vs long-term care options
- Consider a plain-language PTSD checklist or self-assessment entry point
