# Conversation Guide – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study

---

## Moderator logistics 📋✅

### 2–3 days before the session
- [ ] Review research protocol and participant consent status
- [ ] Load and test prototype (if applicable)
- [ ] Confirm session logistics with observers

### Day of the session
- Mute notifications
- Open #feedback-backchannel on Slack
- Admit observers once participant consents
- Share screen and enable captions

---

## Intro – 5 minutes

Thanks for joining us today! My name is **Lapedra** and **Michelle** is here taking notes.

Today we're going to talk about your experience finding mental health support through VA.gov, particularly around PTSD-related resources.

**Session details:**
- The session will take around 60 minutes.
- We’re looking for your honest opinions—there are no right or wrong answers.
- You may skip any question or stop the session at any time.
- Your benefits will not be affected by your participation.

**Consent:**
- Are you okay with us having a couple of observers?
- Are you okay with us recording the screen and audio?
- Are you okay with us potentially using a de-identified clip from this session?

➡️ **Start recording. Confirm consent again.**

---

## Warm-up Questions – 5 minutes

1. Have you ever visited VA.gov looking for mental health support?
2. If yes: What were you hoping to find?
3. Have you ever received a PTSD diagnosis or self-identified with symptoms?
4. What device do you usually use to look up information (phone, computer, tablet)?

---

## First Task: Finding PTSD Support Services – 10 minutes

- [ ] Ask participant to open VA.gov
- [ ] Prompt: “Show me how you’d look for help related to PTSD.”

> Things to watch for:
> - Do they use search vs menu navigation?
> - Do they notice women-specific services?
> - Are they confident in the results they find?

---

## Second Task: Reviewing PTSD Content – 10 minutes

- [ ] Ask participant to click into a PTSD resource page
- [ ] Prompt: “What stands out to you on this page?”

> Things to watch for:
> - Is the tone supportive or clinical?
> - Do they understand available next steps?
> - Do they see themselves in the language?

---

## Third Task: Exploring Eligibility and Appointment Flow – 10 minutes

- [ ] Prompt: “If you wanted to take the next step to get help, what would you do here?”

> Things to watch for:
> - Do they find eligibility information?
> - Do they hesitate based on trust, privacy, or unclear flow?
> - Do they know whether a referral is needed?

---

## Post-Task Interview – 10 minutes

- How did the experience make you feel?
- What felt supportive or frustrating?
- What would have helped you feel more confident?
- Do you have any suggestions to improve it?

## Thank-You and Closing – 5 minutes

Thank you again for sharing your time and experience today. We really appreciate it. You’ll receive a thank-you email and you’re welcome to share the research opportunity with other Veterans who may want to participate.

---

## Emergency Exit 🚨

If a participant shows signs of distress:
- Pause the session.
- Gently ask if they’d like to stop or take a break.
- Offer to reschedule or cancel without pressure.
- DM research ops contact for real-time support.

> Practice the exit language to ensure it feels natural if needed.
