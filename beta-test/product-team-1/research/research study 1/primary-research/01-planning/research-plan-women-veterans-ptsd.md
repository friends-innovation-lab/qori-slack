# 📝 Research Plan – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study

**📦 Product/Feature Area:**  
Mental health services discovery and navigation within VA.gov

**🏛️ Team/Office:**  
VA Digital Services in collaboration with VA Women’s Health Program Office

**👩🏽‍🔬 Researchers:**  
Trish Longhorn (Lead), Sara Hill (Co-Researcher), Michelle Tran (Collaborator)

**🗓️ Date:**  
July 18, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background  
The VA is prioritizing equitable access to mental health resources, particularly for historically underrepresented Veteran groups. This study explores how women Veterans with PTSD identify, navigate, and access mental health care pathways through VA digital services. While VA.gov has improved information access, early data suggests that women Veterans face distinct challenges tied to stigma, gendered assumptions, and navigation complexity.

---

## 🎯 Organizational Priorities  
- Improve access and trust in VA mental health services  
- Support gender equity and trauma-informed care  
- Reduce digital and emotional burden during high-stress moments

---

## 🧭 Veteran/User Journey  
This research focuses on the early discovery phase—when a woman Veteran identifies a mental health need and begins seeking support or information on PTSD treatment options.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals  
- Understand how women Veterans with PTSD look for care options  
- Identify usability gaps, emotional friction, or content mismatches  
- Evaluate how VA.gov communicates eligibility and support pathways

---

## ❓ Research Questions  
- How do women Veterans with PTSD begin looking for support through VA platforms?  
- What barriers (emotional, technical, cultural) do they face in the first few clicks?  
- Do users understand the difference between urgent, ongoing, and community-based care?

---

## 🧪 Hypotheses  
- Women Veterans hesitate to seek PTSD treatment due to lack of visible, gender-specific content  
- Participants will struggle to locate appropriate care pathways when using general search terms  
- Trust signals are weaker for this cohort, leading to drop-off before accessing care

---

## 🛠️ Methodology  
Mixed-methods:  
- Remote semi-structured interviews  
- Think-aloud usability tests (mobile + desktop)  
- Concept testing of improved PTSD landing pages
