# 📊 Usability Readout – Women Veterans & PTSD Access Study

**Product Area:** VA.gov Mental Health Navigation  
**Team:** VA Digital Services + Women's Health Collaboration  
**Date:** 07/18/2025  
**Research Lead:** Lapedra Tolson  
**Notetaker(s):** Michelle Tran  
**Observers:** Sara Hill, Anthony Ramirez

---

## 🔍 Key Insights Summary

1. **Lack of Gender-Specific Pathways:** Participants could not easily locate women-specific PTSD resources.  
2. **Overwhelming Information Density:** Pages were perceived as too text-heavy and hard to skim.  
3. **Low Trust in Contact Options:** Participants were hesitant to use general contact numbers due to past poor experiences.

---

## 🎯 Research Goals

- Understand how women Veterans discover and evaluate PTSD support options through VA.gov.
- Identify UX and content gaps specific to trauma-informed, gender-equitable care discovery.
- Uncover emotional and usability friction during the search and exploration journey.

---

## ❓ Research Questions

- Can women Veterans easily find mental health resources specific to PTSD on VA.gov?
- What barriers (emotional or interface) exist in navigating the PTSD care pathway?
- Do participants feel confident in next steps after reading a mental health resource page?

---

## 🧪 Methodology

- **Type:** Remote moderated interviews and task-based usability testing  
- **Session Length:** 60 minutes  
- **Platform:** Zoom + CivicMind transcription  
- **Tools:** Live VA.gov site, Slack for observer backchannel, Whisper API for transcripts

---

## 🧠 Detailed Findings & Recommendations

### 🧭 Finding 1: Lack of Gender-Specific Pathways

**Summary:**  
None of the participants located women-specific content during natural task flow.

**Evidence:**  
> “I didn’t even know there was a section just for women.” – P01

**Recommendation:**  
Add persistent top-level navigation to Women Veterans Health + PTSD pathways.

---

### 🧭 Finding 2: Overwhelming Information Density

**Summary:**  
Participants consistently described resource pages as “too much” to process during emotional moments.

**Evidence:**  
> “If I’m anxious, I’m not reading three pages of links.” – P02

**Recommendation:**  
Use bulleted summaries and skimmable page structures to reduce cognitive load.

---

### 🧭 Finding 3: Low Trust in Contact Options

**Summary:**  
General hotlines or unlabeled contact links felt ambiguous and untrustworthy.

**Evidence:**  
> “I don’t know who’s going to pick up the phone. That’s scary.” – P03

**Recommendation:**  
Provide clear context for contact numbers—who’s answering, what to expect, trauma-informed language.
