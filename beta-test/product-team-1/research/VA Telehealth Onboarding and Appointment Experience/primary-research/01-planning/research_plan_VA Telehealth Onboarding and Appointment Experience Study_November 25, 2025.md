# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect — Provides Veterans with a secure, convenient way to connect with their healthcare providers through telehealth appointments on mobile and desktop devices.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digital Services HCD Team

🗓️ **Date:** November 25, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to simplify the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other touchpoints. By guiding Veterans through appointment scheduling, providing timely technical checks and instructions, and coordinating messages across platforms, the goal is to improve the overall telehealth experience for Veterans. This will benefit Veterans by making it easier and more intuitive to access telehealth services, ultimately leading to increased utilization and improved health outcomes. From a business perspective, streamlining the telehealth experience for Veterans can lead to higher patient satisfaction, increased appointment attendance, and overall cost savings for the VA.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Improve the Veteran login experience

**Explanation:** Enhancing the login experience will streamline access to VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints, making it easier for Veterans to join appointments.

**Additional Priorities:**
- Solidify the VA.gov platform: Ensuring a stable and reliable platform will support the seamless integration of telehealth services for Veterans.
- Expand the flagship mobile app: Increasing the functionality and accessibility of the mobile app will cater to Veterans who prefer video visits and need convenient access to telehealth services.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in connecting veterans with healthcare providers remotely, making it easier for them to access care and support throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop help veterans manage their health by providing convenient access to healthcare services, reducing the need for in-person visits.
- **Putting down roots:** These telehealth options support veterans in settling into civilian life by offering continuous access to healthcare resources, even if they relocate or have limited mobility.
- **Retiring:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop assist veterans in retirement by ensuring they can easily connect with healthcare providers for ongoing support and monitoring.

**Critical Pain Points:** 
- Connectivity issues or technical difficulties may hinder veterans from accessing VA Video Connect or telehealth services effectively.
- Lack of awareness or training on how to use these telehealth platforms may prevent veterans from utilizing them to their full potential.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify specific pain points and drop-off moments experienced by Veterans while preparing for and joining telehealth visits using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with the aim of reducing technical issues and increasing confidence in utilizing the platform.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most common technical issues encountered by Veterans when using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, and provide actionable recommendations for improvement.
- Identify the content and touchpoints within VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth platforms that contribute to building confidence in Veterans and reducing drop-off rates.
- Analyze interaction patterns on VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop to determine which are most effective in supporting Veterans and reducing last-minute technical issues.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for direct observation of how users interact with the platform, identifying pain points and areas for improvement in the user experience.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** Individual usability testing sessions lasting 60 minutes each, allowing for in-depth exploration of user interactions.
- **Key Activities:** Task-based scenarios to assess users' ability to navigate VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints on both mobile and desktop platforms. Think-aloud protocols to capture users' thought processes.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Use of standardized usability metrics such as task completion rates, time on task, and user satisfaction surveys to evaluate the effectiveness and efficiency of the user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Regular check-ins with VA stakeholders to ensure alignment with project requirements and timelines.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Sensitivity to potential triggers for veteran participants, providing a safe and supportive environment for feedback.
- **Accessibility:** Providing options for participants with diverse needs, such as screen reader compatibility and alternative input methods.
