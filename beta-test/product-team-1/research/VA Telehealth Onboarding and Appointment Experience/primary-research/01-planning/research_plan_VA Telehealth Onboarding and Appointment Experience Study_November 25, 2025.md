# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect — Allows Veterans to connect with their healthcare providers remotely through video appointments on their mobile device or desktop computer.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digital Services HCD Team

🗓️ **Date:** November 25, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other touchpoints across mobile and desktop platforms. By improving the appointment scheduling process, providing timely technical guidance, and coordinating messages effectively, Veterans will have a more seamless and user-friendly experience accessing telehealth services. Ultimately, this will lead to increased utilization of telehealth services, improved patient satisfaction, and potentially cost savings for the VA.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Improve the Veteran login experience

**Explanation:** Enhancing the login experience will make it easier for Veterans to access VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across different devices.

**Additional Priorities:**
- Solidify the VA.gov platform: Ensuring a stable and reliable platform will support the seamless delivery of telehealth services.
- Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience: Tailoring the online experience to Veterans' preferences will improve engagement with telehealth services.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in providing convenient and accessible healthcare services to veterans throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop enable veterans to easily connect with healthcare providers, schedule appointments, and receive medical care without the need to travel to a VA facility.
- **Putting down roots:** By offering telehealth services, VA Video Connect and supporting touchpoints help veterans transition into civilian life by ensuring they have continued access to healthcare resources and support.
- **Retiring:** VA Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop assist veterans in retirement by providing a convenient way to manage their health and wellness needs remotely.

**Critical Pain Points:** 
- Limited access to technology or internet connectivity may hinder veterans from utilizing VA Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints.
- Lack of awareness or education about telehealth services may prevent veterans from taking advantage of these convenient healthcare options.
- Difficulty navigating the VA Video Connect platform or mobile/desktop touchpoints could lead to frustration and barriers to accessing necessary healthcare services.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Determine the specific stages in the user journey where Veterans encounter difficulties or drop off while using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with the aim of reducing technical issues and enhancing user confidence.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Identify the key content elements within VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop that contribute to user confusion or frustration, leading to drop off.
- Analyze the touchpoints and interaction patterns that are most effective in guiding Veterans through the preparation and joining process of telehealth visits using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.
- Evaluate user feedback and behavior to determine which specific features or functionalities within VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop build confidence and alleviate last-minute technical issues for Veterans.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows us to understand how veterans interact with the platform and identify any usability issues that may impact their experience and satisfaction.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting approximately 60 minutes each.
- **Key Activities:** Task-based testing, thinking aloud protocol, post-task interviews, and surveys to gather feedback on user experience.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Use a standardized usability testing script with predefined tasks and success criteria to evaluate user interactions with VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Coordinate with VA stakeholders to ensure compliance with requirements and adhere to project timeline.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Provide a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants, allowing them to opt-out of any tasks that may trigger trauma.
- **Accessibility:** Ensure usability testing materials and sessions are accessible to participants with diverse needs, such as providing screen readers or alternative input methods.
