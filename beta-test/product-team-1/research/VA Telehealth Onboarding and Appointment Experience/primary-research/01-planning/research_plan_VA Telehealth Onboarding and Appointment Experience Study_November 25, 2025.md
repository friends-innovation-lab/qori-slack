# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

📦 **Product Area:** A Video Connect — Provides Veterans with convenient access to telehealth services on both mobile and desktop platforms.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA HCD Team

🗓️ **Date:** November 25, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using A Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop. By guiding Veterans through appointment scheduling, providing timely technical checks and instructions, and coordinating messages across various platforms, the goal is to make the telehealth process more user-friendly and efficient. This will benefit Veterans by reducing confusion and frustration, ultimately improving their overall experience with telehealth services. From a business perspective, these improvements can lead to increased usage of telehealth services, better patient outcomes, and higher satisfaction rates among Veterans.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in the VA Journey of Veterans by providing convenient and accessible healthcare services for veterans.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop help veterans manage their health and wellbeing by allowing them to easily connect with healthcare providers for virtual appointments and consultations.
- **Putting down roots:** A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop support veterans settling into civilian life by offering remote access to healthcare services, making it easier for them to transition and adapt to their new lifestyle.
- **Retiring:** A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop assist veterans in retirement by providing them with continued access to healthcare resources and support, even after leaving active duty.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Limited access to in-person healthcare services for veterans living in remote or rural areas.
- Technical difficulties or connectivity issues that may hinder veterans from utilizing A Video Connect and telehealth services effectively.
- Lack of awareness or education about the benefits and availability of telehealth options for veterans.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Improve user experience for Veterans using A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop by reducing drop-off rates by 15% during the preparation and joining process for telehealth visits.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Identify the specific moments where Veterans encounter technical issues or confusion while using A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, leading to drop-offs.
- Determine which content, touchpoints, and interaction patterns are most effective in building confidence and reducing last-minute technical issues for Veterans using A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.
- Provide actionable recommendations for optimizing A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop to enhance user confidence and reduce drop-off rates during telehealth visits.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for a deep understanding of user interactions, pain points, and preferences, which are crucial for optimizing the user experience and ensuring effective telehealth services.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting approximately 45-60 minutes.
- **Key Activities:** Task-based testing, thinking aloud protocol, post-task interviews, and usability questionnaires to gather comprehensive data on user interactions with A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Use of standardized usability metrics such as task success rate, time on task, error rate, and user satisfaction scores to evaluate the effectiveness and efficiency of A Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Ensure close collaboration with VA stakeholders throughout the research process, including regular check-ins and feedback sessions.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Sensitivity to potential trauma triggers for veteran participants, providing a safe and supportive environment for their participation.
- **Accessibility:** Provide accommodations such as screen readers, captioning, and alternative input methods to ensure accessibility for participants with diverse needs.

**For Usability Testing:**
- **Task Scenarios:** 
1. Schedule a telehealth appointment using A Video Connect on a mobile device.
2. Join a virtual consultation with a healthcare provider through A Video Connect on a desktop.
3. Access and review medical records on A Video Connect across mobile and desktop platforms.
4. Send a secure message to a healthcare provider using A Video Connect.

- **Think-Aloud Protocol:** Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts, actions, and feelings as they interact with A Video Connect and its telehealth touchpoints.

- **Data Collection:** Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions with A Video Connect. Use an observation notes template to record usability issues, task completion times, and user feedback. Time each task completion to measure efficiency.

- **Post-Session Interviews:** Ask participants about their overall experience using A Video Connect, any challenges encountered, suggestions for improvement, and their satisfaction with the platform's usability.

**For User Interviews:**
- **Interview Guide Structure:** 
Opening: Introduce the purpose of the interview and establish rapport.
Main Topics: Explore participants' experiences with A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints.
Probing Questions: Dive deeper into specific aspects of usability and user preferences.

- **Question Types:** Include behavioral questions about past interactions with A Video Connect, attitudinal questions about user satisfaction, and contextual questions about usage contexts.

- **Recording Protocols:** Obtain consent from participants to record audio/video of the interview. Ensure a quiet and well-lit environment for clear recording.

- **Analysis Approach:** Conduct thematic analysis to identify patterns and insights from user interviews. Use affinity mapping to visually organize common themes and issues. Develop a coding framework to categorize interview responses.

**For Card Sorting:**
- **Card Preparation:** Create cards representing different features of A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints. Include features like appointment scheduling, video consultation, messaging, and medical records access.

- **Sorting Methodology:** Conduct open and closed card sorts to understand how users categorize and prioritize features. Use hybrid approaches to combine quantitative and qualitative insights.

- **Analysis Procedures:** Analyze card sorting results using statistical measures like cluster analysis, dendrograms, and similarity matrices. Identify patterns in how users group features and their preferences.

- **Results Interpretation:** Translate card sorting findings into recommendations for optimizing the layout and organization of A Video Connect and its telehealth touchpoints. Use insights to improve user navigation and accessibility.

**For Survey Research:**
- **Question Design:** Design survey questions to measure user satisfaction, usability, and preferences related to A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints. Use validated scales for assessing usability metrics.

- **Sampling Strategy:** Define target demographics for survey participants, calculate sample sizes based on statistical significance, and ensure diversity in the sample population.

- **Distribution Methods:** Select appropriate platforms for survey distribution, schedule reminders to increase response rates, and adhere to ethical guidelines for data collection.

- **Statistical Analysis:** Analyze survey data using descriptive statistics to summarize responses, conduct significance testing to identify trends, and follow reporting standards for presenting findings.

**For Concept Testing:**
- **Concept Presentation:** Present prototypes of new features or design changes for A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints. Set up scenarios that simulate real-world usage situations.

- **Testing Protocol:** Have participants complete tasks related to the concept, collect feedback on usability, and rank preferences for different design options.

- **Measurement Criteria:** Evaluate usability metrics such as task completion rates, error rates, and user satisfaction. Test comprehension of new features and assess user appeal.

- **Iteration Framework:** Incorporate feedback from concept testing into iterative design improvements for A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints. Use insights to refine prototypes and enhance user experience.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- **Evaluation Framework:** Evaluate A Video Connect against competitors using heuristics and feature comparison matrices. Identify strengths and weaknesses in usability and feature offerings.

- **Documentation Standards:** Document screenshots of competitor platforms, map out interaction flows, and take detailed usability notes during comparative analysis.

- **Analysis Criteria:** Identify best practices from competitors in telehealth usability, extract insights on innovative features, and pinpoint areas for improvement in A Video Connect.

- **Synthesis Approach:** Synthesize competitive insights into actionable recommendations for enhancing A Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints. Implement strategies to address usability gaps and stay competitive in the telehealth market.
