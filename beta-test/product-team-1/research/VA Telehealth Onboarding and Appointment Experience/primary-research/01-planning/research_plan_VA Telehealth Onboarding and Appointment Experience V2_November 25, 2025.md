# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience V2

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop - Provides convenient and secure virtual healthcare appointments for Veterans, allowing them to easily connect with healthcare providers from the comfort of their own homes.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digital Services

🗓️ **Date:** November 25, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to simplify the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop. By guiding Veterans through appointment scheduling, video visit joining, and providing timely technical checks and instructions, the research aims to improve the overall telehealth experience for Veterans. This streamlined process will benefit Veterans by making it easier for them to access and utilize telehealth services, ultimately leading to increased engagement and satisfaction. From a business perspective, these improvements can result in higher utilization rates of telehealth services, increased efficiency in scheduling and conducting appointments, and overall improved patient outcomes for the VA.

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Improve the Veteran login experience

**Explanation:** Enhancing the Veteran login experience will streamline access to VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across both mobile and desktop platforms.

**Additional Priorities:**
- **Solidify the VA.gov platform:** Ensuring the stability and reliability of the VA.gov platform is essential for providing a seamless experience for Veterans using video visits.
- **Expand the flagship mobile app:** Expanding the mobile app will cater to Veterans who prefer video visits for their healthcare needs, especially those with mobility, transportation, or caregiving responsibilities.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing convenient access to healthcare services, especially for those who may have difficulty traveling to VA facilities.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use VA Video Connect and telehealth services to easily connect with healthcare providers for routine check-ups, medication management, and mental health support.
- **Putting down roots:** For veterans who are settling into civilian life and may have limited access to VA facilities, VA Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop offer a convenient way to continue receiving quality healthcare services.
- **Retiring:** As veterans transition into retirement, VA Video Connect and telehealth services can help them maintain their health and well-being without the need for frequent in-person visits to VA facilities.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop may include technical difficulties with the platform, challenges with scheduling appointments, and concerns about the quality of care provided through virtual visits.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Increase the completion rate of Veterans successfully joining telehealth visits by 20% within the next 6 months by identifying and addressing specific moments of drop-off or technical issues.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to identify specific pain points or confusion in the process of preparing for and joining telehealth visits for Veterans.
- Analyze user feedback and data on VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints to determine which content, touchpoints, and interaction patterns are most effective in building confidence and reducing technical issues.
- Implement changes based on research findings to streamline the telehealth visit process and improve user experience for Veterans.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for a detailed examination of how users interact with the platform and identifies any usability issues that may hinder their experience. This research method can provide valuable insights into how to improve the user experience and ensure that veterans can easily access and utilize telehealth services.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration appropriate for usability: Conducting usability testing sessions with participants using VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with a duration of approximately 60 minutes per session to allow for in-depth exploration of user interactions.
- Key activities and data collection techniques specific to VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop: Observing participants as they navigate the platform, conducting task-based scenarios to assess usability, collecting qualitative feedback through interviews or surveys, and analyzing user behavior and interactions.
- Specific protocols for evaluating VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop user experience: Developing a usability testing protocol that outlines the research objectives, participant recruitment criteria, tasks to be completed, data collection methods, and analysis procedures to ensure a systematic and comprehensive evaluation of the user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring that the research aligns with VA guidelines and timelines for collaboration and approval processes to meet regulatory requirements.
- Trauma-informed research considerations for veteran participants: Implementing trauma-informed practices to create a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants during the research process, taking into account potential triggers and sensitivities.
- Accessibility accommodations for diverse participant needs: Providing accommodations for participants with disabilities or diverse needs to ensure that all veterans can participate in the research and have their experiences represented.

By following this methodology, we can effectively assess the usability of VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, gather valuable insights from veteran users, and make informed recommendations for improving the user experience to better serve the needs of our target audience.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Schedule a telehealth appointment using VA Video Connect on a mobile device.

3. Send a message to your provider through the telehealth touchpoints on mobile.
4. Check your medication list using the desktop version of VA Video Connect.

- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they complete each task.

- Data collection: Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions. Use an observation notes template to record participant behaviors and comments. Time each task completion.

- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop. Inquire about any difficulties encountered and suggestions for improvement.

**For User Interviews:**
- Interview guide structure: Start with general questions about participants' experience with telehealth. Dive into specific topics related to VA Video Connect and the supporting telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop. Use probing questions to gather detailed insights.

- Question types: Include behavioral questions about actual usage, attitudinal questions about satisfaction, and contextual questions about usage environments.

- Recording protocols: Obtain consent for audio/video recording. Ensure proper setup for clear recording.

- Analysis approach: Conduct thematic analysis to identify common themes and patterns in participants' responses. Use affinity mapping to visually organize data. Develop a coding framework to categorize interview responses.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different features or content from VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop.

- Sorting methodology: Allow participants to categorize cards in a way that makes sense to them. Consider a hybrid approach combining open and closed sorting methods.

- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures to analyze sorting patterns. Create dendrograms or similarity matrices to visualize relationships between cards. Interpret results to identify user preferences and groupings.

**For Survey Research:**
- Question design: Design survey questions to measure user satisfaction, usability, and preferences related to VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop.

- Sampling strategy: Determine target demographics for the survey and calculate an appropriate sample size. 

- Distribution methods: Choose suitable platforms for survey distribution. Establish timing for sending out surveys and protocols for sending reminders to increase response rates.

- Statistical analysis: Analyze survey responses using descriptive statistics to summarize data. Conduct significance testing to identify significant differences in responses. Adhere to reporting standards for presenting survey results.

**For Concept Testing:**
- Concept presentation: Present prototypes of new features or designs for VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop.

- Testing protocol: Have participants complete tasks related to the concept, provide feedback on usability and appeal, and rank preferences.

- Measurement criteria: Use established usability metrics to evaluate the concept's effectiveness. Test participants' comprehension of the concept and assess its overall appeal.

- Iteration framework: Incorporate feedback from concept testing into the design of VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop for further refinement.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Evaluate VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop against industry heuristics and create feature comparison matrices.

- Documentation standards: Take screenshots and document interaction flows during the competitive analysis. Make detailed usability notes for each platform.

- Analysis criteria: Identify strengths and weaknesses of VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop. Extract best practices from competitors for potential implementation.

- Synthesis approach: Use competitive insights to make recommendations for improving VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop.
