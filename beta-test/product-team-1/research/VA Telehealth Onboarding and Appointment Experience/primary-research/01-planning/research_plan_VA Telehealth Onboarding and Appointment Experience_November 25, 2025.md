# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop — Provides convenient, secure access to healthcare services for Veterans through virtual appointments.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA HCD Team

🗓️ **Date:** November 25, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline and simplify the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using "My HealtheVet" (MHV). By guiding Veterans through appointment scheduling, video visit setup, and providing timely technical instructions, the research aims to improve the overall telehealth experience for Veterans. The streamlined process will benefit Veterans by reducing confusion and frustration, ultimately increasing their engagement and satisfaction with VA telehealth services. From a business perspective, the improved telehealth experience will lead to increased utilization of VA telehealth services, resulting in better health outcomes for Veterans and operational efficiencies for the VA.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for understanding how veterans interact with "" and identifying any barriers to use, ultimately improving the overall user experience.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting 45-60 minutes to allow for in-depth exploration of user interactions.
- **Key Activities:** Task-based scenarios to observe how veterans navigate through "" features, thinking aloud protocols to capture user thought process, and post-task interviews for feedback.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Use a combination of quantitative metrics (task success rates, time on task) and qualitative data (user feedback, observations) to evaluate the effectiveness and efficiency of user interactions.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Collaborate with VA stakeholders throughout the research process, providing regular updates and incorporating feedback in a timely manner.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Prioritize creating a safe and comfortable environment for veteran participants, offering breaks and ensuring consent and confidentiality throughout the sessions.
- **Accessibility:** Provide accommodations such as screen readers, large font options, and alternative input methods to meet the diverse needs of veteran participants.

**For Usability Testing:**
- **Task Scenarios:** Participants will be asked to complete the following tasks using the product: 
1. Create a new account 
2. Search for a specific item 
3. Add an item to the shopping cart 
- **Think-Aloud Protocol:** Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate through the tasks.
- **Data Collection:** Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions. Use an observation notes template to record key behaviors and issues. Time each task completion for analysis purposes.
- **Post-Session Interviews:** Ask participants about their overall experience with the product, any difficulties they encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For User Interviews:**
- **Interview Guide Structure:** Begin with an introduction, then cover topics such as shopping habits, online preferences, and experiences with similar products. Use probing questions to delve deeper into specific areas.
- **Question Types:** Include behavioral questions about past interactions with the product, attitudinal questions about satisfaction levels, and contextual questions about usage scenarios.
- **Recording Protocols:** Obtain consent for audio/video recording of the interviews. Ensure a quiet and private setting for the interviews.
- **Analysis Approach:** Conduct thematic analysis of the interview responses, create affinity maps to identify patterns, and develop a coding framework for categorizing key insights.

**For Card Sorting:**
- **Card Preparation:** Create cards representing different product features such as search functionality, product categories, and account settings.
- **Sorting Methodology:** Allow participants to conduct an open sort where they group cards based on their own criteria, followed by a closed sort where they categorize cards according to predefined categories.
- **Analysis Procedures:** Use statistical measures such as cluster analysis, dendrograms, and similarity matrices to identify patterns in the card sorting results.
- **Results Interpretation:** Translate the findings into recommendations for improving the product's information architecture and navigation.

**For Survey Research:**
- **Question Design:** Design survey questions using Likert scales for measuring satisfaction levels and validation approaches such as pilot testing to ensure question clarity.
- **Sampling Strategy:** Determine target demographics for the survey and calculate the appropriate sample size based on statistical significance.
- **Distribution Methods:** Choose an online platform for survey distribution, consider timing factors for maximum response rates, and implement reminder protocols for non-respondents.
- **Statistical Analysis:** Analyze survey responses using descriptive statistics, conduct significance testing to identify correlations, and adhere to reporting standards for presenting the results.

**For Concept Testing:**
- **Concept Presentation:** Present a prototype of the product with medium fidelity, set up scenarios for participants to interact with different features.
- **Testing Protocol:** Have participants complete tasks related to the product, collect feedback on usability, and rank their preferences for specific features.
- **Measurement Criteria:** Use usability metrics such as task success rates, conduct comprehension testing to assess understanding, and evaluate the appeal of the product concept.
- **Iteration Framework:** Incorporate participant feedback into the design process, prioritize changes based on usability issues and user preferences.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- **Evaluation Framework:** Evaluate competitors using established heuristics, compare features through matrices highlighting strengths and weaknesses.
- **Documentation Standards:** Capture screenshots of competitor products, document interaction flows, and record usability notes for reference.
- **Analysis Criteria:** Identify strengths and weaknesses of competitor products, extract best practices for potential implementation in the product.
- **Synthesis Approach:** Translate competitive insights into actionable recommendations for enhancing the product's usability and competitive edge.
