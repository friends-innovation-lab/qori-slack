## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other touchpoints. By guiding Veterans through the appointment scheduling process and providing clear technical instructions, this initiative aims to improve the overall telehealth experience and increase patient engagement. Ultimately, this will benefit Veterans by making it easier for them to access and participate in telehealth appointments, leading to improved health outcomes and satisfaction. From a business standpoint, simplifying the telehealth experience can increase utilization rates, reduce appointment no-shows, and enhance the overall efficiency of VA telehealth services.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing convenient access to healthcare services, especially for those who may have difficulty traveling to a VA facility.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use VA Video Connect and telehealth services to easily connect with healthcare providers for routine check-ups, medication management, and mental health support.
- **Putting down roots:** For veterans who are settling into civilian life or transitioning to a new location, VA Video Connect offers continuity of care and access to VA healthcare services regardless of their physical location.
- **Retiring:** As veterans enter retirement and may experience changes in their healthcare needs, VA Video Connect and telehealth services provide a convenient way to access specialized care and support without the need for frequent in-person visits.

**Critical Pain Points:** 
- Limited access to technology or internet connectivity may hinder veterans from utilizing VA Video Connect and telehealth services effectively.
- Lack of awareness or training on how to use the technology for telehealth appointments may create barriers for some veterans in accessing care remotely.
- Privacy and security concerns related to sharing personal health information online may deter some veterans from using telehealth services.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** 
To identify and address specific moments where Veterans experience technical issues or drop off during preparation for and joining telehealth visits, ultimately increasing user confidence and reducing barriers to access.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to pinpoint common pain points in the user journey of Veterans using VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints, providing actionable insights for improvement.
- Analyze user feedback and behavior to determine which content, touchpoints, and interaction patterns are most effective in building user confidence and reducing last-minute technical issues.
- Implement changes based on research findings to optimize the user experience of Veterans using telehealth services, resulting in increased engagement and successful visits.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for a deep understanding of how veterans interact with the platform and identifies areas for improvement to enhance user experience and accessibility.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration appropriate for usability: Conducting usability testing sessions with veterans using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, lasting approximately 1-2 hours each to allow for in-depth exploration and feedback.
- Key activities and data collection techniques specific to VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop: Observing participants as they navigate the platform, conducting think-aloud protocols to understand their thought process, and collecting quantitative data on task completion rates and time taken.
- Specific protocols for evaluating VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop user experience: Developing standardized tasks for participants to complete, such as scheduling appointments, joining video calls, and accessing medical records, to assess the usability and functionality of the platform.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring that all research activities align with VA policies and procedures, including obtaining necessary approvals and adhering to data security protocols.
- Trauma-informed research considerations for veteran participants: Implementing trauma-informed practices to create a safe and supportive environment for participants, acknowledging potential triggers and providing resources for support.
- Accessibility accommodations for diverse participant needs: Making accommodations for participants with disabilities or unique accessibility needs, such as providing alternative communication methods or assistive technologies.

By following this methodology, researchers can gather valuable insights into how veterans interact with VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, identify usability issues, and make informed recommendations for improving the platform to better serve the needs of users.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Schedule a telehealth appointment using VA Video Connect on mobile.
2. Join a video call with a healthcare provider using VA Video Connect on desktop.
3. Send a secure message to your provider through the telehealth touchpoint on mobile.
4. Update your personal information on the VA Video Connect app on mobile.

- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate through VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints on both mobile and desktop.

- Data collection: Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions. Use an observation notes template to record participant behavior and comments. Time each task completion for analysis.

- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Inquire about any challenges faced, suggestions for improvement, and overall satisfaction with the platform.

**For User Interviews:**
- Interview guide structure: Begin with an introduction and warm-up questions. Cover main topics such as user experiences with VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints, preferences for mobile vs. desktop usage, and suggestions for enhancement. Use probing questions to delve deeper into specific issues.

- Question types: Include behavioral questions about past experiences, attitudinal questions about preferences, and contextual questions about specific scenarios using the platform.

- Recording protocols: Obtain consent for audio/video recording. Ensure a quiet and distraction-free environment for the interview. 

- Analysis approach: Use thematic analysis to identify recurring themes in user responses. Create affinity maps to visualize connections between different user insights. Develop a coding framework to categorize and analyze interview data.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different features and functions of VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Include cards for scheduling appointments, video calls, secure messaging, updating information, etc.

- Sorting methodology: Conduct open and closed card sorting exercises to understand how users categorize and prioritize the features. Use a hybrid approach if needed to combine insights from both methods.

- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures to analyze the results of the card sorting exercises. Create dendrograms and similarity matrices to visualize the relationships between different features. Interpret the findings to make recommendations for improving the platform.

**For Survey Research:**
- Question design: Design survey questions to gather feedback on usability, satisfaction, and preferences related to VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Include validated scales for measuring user perceptions.

- Sampling strategy: Determine the target demographics for the survey and calculate the appropriate sample size. Ensure a diverse representation of users who have experience with telehealth services.

- Distribution methods: Choose suitable platforms for survey distribution. Consider timing and frequency of reminders to maximize response rates.

- Statistical analysis: Use descriptive statistics to summarize survey responses. Conduct significance testing to identify any significant differences in user perceptions. Follow reporting standards for presenting survey findings.

**For Concept Testing:**
- Concept presentation: Present prototypes of new features or enhancements for VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Set up scenarios for participants to interact with the concepts and provide feedback.

- Testing protocol: Have participants complete tasks related to the new concepts and provide feedback on usability, comprehension, and appeal. Collect preference rankings to prioritize features.

- Measurement criteria: Use established usability metrics to evaluate the concepts. Test for user comprehension of the new features and assess the overall appeal of the concepts.

- Iteration framework: Incorporate feedback from concept testing into the design process. Make iterative improvements based on user suggestions and preferences.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Use established heuristics to evaluate the usability of VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Create feature comparison matrices to compare functionalities with competitors.

- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of competitor platforms and document interaction flows. Record usability notes and observations during the evaluation process.

- Analysis criteria: Identify strengths and weaknesses of competitor platforms in comparison to VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Extract best practices to inform design decisions.

- Synthesis approach: Synthesize competitive insights to make recommendations for improving VA Video Connect and the telehealth touchpoints. Use the analysis to enhance the platform's usability and competitiveness in the telehealth market.
