## 👀 Notable Observations

1. Participants appreciated the clarity and organization of the application process, particularly noting the upfront display of different types of housing help and the progress bar for tracking completion.
2. Frustration points arose when participants encountered unclear terminology (e.g., "household composition") and technical issues such as document upload failures and slow server response times.
3. Emotional reactions varied from visible frustration during technical difficulties to relief and surprise when certain features, like the status check, worked smoothly.
4. Accessibility observations highlighted both positive features like proper form labels and keyboard navigation, as well as issues such as error messages not being announced correctly and a lack of clear instructions for keyboard users in the file upload area.

## ✅ Quick Action Items

1. Improve clarity on the household composition terminology by providing a clear definition or examples within the application form to reduce confusion for users.
   
2. Implement clear and visible file size and format requirements for document uploads to prevent users from encountering technical issues and frustration during the process.

3. Conduct a review of the disability rating validation process to ensure accurate and timely acceptance of user-provided information, enhancing trust and confidence in the system.
