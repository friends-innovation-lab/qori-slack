# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Housing Application UX Research - Pain Point Identification and Process Optimization

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal - facilitates online application, eligibility screening, document upload, and application status tracking for Veterans seeking housing benefits. This portal streamlines the application process, ensuring timely access to critical benefits within the VA ecosystem."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** Primary Team: VA Digital Experience Team Collaborating Office: Office of Veterans Experience (VEO) Support: VA Housing Benefits Administration Technical Partner: VA.gov Platform Team Accessibility Consultant: Section 508 Compliance Office  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Jordan Martinez  
**🗓️ Date:** August 25, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on redesigning the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal, focusing on the online application workflow, eligibility screening, document upload system, and application status tracking. This decision will streamline the application process for Veterans, reducing support calls and improving accessibility compliance. The findings will directly impact the roadmap for Q4 2025, guiding priorities for enhancing user experience and increasing application completion rates.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims

**Explanation:** This priority aligns with the goal of improving the efficiency and speed of the online application workflow for VA housing benefits, ensuring that veterans receive timely assistance with their disability claims.

**Additional Priorities (if applicable):**
- **Solidify the VA.gov platform:** Ensuring a stable and reliable platform for veterans to access and navigate the online application process seamlessly.
- **Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience:** Customizing the user experience to meet the specific needs and preferences of veterans applying for VA housing benefits.

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal plays a crucial role in the Putting down roots life stage for Veterans by providing a streamlined online application process for housing benefits.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans start the application process by completing eligibility screening for housing benefits.
- **Putting down roots:** Veterans upload required documents to support their housing benefits application.
- **Retiring:** Veterans track the status of their housing benefits application through the portal.

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Veterans may experience frustration if the document upload system is not user-friendly or if there are delays in updating application status.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and prioritize the top 3-5 usability barriers hindering veterans from completing online housing benefit applications, aiming to reduce the current 34% application abandonment rate by uncovering root causes.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Uncover pain points in the eligibility screening process to streamline user decision-making.
- Identify technical issues within the document upload system to improve user experience.
- Understand user frustrations with the application status tracking feature to enhance transparency and completion rates.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Competitive, usability, and interviews were chosen to directly observe veterans' interactions with the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal, identify usability barriers, and gather in-depth insights into their experiences and challenges.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Sessions will consist of competitive analysis, usability testing, and in-depth interviews to gather data on usability barriers and root causes
- Key activities include task-based usability testing, competitive analysis of similar platforms, and in-depth interviews with participants to uncover insights
- Protocols will focus on evaluating the online application workflow, eligibility screening process, document upload system, and application status tracking features of the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met by submitting research plans for approval within the designated timeline
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be integrated into the study design and participant interactions
- Accessibility accommodations will be provided for participants with assistive technologies or documented accessibility needs

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding qualified participants meeting the specific criteria
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Expand recruitment efforts to include partnerships with veteran organizations, social media outreach, and targeted advertising on platforms frequented by veterans.

**Execution issues:** Problems with implementing the competitive, usability, and interview methodologies effectively
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough training sessions for the research team on the methodologies, provide detailed guidelines and checklists for each session, and conduct pilot tests to identify and address any potential issues beforehand.

**Timeline risks:** Delays in the research process that impact the standard timeline
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones, assign responsibilities to team members, regularly track progress, and have contingency plans in place for any unexpected delays.

**Quality concerns:** Potential issues with data validity and stakeholder satisfaction
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation checks, ensure data collection tools are user-friendly and error-proof, involve stakeholders in the research process to gather feedback and address any concerns promptly.
