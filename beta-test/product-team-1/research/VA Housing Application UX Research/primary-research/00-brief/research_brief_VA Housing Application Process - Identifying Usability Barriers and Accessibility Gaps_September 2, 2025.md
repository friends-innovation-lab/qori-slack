# 📄 Research Brief – VA Housing Application Process - Identifying Usability Barriers and Accessibility Gaps

> **📦 Project Area:** Digital Services - Housing Application &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **👩🏽‍🔬 Prepared by:** Jordan Addison &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **🗓️ Date:** September 2, 2025

---

## 🎯 Business Context

The current completion rate for VA housing applications stands at 66%, with a notable drop-off occurring during eligibility screening and document upload stages. Increased call volume to the support line for housing benefits suggests significant usability challenges. An accessibility audit recently identified 12 WCAG violations. Leadership aims to prioritize improvements for the Q4 2025 roadmap, highlighting the need for data to pinpoint the most impactful pain points affecting veteran success.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Limited access to reliable transportation options is causing a decrease in employee productivity and attendance
- High transportation costs are preventing low-income individuals from accessing essential services and opportunities
- Inefficient public transportation routes are leading to longer commute times and increased stress for residents

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter the most frustration and confusion in the application process.
- Which aspects of the application process are most critical for veterans to successfully complete.
- Why veterans may struggle with certain sections of the application form.
- How veterans currently navigate the status checking and follow-up communication process.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Select desired program and complete eligibility screening questionnaire
2. Gather required documents (e.g. ID, proof of income) and upload them to the application portal
3. Complete sections 1-4 of the application form, providing accurate information
4. Review application for accuracy and submit it for processing
5. Check application status regularly and follow up with any requested information
6. Seek support from customer service if experiencing difficulties or delays in the application process

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Confusion navigating between different housing program types (e.g. loans, grants, rental assistance) leading to difficulty in selecting the most suitable option
- Document upload failures and unclear requirements causing delays in application processing
- Complex and confusing eligibility screening questions hindering veterans from accurately determining their eligibility for housing assistance programs
- Mobile experience issues, with 68% of veterans encountering usability challenges when starting applications on mobile devices
- Status tracking system lacking meaningful updates, leaving veterans uncertain about the progress of their applications
- Accessibility barriers for veterans using assistive technologies, preventing them from easily accessing and navigating housing program information

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the initial program selection and eligibility screening process, and what specific challenges do they encounter during this stage?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from successfully completing the document gathering and upload process when applying for benefits, and how do these barriers impact their overall application experience?
3. Why do veterans struggle with completing sections 1-4 of the application form, and what specific aspects of these sections prove to be the most challenging for them?
4. What causes veterans to experience difficulties during the application review and submission stage, and how do these challenges impact their ability to successfully submit their application for benefits?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Limited access to mental health services increases the risk of substance abuse among adolescents in rural communities.
- Stakeholder input suggests that lack of transportation options leads to decreased attendance at community health screenings.
- Parents' lack of knowledge about healthy eating habits contributes to childhood obesity rates in low-income neighborhoods.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, including qualitative user interviews with 5 veterans and moderated usability testing with 5 veterans who have attempted applications in the last 6 months, particularly focusing on those who utilize assistive technologies. The sessions will be conducted remotely to facilitate participation from veterans across diverse geographic locations.

**Reasoning:** This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behaviors and preferences related to the product. By combining qualitative interviews and quantitative surveys, it will provide a comprehensive understanding of both attitudes and behaviors within the scope and constraints of the study.
