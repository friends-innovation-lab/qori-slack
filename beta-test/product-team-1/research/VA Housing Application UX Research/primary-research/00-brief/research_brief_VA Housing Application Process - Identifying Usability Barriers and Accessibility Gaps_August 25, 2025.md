# 📄 Research Brief – VA Housing Application Process - Identifying Usability Barriers and Accessibility Gaps

> **📦 Project Area:** Digital Services &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **👩🏽‍🔬 Prepared by:** Jordan Martinez, Senior UX Researcher &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **🗓️ Date:** August 25, 2025

---

## 🎯 Business Context

The current completion rate for VA housing applications stands at 66%, with a notable drop-off during eligibility screening and document upload stages. An increase in support line calls from veterans, particularly in comparison to other VA benefits, suggests significant usability challenges. An accessibility audit revealed 12 WCAG violations, prompting leadership to prioritize improvements for the Q4 2025 roadmap. However, there is a need for data to identify the key pain points impacting veteran success.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Inefficient communication and collaboration among team members is causing delays in project completion and impacting overall productivity
- Users are experiencing difficulty accessing and sharing important documents, leading to frustration and decreased efficiency in their daily tasks
- Stakeholders want to address the lack of a centralized platform for information sharing and collaboration, which is hindering effective teamwork and decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter the most frustration and confusion in the initial program selection and eligibility screening process.
- Which aspects of the document gathering and upload process are most challenging for veterans.
- Why veterans struggle with completing sections 1-4 of the application form.
- How veterans currently navigate the application review and submission process.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Choose a program and complete eligibility screening questionnaire.
2. Gather required documents (e.g., ID, proof of income) and upload them to the application portal.
3. Fill out sections 1-4 of the application form with accurate information.
4. Review the completed application form for any errors or missing information, then submit it.
5. Check the status of your application regularly and follow up with any communication received.
6. Seek support from customer service or help resources if you encounter any difficulties during the application process.

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Confusion in navigating different housing program types (e.g., loans, grants, rental assistance) leading to difficulty in choosing the right option
- Challenges with uploading documents due to unclear requirements causing application delays
- Complex and confusing eligibility screening questions hindering veterans from completing applications
- Mobile experience issues affecting 68% of veterans who initiate applications on mobile devices
- Inadequate status tracking system providing unhelpful updates on application progress
- Accessibility barriers for veterans using assistive technologies impacting their ability to access and use the system efficiently

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the initial program selection and eligibility screening process, especially when using assistive technologies?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from successfully completing the document gathering and upload process when applying for programs or benefits?
3. Why do veterans experience difficulties in completing sections 1-4 of the application form, and how do these challenges impact their overall application experience?
4. What causes veterans to seek support when they encounter obstacles during the application process, and how do they typically go about seeking assistance?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Limited access to mental health resources increases the likelihood of veterans experiencing post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms.
- Stakeholder input suggests that veterans with physical disabilities face more challenges in finding employment opportunities.
- The current approach of relying solely on medication for treating chronic pain leads to negative long-term health outcomes for patients.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, incorporating qualitative user interviews with 5 veterans and moderated usability testing with 5 veterans to explore attitudinal and behavioral barriers. The focus will be on veterans who have attempted applications in the last 6 months, particularly those utilizing assistive technologies, with remote sessions conducted to accommodate participants from diverse geographic locations.

**Reasoning:** This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for a deeper understanding of consumer behavior by collecting both quantitative and qualitative data. It will provide insights into both attitudes and behaviors related to the product or service, aligning with the research objectives while staying within the scope and constraints of the study.
