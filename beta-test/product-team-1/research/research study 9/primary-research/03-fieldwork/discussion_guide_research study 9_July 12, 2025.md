# 💬 Discussion Guide – research study 9

**Session Type:** Discovery Interview  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Participant:** Veteran  

**Session Overview:**  
This Discovery Interview session will focus on exploring the experiences and attitudes of Veterans regarding the VA healthcare system. The session will delve into their interactions with VA services, their satisfaction levels, and any areas for improvement. The duration of 60 minutes allows for in-depth discussions and insights from the participants.

## 🗣️ Intro – 5 Minutes

Welcome the participant. Cover:
- Session duration and purpose
- That feedback is welcome and voluntary
- Consent for screen/audio recording
- Confirmation of observer presence
- If applicable, explain it's a prototype
- Reconfirm VA clip usage consent

> **Prompt**: "Are you okay with being recorded and having observers? This helps our team learn from your experience."

---

## ❄️ Warm-Up Questions – 5 Minutes
- Have you ever used VA benefits or services before? How was your experience?
- How comfortable are you with using digital tools or websites, especially when it comes to managing your healthcare or benefits?
- When filling out online forms or applications, have you encountered any challenges or had any positive experiences?
- Can you share a bit about any previous interactions you've had with the VA or other government services?

---

## 🧪 Task Sections

## 🎯 Task 1: Search for a Specific Product – 10 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Ask the participant to search for a specific product on the website.
2. Instruct them to use the search bar to find the product.
3. Ask them to click on the product once they have located it.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to successfully use the search bar
- Ease of finding the specific product
- Speed of locating and clicking on the product

## 🎯 Task 2: Add an Item to the Shopping Cart – 5 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Request the participant to select a product and add it to the shopping cart.
2. Instruct them to navigate to the product page and click on the "Add to Cart" button.
3. Ask them to confirm that the item has been added to the cart.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to navigate to the product page
- Understanding of the "Add to Cart" button functionality
- Confirmation of successful addition to the shopping cart

## 🎯 Task 3: Check Out and Complete a Purchase – 15 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Direct the participant to proceed to check out and complete a purchase.
2. Instruct them to review their shopping cart, enter shipping information, and proceed to payment.
3. Ask them to complete the purchase by entering payment details and confirming the order.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to review and edit items in the shopping cart
- Ease of entering shipping information
- Successful completion of the payment process

## 🎯 Task 4: Contact Customer Support for Assistance – 10 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Request the participant to find the customer support contact information on the website.
2. Instruct them to initiate contact with customer support through the provided channels.
3. Ask them to inquire about a specific product or service-related question.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to locate and access customer support information
- Ease of initiating contact through the provided channels
- Clarity and effectiveness of communication with customer support

---

## 🎤 Post-Task Interview – 10 Minutes
- Can you share your overall experience using the VA digital service? What were your initial impressions and how did they evolve throughout the tasks?
- In comparison to other government services you have used, how would you rate the usability and effectiveness of the VA digital service?
- Were there any specific features or aspects of the service that stood out to you as particularly helpful or challenging? Can you elaborate on why?
- How confident do you feel in using the VA digital service independently in the future? Are there any areas where you would like to see additional support or guidance?
- Based on your experience today, what recommendations would you make to improve the VA digital service for other users in the future?

## 🚨 Emergency Exit Strategy
In the event of technical difficulties during a VA research session, the moderator should pause the recording immediately and inform the participant of the issue. The moderator should then attempt basic troubleshooting steps such as refreshing the browser, checking the connection, or restarting the application. If the issues persist beyond 5 minutes, the moderator should offer to reschedule the session at the participant's convenience and document the technical problems encountered for future session planning.

If a participant expresses distress during a research session, the moderator should immediately pause the session and ask if the participant needs a break. The moderator should remind the participant that participation is voluntary and they can stop at any time. If the distress is related to military service, the moderator should offer mental health resources and allow the participant to collect themselves and decide whether to continue.

In the case of inappropriate content or behavior during a research session, the moderator should calmly but firmly redirect the conversation back to research topics. If the behavior continues, the moderator should politely end the session by stating, "I think we have enough information for today." The moderator should document the incident details immediately after the session and contact their research supervisor within 24 hours for guidance on next steps.

If a research session needs to be terminated for any reason, the moderator should save and secure any recordings immediately. The moderator should thank the participant professionally regardless of the reason for termination and complete incident documentation including the time, reason, and participant response. If appropriate, the moderator should follow up with the participant via email.

In case of any support needed during a research session, the moderator should provide the following contacts:
- VA Research Ethics Office: research.ethics@va.gov | (202) 443-5930
- Study supervisor: [Insert supervisor contact from research protocol]
- Mental health crisis support: Veterans Crisis Line 1-800-273-8255
- For immediate emergencies: 911

It is important to remember that participant well-being is always the top priority over research objectives in VA research sessions.
