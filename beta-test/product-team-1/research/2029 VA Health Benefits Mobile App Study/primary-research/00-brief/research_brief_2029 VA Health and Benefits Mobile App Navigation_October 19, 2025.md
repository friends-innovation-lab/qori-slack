## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks midway through. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating a poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight difficulties in finding necessary information and navigating the app. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, improving navigation is crucial for enhancing access to essential services. Actionable insights are needed for the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026, to address these challenges effectively.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential services, leading to frustration and task abandonment.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is impacting veterans' ability to access necessary healthcare services in a timely manner.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are preventing veterans from efficiently completing tasks and accessing important information, leading to a negative user experience.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most with the main menu structure and overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile).
- Which key features, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, are most difficult for veterans to discover.
- Why the information hierarchy does not align with veteran mental models, leading to confusion and frustration.
- How veterans interact with the search functionality and navigate through irrelevant or outdated search results.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Complex main menu structure with overlapping categories (e.g., Health, Benefits, Profile) causing confusion for users
- Difficulty in locating key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling leading to poor discoverability
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app causing user frustration
- Search functionality returning irrelevant or outdated results impacting user experience
- Information hierarchy not aligning with veteran mental models leading to confusion
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation hindering user interaction and usability

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to complete tasks efficiently?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation while using the app, and how does this impact their ability to access important information and features?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases user frustration and decreases overall app engagement.
2. Veterans with accessibility issues face more difficulty navigating the app and accessing key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling.
3. Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections lead to confusion and lower user satisfaction with the app experience.

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

| 🔬 **Aspect** | 📝 **Details** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Method** | This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 16 veterans to assess the current information architecture and identify optimal navigation patterns of the mobile app. The study will focus on veterans who are regular users of the app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users, with testing conducted on both iOS and Android platforms to account for differences. The research will adhere to VA research standards to ensure rigor and validity. |
| **Rationale** | This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and attitudes towards the product, providing valuable insights on their preferences and decision-making processes. The method aligns with the scope and constraints by efficiently gathering data to address key questions and generate meaningful insights for decision-making. |
