## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements, restructuring the main menu, enhancing search capabilities, and improving information hierarchy, the VA Mobile App will become more user-friendly and efficient for Veterans accessing health and benefits features. This will ultimately lead to a better user experience, increased engagement, and improved access to important resources for Veterans, resulting in higher satisfaction rates and potentially increased usage of the app.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in providing veterans with easy access to health and benefits information and services throughout their journey, from taking care of themselves to retiring.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the VA Mobile App to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, and access important health resources to take care of their physical and mental well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** The app helps veterans navigate the process of accessing benefits, such as housing assistance and education benefits, as they transition to civilian life and put down roots in their communities.
- **Retiring:** As veterans approach retirement, the VA Mobile App assists them in understanding and accessing their retirement benefits, healthcare options, and other resources available to them.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some pain points for the VA Mobile App may include a complex navigation structure, difficulty in finding specific information or services, and potential technical issues that hinder task completion for veterans trying to access health and benefits features.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing 45% of veterans to abandon tasks on the VA Mobile App, leading to a current user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to pinpoint specific pain points in the app's navigation and information architecture that contribute to user abandonment rates.
- Utilize card sorting exercises to reorganize the app's main navigation structure based on user preferences and needs.
- Analyze competitive apps in the healthcare and benefits sector to identify best practices and areas for improvement in the VA Mobile App's functionality.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research are specifically right for the VA Mobile App as they provide valuable insights into how users interact with the app, how they navigate through the main features, and how they search for health and benefits information. These methods will help identify any usability issues, optimize the information architecture, improve search functionality, and enhance task completion flows for users.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Conduct usability testing sessions with real users, card sorting exercises to understand how users categorize information, and competitive research to benchmark against other similar apps. Sessions will be approximately 1-2 hours long to allow for in-depth exploration.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Use task scenarios to evaluate navigation, observe users as they complete tasks related to health and benefits features, analyze card sorting results to inform information architecture, and compare the app's features with competitors.
- Specific protocols: Develop a usability testing script with specific tasks related to health and benefits features, create a card sorting exercise with relevant categories, and establish criteria for evaluating the app's performance against competitors.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements: Ensure that all research activities align with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and adhere to the timeline set forth by the VA.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: Approach research with sensitivity to potential trauma experienced by veteran participants, provide a safe and supportive environment, and offer resources for support if needed.
- Accessibility accommodations: Provide accommodations for diverse participant needs, such as screen readers, alternative formats, and assistive technologies, to ensure all users can participate in the research.

By implementing usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research, we can gather valuable insights into how veterans interact with the VA Mobile App, understand their needs and preferences, and make informed decisions to improve the app's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features. These methods will help optimize the user experience and ensure that the app meets the needs of its users effectively.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Find information on mental health resources available through the VA Mobile App.
2. Update your personal information in the app.
3. Schedule an appointment with a healthcare provider using the app.
- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate the app.
- Data collection: Use screen recording software to capture participants' interactions with the app, take detailed observation notes, and time how long it takes for participants to complete each task.
- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using the app, any difficulties they encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different features and functions of the VA Mobile App related to health and benefits.
- Sorting methodology: Conduct an open card sort where participants organize the cards into categories that make sense to them.
- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures to identify patterns in how participants grouped the cards, create dendrograms to visualize relationships between categories, and use similarity matrices to compare participants' sorting results.
- Results interpretation: Analyze the data to identify common themes and patterns in how participants organized the app's content, and use these insights to make recommendations for improving the app's information architecture.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Evaluate the VA Mobile App against competitors using established usability heuristics and create feature comparison matrices to identify strengths and weaknesses.
- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of key features and interactions in the app, document usability notes on the app's performance, and compare these findings to competitors.
- Analysis criteria: Identify areas where the VA Mobile App excels compared to competitors, as well as areas for improvement based on best practices observed in the competitive analysis.
- Synthesis approach: Use the insights gained from the competitive analysis to make recommendations for enhancing the app's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features.
