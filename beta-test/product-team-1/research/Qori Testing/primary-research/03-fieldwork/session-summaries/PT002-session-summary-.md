# 📋 Session Summary - PT002

**Study:** Qori Testing  
**Date:**

## 🔴 Top Pain Points
1. **Critical - Difficulty Finding Checkout Button**
The participant struggled to locate the checkout button on the product page, leading to frustration and a longer checkout process.

2. **High - Confusion with Navigation Menu**
Participant expressed confusion with the navigation menu labels, causing them to click on incorrect categories and waste time searching for specific items.

3. **Medium - Lack of Filter Options**
The absence of filter options on the search results page made it challenging for the participant to narrow down their choices, resulting in a tedious browsing experience.

## 🟡 Key Opportunities
1. **High Impact - Clearer Call-to-Action Buttons**
Opportunity to improve the visibility and clarity of call-to-action buttons, especially the checkout button, to guide users through the purchasing process more smoothly.

2. **Medium Impact - Enhanced Navigation Labels**
Opportunity to reevaluate and improve the labeling of navigation menu categories to align better with user expectations and reduce confusion during browsing.

## 💡 Key Insights
1. **User Expectations - Preference for Visual Cues**
The participant showed a strong preference for visual cues, such as prominent buttons and icons, to guide them through the website and make decisions more efficiently.

2. **Behavior Pattern - Sequential Product Evaluation**
The participant followed a pattern of sequentially evaluating products on the search results page, indicating a preference for a systematic approach to decision-making.

## ✅ Recommended Actions
- **Critical:** Improve the visibility and placement of the checkout button on the product page to streamline the purchasing process.
- **High Impact:** Conduct user testing to gather feedback on navigation menu labels and make necessary adjustments for clarity.
- **Quick Win:** Add filter options to the search results page to enhance the browsing experience and help users find products more efficiently.
