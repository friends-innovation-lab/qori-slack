## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Interviews are specifically right for QOri REsearch research because they allow for in-depth exploration of user experiences, preferences, and feedback. By directly engaging with participants, we can gather rich qualitative data that can provide insights into how to improve the QOri REsearch platform.

**Implementation Approach:**
- The interviews will be conducted in a semi-structured format, allowing for flexibility while ensuring key topics are covered. Each session will last approximately 45-60 minutes to allow for detailed discussions.
- Key activities will include asking participants about their usage of QOri REsearch, their satisfaction with the platform, any challenges they have faced, and suggestions for improvement. Data will be collected through audio recordings and detailed notes.
- Specific protocols will be developed to evaluate the user experience of QOri REsearch, including asking participants to perform specific tasks on the platform and providing feedback on their interactions.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met by regularly updating VA stakeholders on the progress of the research and incorporating their feedback into the study design.
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be taken into account when recruiting veteran participants, ensuring that sensitive topics are approached with care and respect.
- Accessibility accommodations, such as providing alternative formats for interviews or offering support for participants with disabilities, will be made to ensure that all veterans can participate in the research.

Overall, interviews are the ideal methodology for exploring the user experience of QOri REsearch as they allow for personalized interactions with participants, enabling us to gather detailed insights that can inform future improvements to the platform.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Navigate through QOri REsearch to find a specific article.
2. Use the search function to find information on a particular topic.
3. Create a new account on QOri REsearch and customize your profile.

- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they interact with QOri REsearch.

- Data collection: Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions with QOri REsearch. Use an observation notes template to record participant behaviors and comments. Time each task to measure efficiency.

- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using QOri REsearch, any difficulties encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For User Interviews:**
- Interview guide structure: 
Opening: Introduce the purpose of the interview and establish rapport.
Main topics: Explore participants' experiences with QOri REsearch, their needs and preferences, and suggestions for enhancement.
Probing questions: Ask about specific features, usability issues, and overall satisfaction with QOri REsearch.

- Question types: Include behavioral questions about usage patterns, attitudinal questions about satisfaction, and contextual questions about the environment in which QOri REsearch is used.

- Recording protocols: Obtain consent for audio/video recording of the interview. Ensure a clear recording setup to capture participant responses accurately.

- Analysis approach: Use thematic analysis to identify common themes in participants' feedback. Create affinity maps to visualize connections between different responses. Develop a coding framework to categorize interview data.

**For Concept Testing:**
- Concept presentation: Show participants a prototype of QOri REsearch with key features and functionalities. Set up scenarios for participants to interact with the prototype.

- Testing protocol: Have participants complete tasks using the prototype, provide feedback on their experience, and rank their preferences for different aspects of QOri REsearch.

- Measurement criteria: Assess usability metrics such as task completion time and error rates. Test participants' comprehension of QOri REsearch features and gather feedback on the appeal of the concept.

- Iteration framework: Incorporate feedback from concept testing into the design of QOri REsearch. Make adjustments based on participant suggestions to improve the overall user experience.
