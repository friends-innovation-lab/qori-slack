## 🗣️ Warm-up Questions

I notice you haven't provided specific details about your participants or research focus. Here's a template with general warm-up questions that can be adapted for most research sessions:

1. **Tell me a bit about yourself and what you do day-to-day.**
   - *Why this matters:* Gets participant talking comfortably about familiar territory while giving you context about their background and routine.

2. **What does a typical day look like for you?**
   - *Why this matters:* Helps understand their environment, priorities, and potential pain points without directly asking about problems yet.

3. **What's something you've been spending a lot of time on lately?**
   - *Why this matters:* Reveals current focus areas and challenges, often naturally connecting to your research topic.

4. **How do you usually approach [broad category related to your research topic]?**
   - *Why this matters:* Begins transitioning toward your research focus while still feeling conversational and non-threatening.

5. **What's working well for you right now, and what feels more challenging?**
   - *Why this matters:* Opens the door to discussing frustrations and needs, setting up your deeper research questions.

**To customize these questions:** Replace the bracketed sections with specifics about your participants (role, industry, demographics) and research focus (product, service, behavior you're studying).

## 🎯 Main Research Tasks

I notice that the research focus, questions, and participants are all listed as "Nothing." I'll assume you're asking me to design usability tasks for Nothing, the consumer technology company known for their smartphones, earbuds, and distinctive transparent design aesthetic. Here are 4 usability tasks for testing their website or app experience:

### Task 1: Product Discovery and Comparison
**Time:** 8 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You're in the market for new wireless earbuds and have heard good things about Nothing's products. You want to explore their earbud options and understand the differences between models to make an informed decision.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please find Nothing's wireless earbuds and compare the different models available. Tell me which model you would choose and why."

**Success Criteria:**
- Successfully navigates to earbuds section
- Views at least 2 different earbud models
- Can articulate key differences between models
- Makes a selection based on features or price

**What to Observe:**
- How easily they find the product category
- Whether they use comparison tools or features
- Time spent reading specifications vs. looking at images
- Confusion around product naming or model differences
- Use of filters or sorting options

**Probing Questions:**
- "What information was most important to you in making this decision?"
- "Was there anything missing that you would have wanted to know?"
- "How clear were the differences between the models?"

---

### Task 2: Community Engagement and Support
**Time:** 6 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You've been using Nothing earbuds for a few weeks and want to connect with other users to share tips and get advice. You're also curious about upcoming product releases and company news.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Find where you can connect with other Nothing users and stay updated on company news. Show me how you would ask a question about your earbuds."

**Success Criteria:**
- Locates community section or forum
- Understands how to post a question or engage
- Finds news or updates section
- Successfully navigates community features

**What to Observe:**
- Ability to locate community features
- Understanding of how to participate in discussions
- Whether they explore existing conversations
- Confusion about account requirements or sign-up process
- Interest level in community-generated content

**Probing Questions:**
- "How likely would you be to actually use this community feature?"
- "What would make you more likely to engage with other users?"
- "Did you find the type of information you were looking for?"

---

### Task 3: Purchase Process and Customization
**Time:** 10 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You've decided to purchase a Nothing Phone and want to explore any customization options available. You're ready to add it to your cart but want to make sure you're getting exactly what you want.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please go through the process of purchasing a Nothing Phone. Explore any customization or accessory options, but stop before actually completing the purchase."

**Success Criteria:**
- Successfully adds phone to cart
- Explores available colors/storage options
- Reviews accessories or add-ons
- Reaches checkout process
- Understands pricing and shipping information

**What to Observe:**
- Decision-making process for configuration options
- Whether they explore accessories or cases
- Reaction to pricing and shipping costs
- Ease of modifying cart contents
- Understanding of warranty or protection plans
- Checkout flow navigation

**Probing Questions:**
- "How did you feel about the available customization options?"
- "Was the pricing information clear throughout the process?"
- "What would make you hesitate before completing this purchase?"

### Task 4: Account Management and Order Tracking

**Scenario:** 
You purchased Nothing earbuds two weeks ago and want to check on your order status. You also want to register your product and explore what account features are available to you as a Nothing customer.

**Instruction to Participant:**

**Success Criteria:**

- Locates order tracking functionality

**What to Observe:**

- Ability to navigate account dashboard
- Understanding of product registration benefits
- Exploration of account settings or preferences
- Reaction to marketing communications options
- Use of support or help features

**Probing Questions:**
- "What value do you see in having a Nothing account?"
- "How useful is the order tracking information provided?"
- "Would you register your product? Why or why not?"

## 💭 Post-Session Interview

Here are 7 post-session interview questions for your research:

1. **What was your overall impression of the experience we just went through?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* How did it make you feel? What stood out to you most?

2. **Were there any moments where you felt confused, frustrated, or stuck during the tasks?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Can you walk me through what was happening in your mind at that moment?

3. **Before we started, what did you expect this experience would be like? How did the reality compare?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* What surprised you, either positively or negatively?

4. **How would you explain what we just did to a friend or colleague who hasn't seen it?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* What do you think the main purpose or benefit is supposed to be?

5. **If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about what we just experienced, what would it be?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Why is that particular change important to you?

6. **What would need to be different for this to feel valuable or useful in your daily life?**
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Are there specific features or improvements that would make a big difference?

7. **On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to use this [product/service]? What would make it a 10?**
