## 🎯 Key Themes

### [Usability Challenges for Older Veterans]

**What the documents show:** 
The documents highlight significant usability challenges faced by older Veterans when using telehealth platforms, with low success rates in tasks like troubleshooting and joining waiting rooms.

**Evidence from documents:**
- "Current VVC interface presents significant usability barriers for older Veterans." - from JMIR_Older_Veterans_Telehealth_Usability_2024.md.pdf
- "The 31% success rate for troubleshooting tasks is particularly concerning, as technical issues are common in real-world telehealth use." - from VA_OIG_Telehealth_Access_Report_2024.md.pdf

**Why it matters for Qori Testing:**
Understanding the specific usability challenges faced by older Veterans is crucial for designing and testing Qori solutions that cater to their unique needs and limitations.

### [Need for Simplified Joining Flows]

**What the documents show:** 
There is a need for simplified joining flows in telehealth platforms to reduce confusion and improve the overall user experience for Veterans, particularly older individuals.

**Evidence from documents:**
- "Design recommendations include simplified joining flows, prominent help-seeking options..." - from JMIR_Older_Veterans_Telehealth_Usability_2024.md.pdf
- "Goal: Reduce time-to-join from 4.5 minutes to under 1.5 minutes" - from VA_Telehealth_Modernization_Strategy_2025.md.pdf

**Why it matters for Qori Testing:**
Streamlining the joining process can enhance user engagement and satisfaction, making it essential to test and optimize simplified flows in Qori solutions.

### [Importance of Human Support Options]

**What the documents show:** 
Participants expressed a strong desire for accessible human technical support over automated troubleshooting, emphasizing the need for human backup options in telehealth platforms.

**Evidence from documents:**
- "Participants expressed strong preference for accessible human technical support over automated troubleshooting." - from JMIR_Older_Veterans_Telehealth_Usability_2024.md.pdf
- "One-click escalation to human support with full context transfer" - from VA_Telehealth_Modernization_Strategy_2025.md.pdf

**Why it matters for Qori Testing:**
Incorporating effective human support options in Qori solutions can enhance user trust, satisfaction, and overall usability, making it a critical aspect to test and refine.

### [Technical Readiness Assessment]

**What the documents show:** 
There is a focus on proactive technical readiness assessments to identify and resolve issues before appointments, aiming to improve the overall user experience and reduce technical barriers.

**Evidence from documents:**
- "Automated device/connection test 24 hours before appointment" - from VA_Telehealth_Modernization_Strategy_2025.md.pdf
- "Veterans who received pre-appointment technical check communications were 67% more likely to successfully join their first appointment without assistance." - from VA_OIG_Telehealth_Access_Report_2024.md.pdf

**Why it matters for Qori Testing:**
Conducting technical readiness assessments as part of Qori testing can help ensure a smoother user experience and address potential issues before they impact the telehealth interaction.

## ✅ Recommendations & Next Steps

**Immediate Actions** (This Week)
- [ ] Simplify Joining Experience: Based on the finding that 34% of Veterans reported difficulty joining video appointments on their first attempt due to technical issues, prioritize initiatives to reduce unnecessary confirmation steps and streamline the joining process.
- [ ] Implement Pre-Appointment Communication Protocols: Considering that only 43% of Veterans reported receiving pre-appointment technical check communications and those who did were more likely to successfully join their first appointment, establish standardized protocols including device testing links and clear joining instructions.
- [ ] Expand Technical Support Hours: Given the technical barriers faced by Veterans, especially in rural areas, expand technical support hours to include evenings and weekends to assist with any issues that may arise during telehealth appointments.

**Short-Term Priorities** (This Month)
- [ ] Enhance VVC Accessibility: Address the accessibility gaps identified, such as screen reader compatibility issues and insufficient captioning accuracy, by improving the VVC interface for users with assistive technologies.
- [ ] Develop Mobile-First Onboarding Experience: Since smartphone users experienced more audio issues but appreciated the convenience, prioritize the development of a mobile-first onboarding experience to cater to their preferences and improve overall usability.
- [ ] Create Simplified Joining Instructions: To mitigate the confusion experienced by Veterans with multiple entry points and unclear instructions, develop simplified joining instructions in multiple formats to guide users through the telehealth appointment process effectively.

**Further Research Needed**
- Explore the impact of caregiver involvement on telehealth sessions, particularly for Veterans over the age of 75, and assess the effectiveness of caregiver-specific access features.
- Investigate the effectiveness of integrating telehealth technical support into MyHealtheVet and the establishment of digital literacy training programs to enhance Veteran outcomes and user experience.
