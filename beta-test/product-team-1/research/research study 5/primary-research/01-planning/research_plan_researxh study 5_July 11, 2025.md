2. Hypothesis 2: Participants who have received therapy following childhood trauma will report lower levels of PTSD symptoms compared to those who have not received therapy.
   - This hypothesis can be tested by comparing self-reported PTSD symptoms between participants who have received therapy and those who have not through the interviews.
   - This hypothesis connects to the research goal of examining the effectiveness of therapy in mitigating the long-term effects of childhood trauma.
  
3. Hypothesis 3: Participants who have a strong support system in place will report higher levels of resilience and coping strategies in dealing with the effects of childhood trauma.
   - This hypothesis can be tested by comparing self-reported levels of resilience and coping strategies between participants with a strong support system and those without through the interviews.
   - This hypothesis connects to the research goal of investigating the role of social support in buffering the impact of childhood trauma on mental health.

## 🛠️ Methodology  
**Approach:** interviews

**Session Structure and Duration:**

- Session Format: One-on-one interviews
- Timing: 60-90 minutes per interview
- Number of Interactions: 1 interview per participant
- Length of Interactions: Each interview session will last 60-90 minutes
- Session Flow: 
  1. Introduction and rapport-building
  2. Interview questions and discussion
  3. Wrap-up and debrief

**Specific Approach and Techniques:**

- Methodology: Semi-structured interviews with open-ended questions
- Data Collection Methods: Audio recording and note-taking
- Protocols: 
  - Ensure confidentiality and anonymity of responses
  - Obtain verbal consent before recording
  - Provide participants with the opportunity to ask questions

**VA Compliance Considerations:**

- IRB Approval: Obtain IRB approval before conducting interviews
- Informed Consent: Clearly explain the purpose of the study and obtain written consent from participants
- Data Privacy: Store audio recordings securely and ensure only authorized personnel have access
- Veteran-specific Ethical Considerations: Be mindful of potential triggers related to military experiences and provide resources for support if needed

**Trauma-Informed Design Elements:**

- Support for Mental Health: Offer breaks during the interview if needed and provide resources for mental health support
- Accommodation for PTSD and Anxiety: Create a calm and safe environment, allow participants to have a support person present if desired
- Safe Research Environment: Ensure confidentiality, privacy, and respect for participants throughout the interview process
