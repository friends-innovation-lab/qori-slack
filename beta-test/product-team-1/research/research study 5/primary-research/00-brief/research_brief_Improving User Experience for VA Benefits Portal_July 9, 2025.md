## Overview

This study focuses on understanding barriers to accessing benefits for veterans through a digital platform. The goal is to identify friction points and optimize the user experience.

## Objectives

1. **Identify Barriers:**  
   1. Complex navigation
2. Lack of accessibility options
3. Long form filling process

2. **Evaluate Navigation Experience:**  
   1. Login to the portal
2. Navigate to benefits section
3. Fill out application form
4. Submit and track application status

3. **Surface Insights for Design & Content Improvements:**  
   1. Simplify navigation by implementing a clear and intuitive menu structure that guides users directly to the benefits section. This could involve reorganizing the portal layout, using descriptive labels, and reducing the number of clicks required to reach the desired page.

2. Enhance accessibility options by incorporating features such as text-to-speech functionality, high contrast modes, and keyboard shortcuts. This will make the portal more inclusive and user-friendly for individuals with disabilities or those who require alternative ways of interacting with the interface.

3. Streamline the form filling process by breaking down the application form into smaller, digestible sections. Implement progress indicators, auto-fill options, and error-checking prompts to guide users through the process efficiently and reduce the likelihood of form abandonment. Additionally, consider providing tooltips or help text to clarify any confusing or complex questions.

4. **Support Trauma-Informed Service Design:**  
   1. Simplify navigation: Consider implementing a clear and intuitive user interface with easy-to-follow pathways to access benefits. Use visual cues, such as icons and labels, to guide veterans through the digital platform and reduce cognitive load.

2. Enhance accessibility options: Ensure the digital platform is accessible to veterans with diverse needs, including those with visual or auditory impairments. Provide alternative text for images, captioning for videos, and compatibility with screen readers to make the platform inclusive for all users.

3. Streamline form filling process: Break down lengthy forms into smaller, manageable sections to prevent overwhelming veterans. Incorporate progress indicators to show users how far they have come and how much more is left to complete, reducing stress and increasing engagement. Additionally, consider implementing auto-fill features to save time and effort for veterans filling out forms.

## Initial Scoping Materials

- **Stakeholders Consulted:**  
  VA Product Team, VA Design Team, UX Researchers, Veterans using the benefits portal

- **Initial Hypotheses:**  
  1. Users will spend significantly more time navigating through the portal due to complex navigation compared to portals with simplified navigation options.
2. Users with accessibility needs will encounter more barriers and difficulties in using the portal compared to users without accessibility needs.
3. Users will have a higher rate of abandoning the portal during the long form filling process compared to shorter form filling processes.

- **Framing Questions:**  
  1. How do users currently navigate through the portal to access the benefits section, and what specific barriers do they encounter in terms of complex navigation?
2. What accessibility options do users find lacking within the portal, and how can these options be improved to enhance user experience?
3. How do users perceive the length and complexity of the form filling process within the portal, and what specific pain points do they experience during this process?
4. What are the specific challenges users face when trying to track their application status after submitting it through the portal, and how can the user flow be optimized to make this process more efficient and user-friendly?

- **Research Approach Summary:**  
  A mixed-methods approach involving user interviews, usability testing, and data analysis of portal interactions.
  
  Specific methods and techniques:
1. User interviews: Conduct semi-structured interviews with a diverse group of portal users to gather insights on their needs, preferences, and pain points. Use open-ended questions to encourage detailed responses and follow-up questions to delve deeper into specific areas.
2. Usability testing: Develop a set of tasks for users to complete on the portal while observing their interactions. Use think-aloud protocols to capture users' thought processes and identify usability issues. Consider using tools like eye-tracking software to analyze users' visual attention.
3. Data analysis of portal interactions: Collect quantitative data on users' interactions with the portal, such as click-through rates, time spent on different pages, and completion rates for specific tasks. Use statistical analysis techniques to identify patterns and trends in the data.

Session structure or data collection approach:
1. User interviews: Conduct interviews either in person or virtually, depending on participants' preferences and availability. Record the interviews with participants' consent for later analysis. Take detailed notes during the interviews to capture key insights and observations.
2. Usability testing: Schedule individual testing sessions with participants, providing clear instructions and guidance throughout the process. Use screen recording software to capture users' interactions with the portal for later review. Consider conducting multiple rounds of testing to iteratively improve the portal's usability.
3. Data analysis of portal interactions: Use web analytics tools to track users' behavior on the portal in real-time. Collect data on a continuous basis to capture changes in user interactions over time. Use data visualization techniques to present findings in a clear and actionable format.
