## 🎯 Business Context

The function "sendStudyResultMessage" is designed to efficiently communicate research findings to participants. This feature is crucial for ensuring that participants are informed about the outcomes of the study they have taken part in. By sending these result messages, researchers can maintain transparency and foster trust with their study participants.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Inefficient communication between departments is causing delays in project timelines and impacting overall productivity
- Users are experiencing difficulty accessing necessary information due to a lack of centralized data management system
- Stakeholders want to address the high employee turnover rate by identifying factors contributing to dissatisfaction and implementing retention strategies

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter barriers when trying to send study result messages.
- Which barriers veterans prioritize when attempting to send study result messages.
- Why veterans experience certain barriers when sending study result messages.
- How veterans currently navigate the process of sending study result messages in the refined study context.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Select the "Send Study Result Message" option from the study results page.
2. Choose the recipient for the study result message (e.g. study participants, research team).
3. Compose the message by typing in the text box provided.
4. Attach any relevant files or documents to the message, if necessary.
5. Review the message for accuracy and completeness.
6. Click the "Send" button to deliver the study result message to the selected recipient(s).
7. Confirm the successful delivery of the message by checking for any confirmation notifications.

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users may not receive a confirmation message after submitting their study results, leading to uncertainty about the successful transmission of their data.
- Users may encounter technical issues that prevent the study result message from being sent, such as network connectivity problems or server errors.
- Users may be unsure of the correct format or content required in the study result message, leading to delays or errors in submission.

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the process of sending study result messages to healthcare providers?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from efficiently and effectively sending study result messages through the current system?
3. Why do veterans encounter difficulties in understanding the steps involved in sending study result messages?
4. What causes veterans to experience frustration or confusion when using the sendStudyResultMessage feature?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Limited access to mental health resources increases the likelihood of veterans experiencing post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms.
- Stakeholder input suggests that lack of education on available support services leads to underutilization of mental health resources among veterans.
- Veterans with traumatic brain injuries face more challenges in accessing appropriate healthcare services compared to veterans without such injuries.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** Conduct user interviews to gather study results and insights, and communicate findings through structured messages.

**Reasoning:** This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for a deeper understanding of consumer behavior through observational and experimental methods, providing insights into actual behavior rather than self-reported attitudes. This approach aligns with the scope and constraints of the study by focusing on tangible actions and outcomes related to the research questions.
