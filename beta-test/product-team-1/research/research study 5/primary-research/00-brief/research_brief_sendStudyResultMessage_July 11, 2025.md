## 🎯 Business Context

The function "sendStudyResultMessage" is designed to facilitate the communication of research findings to participants. This feature aims to efficiently deliver study results to participants in a timely and organized manner, enhancing the overall research experience. By implementing this function, researchers can ensure that participants are informed of the outcomes of the study and can contribute to the dissemination of knowledge in a structured and effective way.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Inefficient communication between departments is causing delays in project timelines and hindering collaboration
- Users are experiencing difficulties accessing important information due to a lack of centralized data storage
- Stakeholders want to address the high turnover rate among employees by improving the onboarding process and providing better training opportunities

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter barriers in the process of sending study result messages.
- Which barriers are the highest priority for veterans when sending study result messages.
- Why veterans experience specific barriers when trying to send study result messages.
- How veterans currently navigate the user flow for sending study result messages in the given context.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Select the "Send Study Result Message" option from the study results menu.
2. Choose the recipient of the study result message from a list of participants.
3. Compose the message by typing in the text box provided.
4. Attach any relevant files or documents to the message, if necessary.
5. Review the message for accuracy and completeness.
6. Click the "Send" button to deliver the study result message to the selected participant.
7. Confirm the successful delivery of the message by checking for a confirmation notification.

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users may not receive the study result message due to technical issues with the messaging platform
- Users may not understand the content of the study result message if it is too technical or complex
- Users may overlook the study result message if it is not clearly labeled or easily accessible in their inbox
- Users may not trust the study result message if it appears to be spam or unsolicited communication

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the process of sending study result messages, and what pain points do they encounter along the way?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from efficiently and effectively utilizing the sendStudyResultMessage feature, and how do these barriers impact their overall experience?
3. Why do veterans struggle with understanding the purpose and functionality of the sendStudyResultMessage feature, and how can this confusion be addressed to improve usability?
4. What causes veterans to experience frustration or confusion when attempting to send study result messages, and how can the research approach be adjusted to better support their needs and preferences?
