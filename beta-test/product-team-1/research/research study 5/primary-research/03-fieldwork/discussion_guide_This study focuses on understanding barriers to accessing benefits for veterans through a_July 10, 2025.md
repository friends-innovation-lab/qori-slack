## 🗣️ Intro – 5 Minutes

Welcome the participant. Cover:
- Session duration and purpose
- That feedback is welcome and voluntary
- Consent for screen/audio recording
- Confirmation of observer presence
- If applicable, explain it's a prototype
- Reconfirm VA clip usage consent

> **Prompt**: "Are you okay with being recorded and having observers? This helps our team learn from your experience."

---

## ❄️ Warm-Up Questions – 5 Minutes
- Have you ever used any VA benefits or services before? How was your experience?
- How comfortable are you with using digital tools or websites, especially when it comes to managing your healthcare or benefits?
- Have you ever had to fill out an online form or application for VA benefits? How did that process go for you?
- Can you share any past experiences you've had with filing claims or checking the status of your benefits through the VA?

## 🧪 Task Sections

## 🎯 Task 1: Search for a Specific Product – 10 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Ask the participant to search for a red dress with lace sleeves.
2. Instruct them to use the search bar to find the product.
3. Ask them to click on the first search result that matches the description.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to use the search bar effectively.
- Success in locating the specific product.
- Ease of navigating to the product page.

## 🎯 Task 2: Add Item to Cart and Proceed to Checkout – 15 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Instruct the participant to add the red dress with lace sleeves to their cart.
2. Ask them to navigate to the cart page.
3. Guide them to proceed to checkout and enter their shipping information.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to add items to the cart without confusion.
- Understanding of the checkout process.
- Any difficulties encountered during the shipping information entry.

## 🎯 Task 3: Check Order Status and Tracking Information – 10 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Direct the participant to check the status of their recent order for the red dress with lace sleeves.
2. Instruct them to find the tracking information for the order.
3. Ask them to verify the estimated delivery date.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to locate the order status section.
- Ease of finding the tracking information.
- Understanding of the estimated delivery date.

## 🎤 Post-Task Interview – 10 Minutes
- Can you share your overall experience using the VA digital service? What stood out to you the most during your interaction?
- Were there any aspects of the service that exceeded your expectations? Conversely, were there any areas that fell short of your expectations?
- How does your experience with the VA digital service compare to other government services you have used in the past?
- After completing the tasks, do you feel confident in using the VA digital service independently in the future? Why or why not?
- Based on your experience, what specific suggestions do you have for improving the VA digital service to better meet the needs of users like yourself?

## 🚨 Emergency Exit Strategy
In the event of technical difficulties during a VA research session, the moderator should immediately pause the recording and inform the participant of the issue. Basic troubleshooting steps should be attempted, such as refreshing the browser, checking the connection, or restarting the application. If the issues persist beyond 5 minutes, the moderator should offer to reschedule the session at the participant's convenience and document the technical problems encountered for future session planning.

If a participant expresses distress during a research session, the moderator should immediately pause the session and ask if the participant needs a break. The participant should be reminded that participation is voluntary and they can stop at any time. Mental health resources should be offered if the distress is related to military service, and the participant should be allowed to collect themselves and decide whether to continue.

In the case of inappropriate content or behavior during a research session, the moderator should calmly but firmly redirect the conversation back to the research topics. If the behavior continues, the session should be politely ended with a statement such as "I think we have enough information for today." Incident details should be documented immediately after the session, and the research supervisor should be contacted within 24 hours for guidance on next steps.

If a research session needs to be terminated for any reason, the moderator should save and secure any recordings immediately. The participant should be thanked professionally regardless of the reason for termination, and incident documentation should be completed, including the time, reason, and participant response. A follow-up email with the participant may be appropriate depending on the circumstances.
