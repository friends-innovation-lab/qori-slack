## 🗣️ Intro – 5 Minutes

Welcome the participant. Cover:
- Session duration and purpose
- That feedback is welcome and voluntary
- Consent for screen/audio recording
- Confirmation of observer presence
- If applicable, explain it's a prototype
- Reconfirm VA clip usage consent

> **Prompt**: "Are you okay with being recorded and having observers? This helps our team learn from your experience."

---

## ❄️ Warm-Up Questions – 5 Minutes
- Have you ever interacted with the VA for benefits or services before? 
- How comfortable are you using digital tools or websites, like those the VA might provide? 
- Can you share a bit about any previous experiences you've had with filling out forms or applications online, especially for government services like the VA? 
- Have you ever had to check the status of a claim with the VA or another government agency? How did that go?

## 🧪 Task Sections

## 🎯 Task 1: Search for a Specific Product – 10 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Ask the participant to search for a red dress under $50 on the website.
2. Instruct them to filter the search results by size and color.
3. Ask them to add the dress to their cart and proceed to checkout.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to use search and filter functions effectively
- Understanding of how to add items to the cart
- Any confusion with the checkout process

## 🎯 Task 2: Update Account Information – 5 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Request the participant to update their email address in their account settings.
2. Instruct them to change their password as well.
3. Ask them to save the changes and log out of the account.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ease of locating and accessing account settings
- Ability to update email and password successfully
- Any issues with saving changes or logging out

## 🎯 Task 3: Find Store Locations and Hours – 8 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Direct the participant to find the nearest store location on the website.
2. Ask them to check the store hours for that location.
3. Instruct them to find the contact information for that store.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Efficiency in locating store information
- Understanding of store hours and contact details
- Any difficulties in navigating to the store location page

## 🎯 Task 4: Add a Review for a Previous Purchase – 7 Minutes

**Instructions for Moderator:**
1. Have the participant find a product they have previously purchased on the website.
2. Ask them to write a review for that product and submit it.
3. Instruct them to view the review they just submitted on the product page.

**Things to Watch For:**
- Ability to locate past purchases and write a review
- Understanding of the review submission process
- Any confusion when trying to view the submitted review on the product page

---

## 🎤 Post-Task Interview – 10 Minutes
- Can you describe your overall experience with using the VA digital service? 
- What aspects of the service worked well for you, and what was challenging or frustrating? 
- How does your experience with the VA digital service compare to your expectations or previous experiences with similar government services? 
- Do you feel confident and comfortable using the VA digital service independently in the future? 
- Based on your experience, what specific suggestions do you have for improving the VA digital service?

## 🚨 Emergency Exit Strategy
In the event of technical difficulties during a VA research session, the moderator should pause the recording immediately and inform the participant of the issue. The moderator should then attempt basic troubleshooting such as refreshing the browser, checking the connection, or restarting the application. If the issues persist beyond 5 minutes, the moderator should offer to reschedule the session at the participant's convenience. It is important to document the technical problems encountered for future session planning.

If a participant expresses distress during a research session, the moderator should immediately pause the session and ask if the participant needs a break. The moderator should remind the participant that participation is voluntary and they can stop at any time. If the participant expresses distress related to their military service, the moderator should offer mental health resources. The participant should be allowed to collect themselves and decide whether to continue with the session.

In the case of inappropriate content or behavior during a research session, the moderator should calmly but firmly redirect the conversation back to the research topics. If the behavior continues, the moderator should politely end the session by stating, "I think we have enough information for today." The incident details should be documented immediately after the session, and the research supervisor should be contacted within 24 hours for guidance on next steps.

If a research session needs to be terminated for any reason, the moderator should save and secure any recordings immediately. The participant should be thanked professionally regardless of the reason for termination. Incident documentation should be completed, including the time, reason for termination, and the participant's response. If appropriate, the moderator should follow up with the participant via email.

In case of any emergency or need for support during a research session, the following contacts should be readily available:
- VA Research Ethics Office: research.ethics@va.gov | (202) 443-5930
- Study supervisor: [Insert supervisor contact from research protocol]
- Mental health crisis support: Veterans Crisis Line 1-800-273-8255
- For immediate emergencies: 911

It is important to remember that participant well-being is always the top priority over research objectives in VA research sessions.
