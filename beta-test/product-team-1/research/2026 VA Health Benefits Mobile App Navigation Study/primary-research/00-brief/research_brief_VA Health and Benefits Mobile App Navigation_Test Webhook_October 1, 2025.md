## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues with finding necessary services and confusing navigation. With 78% of veteran interactions occurring on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for ensuring veterans have access to essential services. Therefore, actionable insights are needed to inform the redesign of the mobile app scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to frustration and task abandonment.
- Poor discoverability of key features is impacting veteran access to essential services, potentially resulting in missed appointments or delays in medication refills.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are hindering veterans' ability to efficiently navigate the app, leading to a subpar user experience and potentially discouraging app usage.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most with the main menu structure and overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile)
- Which key features veterans prioritize in the app, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling
- Why veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections
- How veterans interact with the search functionality and why they encounter irrelevant or outdated results

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users are confused by the main menu structure due to overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile.
- Key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling are difficult to discover within the app.
- Navigation patterns vary inconsistently between different sections of the app.
- The search functionality often returns irrelevant or outdated results.
- The information hierarchy does not align with veteran mental models, leading to confusion.
- Accessibility issues exist with touch targets and screen reader navigation, hindering user experience.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate through the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories like Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation while using the app, and how does this affect their ability to access important information and features?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. "Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases the likelihood of users getting lost or frustrated while trying to navigate the app."
2. "Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling leads to decreased user engagement and satisfaction with the app."
3. "Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections result in users feeling disoriented and unsure of how to find the information they need."

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

| 🔬 **Aspect** | 📝 **Details** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Method** | This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 16 veterans to assess the current information architecture and identify optimal navigation patterns of the mobile app. The research will focus on veterans who regularly use the app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users, and will include testing on both iOS and Android platforms to account for platform differences in accordance with VA research standards. |
| **Rationale** | This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and preferences through qualitative interviews and observations, providing valuable insights into the decision-making process.
