# 📄 Research Brief – 2026 VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation

## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks midway. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function, suggesting inadequate information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues with finding necessary features and navigating the app. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving access to essential services. Actionable insights are needed for the redesign of the mobile app, set for Q1 2026.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Limited access to reliable transportation options is causing a decrease in employee productivity and attendance
- Inefficient communication channels are preventing timely and accurate information sharing among team members
- High turnover rates are impacting overall team morale and company performance

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter barriers in accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app.
- Which features within the app are most important to veterans in terms of initial feature discovery and onboarding.
- Why veterans struggle with appointment scheduling and modification processes within the app.
- How veterans interact with search behavior and results within the app to find relevant information efficiently.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Download the app and create an account to begin onboarding process
2. Explore initial features through a guided tutorial or walkthrough
3. Navigate the main dashboard to access different sections and complete tasks
4. Access health records and manage prescriptions through designated tabs or menus
5. Schedule appointments and make modifications as needed through the app
6. Check benefits status and submit documents through a dedicated section
7. Manage profile information and adjust notification settings in the app
8. Conduct searches within the app and interact with results to find desired information

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile leading to difficulty in finding desired information
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling due to buried navigation paths
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app causing user confusion
- Search functionality that returns irrelevant or outdated results, hindering users from finding accurate information
- Information hierarchy that does not align with veteran mental models, leading to cognitive dissonance
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impacting user experience for individuals with disabilities

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the app onboarding process and discover initial features, and what challenges do they face in understanding and utilizing these features effectively?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app, and how do these barriers impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans struggle with the appointment scheduling and modification process within the app, and what specific pain points do they encounter during this task?
4. What causes difficulties for veterans when checking their benefits status and submitting documents through the app, and how can these challenges be addressed to improve the user experience and efficiency of this task?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Lack of access to affordable childcare increases the likelihood of parents missing work or being unable to pursue higher education.
- Limited transportation options lead to decreased attendance at community events and activities.
- Inadequate communication between healthcare providers and patients results in higher rates of medication non-adherence.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 16 veterans to assess the current information architecture and identify optimal navigation patterns of the mobile app. The focus will be on veterans who regularly use the app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users, with testing conducted on both iOS and Android platforms to account for differences.

**Reasoning:** This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and preferences, providing valuable insights into the factors influencing purchasing decisions. By combining both quantitative and qualitative methods, this approach will offer a comprehensive understanding of consumer attitudes and behaviors within the scope and constraints of the study.
