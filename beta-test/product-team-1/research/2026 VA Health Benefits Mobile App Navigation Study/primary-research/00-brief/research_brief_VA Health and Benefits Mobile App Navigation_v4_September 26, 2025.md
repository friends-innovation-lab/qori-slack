## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a user satisfaction rating of 4.2 out of 10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks mid-completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, highlighting issues with the app's information architecture. App store reviews consistently mention difficulties in finding necessary resources and navigating the app. With 78% of veteran interactions occurring on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving access to essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights to inform the redesign of the mobile app scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion.
- Poor discoverability of key features and inconsistent navigation patterns are impacting veteran access to essential services, potentially delaying or preventing them from receiving necessary healthcare and benefits.
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation are hindering veterans with disabilities from effectively using the app, leading to potential exclusion and reduced access to critical services.

<br><br>

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter confusion in the main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile).
- Which key features, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, are most difficult for veterans to discover.
- Why the information hierarchy in the app does not align with veteran mental models.
- How veterans interact with the search functionality and navigate through irrelevant or outdated search results.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Unclear main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) leads to user confusion and difficulty in navigating the app efficiently.
- Key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling are not easily discoverable, causing frustration for users trying to access essential services.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app result in a disjointed user experience and make it challenging for users to predict where to find information.
- Search functionality returns irrelevant or outdated results, hindering users from quickly locating the information they need.
- Information hierarchy does not align with veteran mental models, making it harder for users to understand the app's content and structure.
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation create barriers for users with disabilities, impacting their ability to interact with the app effectively.

<br><br>

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to complete tasks efficiently?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation while using the app, and how does this impact their ability to access important information and features?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. "Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases user frustration and decreases task completion rates."
2. "Veterans with visual impairments face more difficulties with touch targets and screen reader navigation, leading to decreased app usability."
3. "Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections result in users getting lost and abandoning key tasks like prescription refills and appointment scheduling."
