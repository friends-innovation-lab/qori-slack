## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks midway. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues such as difficulty in finding necessary information and confusing navigation. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for ensuring veterans' access to essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights to inform the redesign of the mobile app scheduled for Q1 2026.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential services, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is impacting VA operations by increasing the workload on call centers and in-person service centers as veterans seek assistance outside of the app.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are preventing veterans from efficiently accessing necessary resources, resulting in decreased utilization of VA services and potentially compromising the quality of care provided.

<br>

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most with the main menu structure and overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile).
- Which key features veterans prioritize in the app, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling.
- Why veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections.
- How veterans interact with the search functionality and why they encounter irrelevant or outdated results.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Sign up for the app and complete profile information
2. Explore the app features through a guided tutorial
3. Navigate the main dashboard to view upcoming tasks and notifications
4. Access health records and manage prescriptions through designated tabs
5. Schedule appointments with healthcare providers and make modifications as needed
6. Check benefits status and submit relevant documents for review
7. Manage profile information and adjust notification settings
8. Use the search function to find specific information and interact with search results

<br>

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile, leading to difficulty in finding relevant information.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling due to unclear navigation paths and lack of prominent placement.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections causing confusion and frustration for users.
- Search functionality that returns irrelevant or outdated results, making it challenging for users to find the information they need.
- Information hierarchy that doesn't align with veteran mental models, resulting in confusion and difficulty in navigating the app.
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation, hindering users with disabilities from effectively using the app.

<br>

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to complete tasks efficiently?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation within the app, and how does this impact their ability to access important information and features?

**Team hypotheses:**
- "Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories increases the likelihood of users missing key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling."
- "Veterans with limited digital literacy face more difficulty navigating the app due to inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections."
- "The current design/approach of the app leads to decreased user satisfaction and engagement due to poor information hierarchy and accessibility issues."

<br>

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 8 veterans. The focus will be on evaluating the current information architecture and identifying optimal navigation patterns for the mobile app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users, and conducting testing on both iOS and Android platforms in accordance with VA research standards.

**Reasoning:** This refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it will allow for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and preferences through qualitative interviews and observation. This method will provide valuable insights into the underlying motivations and decision-making processes of the target audience, aligning with the goal of understanding consumer preferences and behaviors within the scope and constraints of the study.
