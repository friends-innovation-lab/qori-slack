## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating a lack of effective information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues such as difficulty in finding necessary resources and confusion in navigating the app. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for ensuring veterans have access to essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights to inform the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle in finding essential features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion.
- Poor information architecture is resulting in veterans relying heavily on the search function to locate basic features, impacting their overall user satisfaction and ability to access essential services efficiently.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns and accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation are hindering veterans from easily navigating the app, leading to a subpar user experience and potentially affecting their access to critical health and benefits information.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter confusion in the main menu structure and how it affects their ability to navigate the app efficiently.
- Which key features veterans struggle to discover, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, and how this impacts their overall experience.
- Why the information hierarchy within the app does not align with veteran mental models and how this contributes to navigation difficulties.
- How accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impact veteran interaction with the app.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Inconsistent main menu structure with overlapping categories (e.g., Health, Benefits, Profile) causing confusion for users trying to navigate the app efficiently
- Lack of visibility for key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to users struggling to access essential functions
- Varied navigation patterns across different sections of the app creating a disjointed user experience
- Search functionality returning irrelevant or outdated results, hindering users from finding the information they need quickly
- Information hierarchy not aligning with veteran mental models, making it challenging for users to locate and prioritize important content
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impacting the usability of the app for individuals with disabilities.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to complete tasks efficiently?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation within the app, and how does this impact their ability to access important information and features?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. The confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories in the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation_v5 increases user frustration and decreases task completion rates.
2. Veterans with visual impairments face more difficulty navigating the app due to accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation.
3. The inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections lead to user confusion and lower overall satisfaction with the app experience.
