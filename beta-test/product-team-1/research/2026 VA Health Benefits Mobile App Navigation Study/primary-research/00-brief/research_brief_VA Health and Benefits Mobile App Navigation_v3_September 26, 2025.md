# Research Brief — VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation_v3

**Project Area:** Digital Services | **Prepared by:** Taylor Kim, Mobile UX Researcher | **Date:** September 26, 2025

---

## Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating a lack of effective information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight difficulties in finding necessary resources and navigating the app. With 78% of veteran interactions occurring on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving access to essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights for the redesign of the mobile app scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion for veterans, impacting their ability to access essential services efficiently.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is leading to increased reliance on the search function, indicating a lack of intuitive information architecture that hinders veterans' ability to navigate the app effectively.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections and accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation are creating barriers for veterans with disabilities, impacting their ability to access and utilize the app's features effectively.

## Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans are experiencing confusion in the main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile).
- Which key features, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, are most difficult for veterans to discover.
- Why the information hierarchy in the app does not match veteran mental models.
- How veterans are interacting with the search functionality and what causes irrelevant or outdated results to be returned.

<details>
<summary><strong>User Journey in Scope</strong></summary>

1. Sign up for the app and complete the onboarding process by entering personal information and setting up a profile.
2. Explore the main dashboard by navigating through different tabs and menus to discover features such as health records access, prescription management, appointment scheduling, benefits status checking, and profile management.
3. Access health records by selecting the appropriate tab on the dashboard, entering necessary credentials, and viewing or updating medical information.
4. Manage prescriptions by locating the prescription management feature on the dashboard, adding new prescriptions, refilling existing ones, or setting reminders for medication.
5. Schedule appointments by selecting the appointment scheduling option, choosing a date and time, and confirming the booking. Modify appointments by accessing the appointment section and making necessary changes.
6. Check benefits status by navigating to the benefits status checking feature, entering relevant details, and viewing current benefit information. Submit documents related to benefits by following the document submission process.
7. Manage profile settings by accessing the profile management section, updating personal information, changing notification settings, and customizing preferences.
8. Perform searches within the app by using the search bar, entering keywords or phrases, and interacting with the search results to find relevant information.

</details>

## Target Barriers

- Complex main menu structure with overlapping categories (e.g., Health, Benefits, Profile) leading to confusion for users
- Lack of visibility for key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, making them difficult to find
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app causing user disorientation
- Search functionality returning irrelevant or outdated results, hindering users from finding the information they need
- Information hierarchy not aligning with veteran mental models, leading to user frustration and difficulty in locating desired content
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impacting user interaction and usability of the app

## Key Questions & Hypotheses

### Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to complete tasks efficiently?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation while using the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and how does this impact their ability to access important information and features?

### Team Hypotheses
1. Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases user frustration and decreases overall app engagement.
2. Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling leads to decreased user satisfaction and increased reliance on alternative methods for accessing healthcare services.
3. Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections result in user confusion and longer task completion times for veterans using the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation_v3.

## Proposed Research Approach

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Method** | This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 16 veterans. The research will focus on evaluating the current information architecture and identifying optimal navigation patterns for the mobile app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users while conducting testing on both iOS and Android platforms to account for platform differences, in accordance with VA research standards. |
| **Rationale** | The refined research approach is suitable for the research objectives as it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and preferences, providing valuable insights into the reasons behind their choices. By focusing on both behavioral and attitudinal aspects, this method will offer a comprehensive understanding of the target audience, aligning with the scope and constraints of the study. |

## Expected Outcomes

Research will inform:
- Improvements to the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation_v3
- Prioritization of features and strategies for the app
- Optimization of the user experience and navigation process within the app
