## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks mid-completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating a lack of effective information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues such as difficulty in finding needed information and confusion in navigation. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, improving navigation is crucial for ensuring veterans have seamless access to essential services. Actionable insights are needed for the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026, to address these challenges and enhance the user experience for veterans.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to frustration and task abandonment.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is impacting veteran access to essential services, potentially delaying necessary medical care and increasing workload for VA staff.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are hindering veterans' ability to efficiently navigate the app, leading to a disjointed user experience and decreased user satisfaction.

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most with the main menu structure and overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile).
- Which key features veterans prioritize in their user flows, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling.
- Why the information hierarchy does not align with veteran mental models, leading to confusion and abandonment of tasks.
- How veterans currently interact with the search functionality and navigate through the app sections with inconsistent navigation patterns.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Complex main menu structure with overlapping categories (e.g., Health, Benefits, Profile) leading to user confusion and difficulty in finding relevant information
- Low discoverability of key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, hindering user efficiency and task completion
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app causing user disorientation and frustration
- Search functionality returning irrelevant or outdated results, impacting user ability to quickly find desired information
- Information hierarchy not aligning with veteran mental models, leading to user confusion and cognitive overload
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation affecting user experience for individuals with disabilities

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to efficiently complete tasks such as accessing health records or managing prescriptions?
4. What causes veterans to encounter accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation while using the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and how does this impact their overall usability and satisfaction with the app?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
- "Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases user frustration and decreases task completion rates"
- "Veterans with visual impairments face more difficulties with touch targets and screen reader navigation, leading to lower app engagement"
- "Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections result in longer task completion times and increased user errors"
