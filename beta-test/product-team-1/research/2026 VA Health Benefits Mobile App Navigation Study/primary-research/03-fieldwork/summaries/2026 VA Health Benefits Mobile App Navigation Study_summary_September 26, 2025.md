## 👀 Notable Observations

1. **Positive Reaction to Accessibility Features**: Users expressed genuine surprise and satisfaction at the large text scaling and clear main navigation buttons, highlighting successful inclusive design implementation that exceeded expectations compared to other apps.

2. **Frustration with Loading Times**: Users experienced frustration due to slow loading times, with one user mentioning that the app took longer than expected even for someone used to slower connectivity in rural areas. This indicates a critical need for performance optimization to enhance user experience.

3. **Critical Accessibility Violations**: Observations revealed significant accessibility gaps, such as data tables lacking proper header associations, form error messages not being announced by screen readers, and focus management issues in modal dialogs. Addressing these issues is crucial to ensure usability for users relying on assistive technology.

4. **Constructive Feedback and Recommendations**: Despite identifying accessibility issues, users provided constructive feedback and recommendations for improvement, emphasizing the app's potential with focused accessibility enhancements and UX refinements. This highlights the importance of incorporating user feedback for iterative design improvements.

## ✅ Quick Action Items

1. Implement proper table header associations in data tables to make information accessible to screen readers.
2. Ensure form error messages are programmatically associated with form fields for TalkBack announcement to assistive technology users.
3. Conduct a comprehensive accessibility audit with assistive technology testing to identify and address all critical violations.
