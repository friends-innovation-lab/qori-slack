## 🔎 Background

This research will guide the product team in determining which navigation improvements to prioritize for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026, including main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes. By improving the app's navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality, Veterans will have a more user-friendly experience accessing health and benefits features, ultimately leading to increased satisfaction and ease of task completion. This strategic redesign will not only enhance the user experience for Veterans but also drive increased engagement and utilization of the VA Mobile App, ultimately supporting the VA's mission to provide accessible and efficient services to those who have served our country.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing easy access to health and benefits information and services, helping veterans take care of themselves, put down roots, and eventually retire with the support they need.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** The VA Mobile App allows veterans to easily access health resources, schedule appointments, and track their wellness progress.
- **Putting down roots:** Veterans can use the app to explore housing and education benefits, as well as connect with community resources to establish a stable post-service life.
- **Retiring:** The app assists veterans in navigating the transition to retirement by providing information on financial benefits, employment opportunities, and healthcare options.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some pain points for the VA Mobile App may include complex navigation, difficulty finding specific information or services, and potential technical glitches that hinder task completion. Improving the user experience and streamlining the app's functionality could help alleviate these issues for veterans.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing 45% of veterans to abandon tasks on the mobile app, and uncover the root causes behind the low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct card sorting exercises to understand how users categorize and prioritize information on the app.
- Evaluate the usability of the main navigation structure, search functionality, and task completion flows within the health and benefits sections of the VA Mobile App.
- Compare the app's navigation and architecture with those of competitors to identify areas for improvement.
- Gather user feedback and conduct user testing to inform decision-making and prioritize improvements to enhance user satisfaction.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting, usability testing, and competitive analysis are appropriate for this research as they provide valuable insights into the user experience, navigation structure, and functionality of the VA Mobile App. Cardsorting helps to understand how users categorize information, usability testing evaluates the app's ease of use, and competitive analysis offers insights into industry best practices.

**Implementation Approach:**
- For cardsorting, we will conduct individual sessions with participants, allowing them to organize content into categories that make sense to them. Sessions will last approximately 30-60 minutes.
- Usability testing will involve participants completing specific tasks within the app while we observe and collect data on their interactions and feedback. Sessions will last around 60-90 minutes.
- Competitive analysis will involve evaluating similar apps in the market to identify strengths and weaknesses that can inform improvements to the VA Mobile App.

**Specific protocols for VA Mobile App:**
- For cardsorting, participants will be asked to organize the main navigation structure of the app, helping us understand how users expect to find information.
- Usability testing will focus on the information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features of the app.
- Competitive analysis will involve comparing features, design elements, and user experiences of similar apps to the VA Mobile App.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be followed throughout the research process, ensuring that stakeholders are involved and informed at each stage.
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be taken into account, ensuring that participants feel safe and comfortable during the sessions.
- Accessibility accommodations will be provided to ensure that all participants, including those with disabilities, can effectively participate in the research.
