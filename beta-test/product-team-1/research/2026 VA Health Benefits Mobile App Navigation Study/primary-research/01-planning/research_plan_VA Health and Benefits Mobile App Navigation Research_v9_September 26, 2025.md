## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions regarding the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing improvements such as main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes, the app will be more user-friendly and efficient for Veterans accessing health and benefits features. Ultimately, the streamlined and user-centric design of the app will improve user experience, increase engagement, and drive higher usage rates, leading to better outcomes for Veterans and a more effective delivery of VA services.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in assisting veterans in accessing and managing their health and benefits information, providing a seamless experience for them throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, track their health information, and access resources for mental health support.
- **Putting down roots:** The app allows veterans to explore housing and education benefits, as well as connect with employment resources to help them transition back to civilian life.
- **Retiring:** Veterans can use the app to navigate their retirement benefits, access financial planning tools, and stay connected to the VA healthcare system as they transition into retirement.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App may include difficulties in navigating the main menu, locating specific health and benefits features, and understanding how to complete tasks effectively within the app. Additionally, search functionality may need to be improved to ensure veterans can easily find the information they need.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Conduct user testing to identify the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that lead to 45% of veterans abandoning mobile app tasks, and determine the root causes behind the current 4.2/10 user satisfaction rating.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to identify specific pain points in the main navigation structure and information architecture that contribute to user abandonment rates.
- Implement card sorting exercises to understand how users categorize information and improve the app's organization based on user preferences.
- Analyze competitive analysis to identify best practices in navigation and information architecture that can be applied to the VA Mobile App.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research are specifically right for evaluating the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features because they provide valuable insights into how users interact with the app, identify pain points, and suggest improvements based on user preferences and behaviors.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Conducting usability testing sessions with a mix of individual and group sessions lasting around 60-90 minutes each to gather in-depth feedback on user interactions with the app.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Utilizing card sorting exercises to understand how users categorize and prioritize information within the app, conducting competitive research to benchmark against other similar apps, and observing task completion flows to identify usability issues and areas for improvement.
- Specific protocols: Using task scenarios to evaluate specific features related to health and benefits, collecting qualitative and quantitative data through surveys, interviews, and observation, and analyzing user behavior and feedback to inform design decisions.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring compliance with VA regulations and guidelines throughout the research process, including obtaining necessary approvals and adhering to timelines for collaboration and feedback.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: Implementing trauma-informed practices to create a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants, including providing resources and support for those who may have experienced trauma.
- Accessibility accommodations: Making accommodations for diverse participant needs, such as providing alternative formats, ensuring compatibility with assistive technologies, and conducting research in accessible locations.

By combining usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research methodologies, we can gain a comprehensive understanding of how veterans interact with the VA Mobile App, identify areas for improvement in the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features, and ensure that the app meets the needs and preferences of its users.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Find information about available health benefits for veterans.
2. Navigate to the search function and find a specific health clinic near the participant's location.
3. Complete the process of scheduling an appointment with a healthcare provider.
- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate the app.
- Data collection: Use screen recording software to capture participant interactions, take detailed observation notes, and time how long it takes to complete each task.
- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using the app, any difficulties they encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different sections and features of the app, such as "Health Benefits," "Search Functionality," "Appointment Scheduling," etc.
- Sorting methodology: Conduct an open card sort where participants group cards based on their understanding of the app's structure.
- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures to determine common groupings and relationships between cards, and create a dendrogram to visualize the results.
- Results interpretation: Translate the card sorting findings into recommendations for restructuring the app's navigation and information architecture.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Use heuristics to evaluate the app's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows compared to competitors.
- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of key features and interactions, document usability notes, and compare features side by side.
- Analysis criteria: Identify strengths and weaknesses in the app compared to competitors, and extract best practices to apply to the app.
- Synthesis approach: Translate insights from the competitive analysis into actionable recommendations for improving the app's features and functionality.
