## 🔎 Background

This research will provide valuable insights to the product team regarding the prioritization of navigation improvements for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026, focusing on main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes. By informing decisions on progressive disclosure, dashboard layout redesign, and core feature discoverability, the research aims to enhance task completion flows for Veterans accessing health and benefits features on the app. Ultimately, these improvements will streamline user experience, making it easier and more efficient for Veterans to access vital information and services, leading to increased user satisfaction and engagement with the VA Mobile App.

<br>

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in helping veterans navigate and access their health and benefits information easily and efficiently throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, track their health data, and access resources for mental health and wellness.
- **Putting down roots:** The app provides tools for veterans to explore housing assistance programs, education benefits, and employment resources to establish stability in their post-military life.
- **Retiring:** As veterans transition into retirement, the app offers information on financial benefits, long-term care options, and support services to ensure a smooth transition into this new phase of life.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App may include complex navigation, difficulty in finding specific information or services, and technical glitches that hinder task completion for veterans trying to access their health and benefits features.

<br>

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing veterans to abandon tasks on the mobile app, leading to low user satisfaction.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct card sorting exercises to understand how veterans categorize and prioritize information on the app.
- Analyze the competitive landscape to identify best practices in navigation and information architecture for similar apps.
- Conduct usability testing to identify pain points in task completion flows within the health and benefits features of the app.
- Gather user feedback and conduct surveys to understand the root causes behind the low user satisfaction rating and prioritize improvements.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting, competitive analysis, and usability testing are appropriate for this research as they will help us understand how users organize and categorize information, compare our app against competitors, and identify usability issues to improve the overall user experience.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Each cardsorting session will last approximately 1 hour, competitive analysis will involve reviewing and comparing competitor apps over a period of 2 weeks, and usability testing sessions will be 1-2 hours each.
- Key activities and data collection: Cardsorting will involve participants organizing content into categories, competitive analysis will focus on identifying strengths and weaknesses of competitor apps, and usability testing will involve participants completing tasks while providing feedback.
- Specific protocols for VA Mobile App: We will focus on evaluating the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for health and benefits features within the VA Mobile App.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements: We will ensure that all research activities align with the Collaboration Cycle requirements set forth by the VA.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: We will approach all research activities with sensitivity to potential trauma triggers and ensure that participants feel safe and supported throughout the process.
- Accessibility accommodations: We will make accommodations for participants with disabilities to ensure that they can fully participate in the research activities.

## 🧲 Recruitment

### Target Participants
- **Primary:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs
- **Total Participants:** Card Sorting: 8 participants (45-minute hybrid sessions)
Mobile Usability Testing: 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

### Demographics
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era: 4 participants from the post-9/11 era, 2 participants from the Gulf War era, 2 participants from the Vietnam era
- Age: 4 participants aged 25-34, 2 participants aged 35-44, 2 participants aged 45-54
- Gender: 4 male participants, 4 female participants
- Accessibility needs: 2 participants with visual impairments, 2 participants with motor impairments, 2 participants with hearing impairments, 2 participants with cognitive impairments

**Secondary targets:**
- VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features usage patterns
- Technology comfort levels: 4 participants who are tech-savvy and comfortable using mobile devices, 4 participants who are less tech-savvy and may struggle with using personal devices
