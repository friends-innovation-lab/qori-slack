## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements and enhancing search capabilities, the app will provide Veterans with a more intuitive and efficient user experience, ultimately improving their access to health and benefits features. This strategic focus on user-centered design will not only enhance user satisfaction and engagement but also drive increased usage and adoption of the VA Mobile App, ultimately leading to improved outcomes for Veterans and a more efficient delivery of services by the VA.

<br>

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing easy access to health and benefits information and services, helping veterans take care of themselves, put down roots, and transition into retirement.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to schedule medical appointments, refill prescriptions, track their health data, and access mental health resources.
- **Putting down roots:** The app offers tools for finding housing assistance, education benefits, employment resources, and other support services to help veterans establish stability and build their lives.
- **Retiring:** As veterans transition into retirement, the app provides information on pension benefits, insurance options, long-term care services, and other resources to support them in this phase of their lives.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App could include confusing navigation, difficulty finding specific information or services, technical glitches or errors, and challenges with completing tasks or transactions within the app. It is important for the app to address these issues to ensure a smooth user experience for veterans seeking assistance and support.

<br>

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that lead to 45% of veterans abandoning mobile app tasks, and uncover the root causes behind the current low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing with veterans to pinpoint specific pain points in the app's navigation and information architecture.
- Interview a sample of veterans to gather qualitative feedback on their experiences with the app's navigation and information structure.
- Analyze the navigation structure of the VA Mobile App in comparison to other similar apps to identify areas for improvement.
- Evaluate the search functionality and task completion flows within the app to determine where users are getting stuck or frustrated.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, interviews, and competitive analysis are appropriate for this research as they will provide valuable insights into the user experience of the VA Mobile App, gather user feedback, and assess how the app compares to competitors in the market.

**Implementation Approach:**
- For usability testing, sessions will be conducted in person or remotely, lasting approximately 1 hour each. Key activities will include task-based testing, thinking aloud protocols, and post-test interviews. Data will be collected through screen recordings, observation notes, and post-test questionnaires.
- Interviews will be conducted with VA Mobile App users to gather feedback on their experiences, preferences, and pain points. Interviews will be semi-structured and last approximately 30-45 minutes each. Data will be collected through audio recordings and notes.
- Competitive analysis will involve evaluating the features, design, and usability of similar apps in the market. Data will be collected through app usage, feature comparison charts, and user reviews.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be followed by involving stakeholders from the VA Mobile App team in the research process and sharing findings with them regularly.
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be taken into account by ensuring a safe and respectful environment for participants, providing informed consent, and offering support resources if needed.
- Accessibility accommodations will be made by ensuring that all research materials and sessions are accessible to participants with disabilities, including providing alternative formats and assistive technologies as needed.

## 🧲 Recruitment

### Target Participants
- **Primary:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app

- Accessibility needs: 2 participants with visual impairments, 2 participants with mobility impairments, 2 participants with hearing impairments, 2 participants with cognitive impairments

- Technology comfort levels: 4 participants who are comfortable using technology, 2 participants who are somewhat comfortable, 2 participants who are not very comfortable
