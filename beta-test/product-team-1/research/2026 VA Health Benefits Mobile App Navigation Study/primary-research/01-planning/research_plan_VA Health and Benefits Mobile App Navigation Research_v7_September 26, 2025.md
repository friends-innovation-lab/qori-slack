## 🔎 Background

This research will guide the product team in determining which navigation improvements to prioritize for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026, including main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes. By improving the app's usability and task completion flows, this will ultimately benefit Veterans by making it easier for them to access health and benefits information. From a business perspective, these enhancements can lead to increased user engagement, satisfaction, and ultimately, better outcomes for Veterans utilizing the app.

<br>

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in helping veterans navigate and access their health and benefits information, connecting them to resources and support throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** The VA Mobile App allows veterans to easily access their health records, schedule appointments, and communicate with their healthcare providers, supporting them in taking care of their physical and mental well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** Veterans can use the app to explore housing and education benefits, as well as connect with resources for employment and financial assistance as they transition to civilian life and establish roots in their communities.
- **Retiring:** The app provides veterans with information on retirement benefits, including pension and disability compensation, helping them navigate the process of transitioning to retirement and accessing the benefits they have earned.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App may include complex navigation, difficulty finding specific information or services, and challenges with the search functionality when trying to locate specific resources or benefits. Improving the user experience and streamlining access to key features could help alleviate these pain points for veterans using the app.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that lead to 45% of veterans abandoning tasks on the mobile app, and determine the underlying reasons for the low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to pinpoint specific pain points in the navigation and information architecture of the app that contribute to user frustration and task abandonment.
- Analyze the competitive landscape to identify best practices in mobile app navigation and information architecture that could be applied to improve the VA Mobile App.
- Conduct card sorting exercises with veterans to understand their mental models and preferences for organizing information within the app.
- Evaluate user feedback and data analytics to identify trends and patterns that may shed light on the root causes of the low user satisfaction rating.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, competitive analysis, and card sorting are appropriate for this research as they provide valuable insights into the user experience, identify strengths and weaknesses in comparison to competitors, and help organize information in a way that is intuitive for users.

**Implementation Approach:**
- For usability testing, we will conduct one-on-one sessions with participants, each lasting approximately 1 hour. We will observe participants as they interact with the VA Mobile App, collect feedback on the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows.
- For competitive analysis, we will compare the VA Mobile App with similar apps in the market, focusing on features, design, and user experience.
- For card sorting, we will ask participants to organize content and features of the VA Mobile App into categories that make sense to them, helping us understand how users expect information to be structured.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- We will ensure compliance with VA Collaboration Cycle requirements by involving stakeholders in the research process and sharing findings with them regularly.
- We will take trauma-informed research considerations into account by creating a safe and supportive environment for participants, being mindful of potential triggers, and offering resources for support if needed.
- We will make accessibility accommodations for participants with disabilities, ensuring that all research materials and sessions are accessible to everyone.

## 🧲 Recruitment

### Target Participants
- **Primary:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs

Mobile Usability Testing: 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

### Demographics
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era: 4 participants who are currently serving in the military or veterans, 4 participants who have family members who are serving or have served
- Age: 4 participants aged 25-35, 4 participants aged 45-55
- Gender: 4 male participants, 4 female participants
- Accessibility needs: 2 participants with visual impairments, 2 participants with hearing impairments, 2 participants with mobility impairments, 2 participants with cognitive impairments

**Secondary targets:**
- VA Mobile App: 4 participants who frequently use health features, 4 participants who frequently use benefits features
- Technology comfort levels: 4 participants who are comfortable using technology, 4 participants who are less comfortable using technology
