## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions regarding the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features in the VA Mobile App. These decisions will directly improve user experience for Veterans, ensuring easier access to vital resources. By prioritizing these enhancements, the VA can increase user satisfaction and streamline access to critical services.

---

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Expand the flagship mobile app

**Explanation:** This priority directly aligns with improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features for veterans who actively use the VA mobile app.

**Additional Priorities:**
- **Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims:** Streamlining task completion flows for veterans who struggle to complete tasks in the mobile app
- **Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience:** Improving the overall user experience for veterans using the VA mobile app

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features are crucial for assisting Veterans in managing their healthcare and benefits efficiently.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Accessing health records and scheduling appointments
- **Putting down roots:** Exploring housing benefits and educational resources
- **Retiring:** Managing retirement benefits and accessing mental health support

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Veterans may struggle with finding specific health information quickly due to a complex navigation structure within the app.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing high abandonment rates and low user satisfaction in the VA Mobile App.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most intuitive main navigation structure through cardsorting exercises.
- Improve search functionality based on usability testing feedback.
- Identify competitive strengths and weaknesses in task completion flows for health and benefits features.

---

**Success Criteria:**
1. Decision Support: By the end of the research period, provide actionable recommendations for improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows of the VA Mobile App. These recommendations should be implemented within 3 months of the research completion to address the primary barriers identified by veterans and improve user satisfaction rating to at least 7/10.

2. Quality Threshold: Ensure that the research findings are comprehensive and actionable, with clear insights into the root causes of user dissatisfaction and abandonment. All recommendations should be supported by data and user feedback, and the research report should be approved by key stakeholders before implementation.

Aim to increase user satisfaction rating to 8/10 and reduce task abandonment by at least 25% compared to pre-implementation levels. Regular user feedback and analytics monitoring should be conducted to track progress and make further improvements as needed.

**Primary Deliverables:**

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting, usability testing, and competitive analysis are chosen for their ability to directly uncover navigation and information architecture barriers, root causes of low user satisfaction, and competitive insights in a practical and actionable manner.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Hybrid sessions for cardsorting to gather insights on navigation preferences
- Task-based usability testing on personal devices to evaluate task completion flows
- Competitive analysis to benchmark against industry standards and identify best practices
- Specific protocols tailored to VA Mobile App features, ensuring a focus on health and benefits functionalities

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements to be met within specified timeline
- Trauma-informed research considerations integrated into participant interactions
- Accessibility accommodations provided for participants with visual/motor accessibility needs, ensuring inclusivity and diverse representation in the study.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs  

**🧍 Participant Count:** Card Sorting: 8 participants (45-minute hybrid sessions)
Mobile Usability Testing: 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

**Secondary targets:**

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty finding qualified participants who meet the inclusion criteria and accessibility focus
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA medical centers and veteran organizations to reach out to potential participants, offer incentives for participation, and provide flexibility in scheduling sessions to accommodate participants' needs.

**Execution issues:** Potential difficulties in implementing the cardsorting, usability, and competitive research methodologies effectively
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct pilot tests with a small group of participants to identify and address any issues with the methodologies, provide training to research team members on the proper execution of each methodology, and have a backup plan in case of technical difficulties.

**Timeline risks:** Delays in the research process that impact the standard timeline
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones and deadlines, regularly monitor progress against the plan, identify potential bottlenecks early on, and allocate additional resources if needed to stay on track.

**Quality concerns:** Risks related to data validity and stakeholder satisfaction
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation checks, ensure that research findings are communicated clearly and effectively to stakeholders, and solicit feedback throughout the research process to address any concerns promptly.

**VA-specific risks:** Challenges related to Collaboration Cycle delays and compliance issues
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Maintain open communication with VA stakeholders, adhere to all relevant VA policies and regulations, and proactively address any compliance issues that may arise during the research project.
