## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features in the VA Mobile App redesign scheduled for Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements such as main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes, the app will become more user-friendly and efficient for Veterans accessing important health and benefits information. This strategic approach will not only enhance the overall user experience but also increase user engagement and satisfaction, ultimately leading to improved outcomes for Veterans and a more successful mobile app for the VA.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing easy access to health and benefits information and services, helping veterans take care of themselves, put down roots, and transition into retirement.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the VA Mobile App to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, and access resources for mental health and wellness.
- **Putting down roots:** The app helps veterans navigate housing and education benefits, find employment opportunities, and connect with community resources.
- **Retiring:** Veterans can use the app to access retirement benefits, financial planning tools, and information on long-term care options.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some pain points for the VA Mobile App may include difficulties in navigating the main menu, locating specific health and benefits features, and completing tasks such as submitting claims or updating personal information. The search functionality may also need improvement to help veterans quickly find the information they need.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Conduct user testing and analysis to identify the primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing 45% of veterans to abandon mobile app tasks, with the aim of reducing this abandonment rate by 20% within the next 6 months.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct a cardsorting exercise with veterans to understand their mental models and preferences for organizing content within the app, with the goal of optimizing the main navigation structure and information architecture to improve user satisfaction and task completion rates.
- Conduct usability testing sessions with veterans to identify pain points in the current search functionality and task completion flows within the app, with the goal of implementing improvements that address these issues and increase user satisfaction ratings to 7/10 within the next quarter.
- Conduct a competitive analysis of other mobile apps in the healthcare and benefits space to identify best practices and innovative features that could be incorporated into the VA Mobile App to enhance user experience and overall satisfaction.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 

Cardsorting is a valuable method for understanding how users categorize and prioritize information, which is crucial for designing an effective main navigation structure and information architecture. Usability testing allows us to observe how users interact with the app and identify pain points in task completion flows. Competitive research helps us benchmark the VA Mobile App against other similar apps in terms of search functionality and user experience.

**Implementation Approach:**

- Session format and duration: Conduct remote cardsorting sessions with veterans and stakeholders to gather insights on how they would organize the app's content. Usability testing sessions should be conducted in-person or remotely, with tasks related to health and benefits features. Competitive research can be done through a combination of expert reviews and user testing of competitor apps.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Use online cardsorting tools to gather data on how users group and label content. Usability testing can involve task scenarios related to health and benefits features, with observations and surveys for feedback. Competitive research involves analyzing features, navigation, and user feedback from competitor apps.
- Specific protocols: Develop a standardized script for cardsorting sessions, usability testing, and competitive research to ensure consistency in data collection. Use a mix of qualitative and quantitative data analysis techniques to identify patterns and insights related to the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows.

**VA Compliance Notes:**

- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensure that all research activities align with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and adhere to the specified timeline for data collection and analysis.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: Prioritize the well-being and comfort of veteran participants by creating a safe and supportive environment for research activities. Be mindful of potential triggers and provide resources for support if needed.
- Accessibility accommodations: Provide accommodations such as screen readers, captioning, and alternative formats for participants with diverse needs to ensure inclusivity in the research process.

By combining cardsorting, usability testing, and competitive research, we can gather comprehensive insights into the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows. This methodology allows us to understand user preferences, pain points, and industry best practices to inform design decisions and enhance the overall user experience for veterans accessing health and benefits features.
