## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements, restructuring the main menu, enhancing search capabilities, and improving information hierarchy, the app will be more user-friendly and efficient for Veterans accessing health and benefits features. This will ultimately lead to a better user experience, increased engagement, and improved access to vital services for Veterans, resulting in higher satisfaction rates and potentially increased usage of the VA Mobile App.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in helping veterans navigate and access their health and benefits resources efficiently and effectively throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, access telehealth services, and track their health progress.
- **Putting down roots:** The app provides resources for veterans looking to purchase a home, find employment opportunities, or access educational benefits.
- **Retiring:** Veterans can use the app to manage their retirement benefits, access financial planning tools, and connect with support services for transitioning into civilian life.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some pain points for the VA Mobile App may include complicated navigation, difficulty finding specific information or services, and potential technical issues that hinder task completion.

<br>

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** To identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that lead to 45% of veterans abandoning mobile app tasks, and to understand the root causes behind the current 4.2/10 user satisfaction rating.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing with veterans to pinpoint specific pain points in navigation and information architecture.
- Interview a sample of veterans to gather qualitative feedback on their experiences with the VA Mobile App.
- Analyze user data to identify patterns in task completion rates and user satisfaction levels across different features of the app.
- Conduct a competitive analysis to understand best practices in mobile app design for veterans and identify areas for improvement in the VA Mobile App.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability interviews and concept testing are appropriate for this research as they allow for direct feedback from users on the VA Mobile App's design and functionality, helping to identify areas for improvement and refinement.

**Implementation Approach:**
- The usability interviews will be conducted in one-on-one sessions lasting approximately 30-60 minutes each.
- Key activities will include task-based scenarios to test navigation, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features.
- Data will be collected through observation, screen recordings, and user feedback during the sessions.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met by involving key stakeholders from the VA Mobile App development team in the usability interviews and concept testing process.
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be taken into account by creating a safe and comfortable environment for participants and being mindful of potential triggers.
- Accessibility accommodations will be made to ensure that all participants, including those with disabilities, can fully participate in the usability interviews and concept testing.

## 🧲 Recruitment

### Target Participants
Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs

### Sample Size
**Total Participants:** Card Sorting: 8 participants (45-minute hybrid sessions)
Mobile Usability Testing: 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

### Demographics
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era: 4 participants from the post-9/11 era, 2 participants from the Gulf War era, 2 participants from the Vietnam era
- Age: 4 participants aged 25-40, 2 participants aged 41-55, 2 participants aged 56+
- Gender: 4 male participants, 4 female participants
- Accessibility needs: 2 participants with visual impairments, 2 participants with hearing impairments, 2 participants with mobility impairments, 2 participants with cognitive impairments

**Secondary targets:**
- VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features usage patterns
- Technology comfort levels: 4 participants who are comfortable using technology, 4 participants who are less comfortable with technology
