📦 **Project Area:** **Digital Services**

## 🎯 Business Context

The Department of Veterans Affairs mobile application currently faces significant usability challenges, with user satisfaction ratings at 4.2 out of 10 and a concerning 45% task abandonment rate among veterans attempting to complete essential services. Analytics data reveals that users frequently resort to the search function to locate fundamental features, suggesting critical deficiencies in the application's information architecture and navigation design. These usability barriers prevent veterans from efficiently accessing vital healthcare, benefits, and support services through the mobile platform, highlighting an urgent need for user experience research and design improvements.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> Based on the barriers and study context, here are the key stakeholder concerns driving this VA research:

- **Poor navigation structure and information architecture** is causing 45% of veterans to abandon critical health and benefits tasks mid-completion, preventing them from accessing essential VA services they've earned

- **Unintuitive feature placement and labeling** is forcing veterans to rely heavily on search functionality to locate basic app functions, creating frustration and inefficient user journeys that undermine the app's purpose as a streamlined access point

- **Low user satisfaction (4.2/10 rating) and high task abandonment rates** are indicating that the VA mobile app is failing to deliver on its mission to provide accessible digital services to veterans, potentially driving them back to more resource-intensive channels like phone calls and in-person visits

## 🎯 Research Objectives

**What we want to learn:**

• **Where veterans** encounter the most significant navigation barriers during critical tasks like health records access, appointment scheduling, and benefits management that lead to the 45% abandonment rate.

• **Which** information architecture and navigation elements are causing veterans to rely heavily on search functionality instead of intuitive menu navigation to complete basic tasks.

• **Why** veterans are experiencing such low satisfaction (4.2/10) with the current app structure, particularly during onboarding and initial feature discovery phases.

• **How** veterans currently navigate through multi-step processes for prescription management, document submission, and appointment modifications before abandoning these tasks.

# Veteran Healthcare App User Flows

## 1. App Onboarding and Initial Feature Discovery
1. Download and install the VA mobile app from app store
2. Create account using DS Logon, ID.me, or Login.gov credentials
3. Verify identity through two-factor authentication
4. Complete initial profile setup with personal information
5. Grant necessary permissions for notifications and location services
6. Take guided tour of main features and navigation
7. Set up biometric login (fingerprint/face ID) for future access

## 2. Main Dashboard Navigation and Task Completion
1. Log into app and view personalized dashboard
2. Review urgent notifications and action items at top of screen
3. Navigate between main sections using bottom navigation bar
4. Access quick actions through dashboard shortcuts
5. Complete priority tasks (appointments, messages, prescriptions)
6. Mark tasks as complete or dismiss notifications
7. Return to dashboard to view updated status

## 3. Health Records Access and Prescription Management
1. Navigate to Health Records section from main menu
2. Select specific record type (lab results, visit notes, imaging)
3. Filter records by date range or healthcare provider
4. View detailed record information and download/share as needed
5. Access Prescription Management from health section
6. Review current prescriptions and refill status
7. Request prescription refills for eligible medications
8. Track refill status and delivery information

## 4. Appointment Scheduling and Modification
1. Navigate to Appointments section from main menu
2. View upcoming appointments in calendar or list format
3. Select "Schedule New Appointment" option
4. Choose appointment type and preferred VA facility
5. Select from available time slots
6. Confirm appointment details and receive confirmation
7. Modify existing appointments by selecting appointment and choosing "Reschedule"
8. Cancel appointments if needed with cancellation reason

## 5. Benefits Status Checking and Document Submission
1. Navigate to Benefits section from main menu
2. View current benefit claims and their status
3. Select specific claim to view detailed progress information
4. Access "Submit Documents" option for active claims
5. Take photos or upload required documents from device
6. Add document descriptions and submit to appropriate claim
7. Receive confirmation of successful document submission
8. Track document processing status in claim details

## 🎯 Target Barriers

**Target Barriers to Investigate:**

- **Navigation hierarchy confusion**: Identify structural issues in the information architecture that prevent users from locating key features and services within the mobile application

- **Task flow interruptions**: Analyze critical user journeys to pinpoint where navigation breakdowns occur, leading to the 45% task abandonment rate

- **Cognitive load from complex menu structures**: Evaluate whether current navigation patterns exceed users' mental processing capacity and create decision paralysis

- **Inconsistent interaction patterns**: Assess variations in UI elements, gestures, and navigation conventions that may confuse users across different app sections

- **Poor content discoverability**: Examine how findability issues with essential veteran services and information contribute to user frustration

- **Mobile-specific usability barriers**: Identify touch target sizing, screen real estate utilization, and responsive design issues that hinder mobile task completion

- **Accessibility and inclusive design gaps**: Evaluate navigation barriers that disproportionately impact veterans with disabilities or varying levels of digital literacy

- **Unclear visual hierarchy and wayfinding**: Analyze how inadequate visual cues and orientation elements contribute to user disorientation within the app

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. **What barriers prevent veterans from successfully completing critical tasks in the mobile app, and which specific navigation or information architecture issues are the primary drivers of the 45% task abandonment rate?**

2. **How do veterans currently navigate through the app's core user flows (onboarding, dashboard navigation, health records access, appointment scheduling, and benefits checking), and where do they encounter the most friction or confusion?**

3. **Why do veterans rate their overall app experience at 4.2/10, and what specific pain points in the information architecture, content organization, or navigation patterns contribute most significantly to this low satisfaction score?**

4. **What causes veterans to struggle during the onboarding process and initial feature discovery, and how do these early-experience barriers impact their ability to successfully use key functions like prescription management, appointment scheduling, and document submission?**

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
Here are 2-3 brief team hypotheses about the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App:

- "Complex navigation structure with too many menu levels increases task abandonment rates among veterans trying to access health records and benefits information"

- "Veterans over 65 face more difficulty with the current touch interface design and small text sizing when completing prescription refill and appointment scheduling tasks"

- "The current mixed information architecture that combines health and benefits features in unclear categories leads to veterans getting lost and unable to complete their intended tasks"
