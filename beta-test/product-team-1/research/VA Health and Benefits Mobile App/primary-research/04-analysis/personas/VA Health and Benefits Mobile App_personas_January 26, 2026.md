## Persona 1: The Accessibility-First Navigator

**Based on:** PT001, PT003

> "I shouldn't have to guess what buttons do. That's basic accessibility." — PT001

**Who they are:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Background** | Veterans who rely on assistive technology for navigation |
| **Tech Setup** | Mobile device with screen reader, requires proper labeling |
| **VA Usage** | Regular users who need accessible design to complete tasks |

**What they're trying to do:**

- Schedule healthcare appointments independently — *Evidence: PT001*
- Navigate messaging systems to contact healthcare providers — *Evidence: PT003*
- Access basic VA services without assistance — *Evidence: PT001, PT003*

**What blocks them:**

| Frustration | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| Unlabeled interactive elements that just say "Button" | PT001 |
| Important functions hidden at bottom of screens | PT001 |
| Slow loading times that interrupt screen reader flow | PT003 |

**How they cope:** When accessibility fails, they resort to calling VA staff directly rather than struggling with unlabeled interface elements.

**Design implication:** Implement comprehensive button labeling standards and position primary actions prominently on screens.

## Persona 2: The Efficiency-Driven Veteran

**Based on:** PT002, PT003

> "By the time this app loads what I need, I could have talked to a person and gotten my answer." — PT003

**Who they are:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Background** | Veterans who regularly use VA services and expect efficiency |
| **Tech Setup** | Mobile-first users who benchmark app performance against phone calls |
| **VA Usage** | Frequent users for prescription refills, messaging, and routine tasks |

**What they're trying to do:**

- Refill prescriptions quickly and reliably — *Evidence: PT002*
- Send simple messages to healthcare providers — *Evidence: PT002*
- Complete routine tasks faster than calling — *Evidence: PT003*

**What blocks them:**

| Frustration | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| Excessive loading times throughout the app | PT003 |
| Complex navigation requiring multiple steps for simple tasks | PT002 |
| Limited functionality for specific tracking needs | PT003 |

**How they cope:** They use the app for basic tasks like prescription refills that work well, but switch to phone calls for complex needs or when the app is too slow.

**Design implication:** Optimize app performance and create direct pathways for common tasks like messaging healthcare providers.

## Methodology

**Framework:** Evidence-Based Persona Development

**Approach:** Composite archetypes from behavioral patterns across participants — not 1:1 mapping.

**Data Sources:** PT001, PT002, PT003

**Why these groupings:** PT001 and PT003 both struggled with interface clarity and accessibility (unlabeled elements, loading issues), while PT002 and PT003 both expressed frustration with app efficiency compared to phone alternatives. Both personas value clear design when it works well.
