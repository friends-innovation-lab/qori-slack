## 🔴 Top Pain Points

**Critical - Unlabeled Interactive Elements**
The participant encountered buttons with no descriptive text, including one that literally just said "Button," creating significant accessibility barriers and forcing them to guess functionality.

**High - Hidden Critical Actions**
Important functionality like scheduling appointments was difficult to locate, with key buttons positioned at the bottom of screens where they were easily missed during navigation.

**Medium - Inconsistent Button Labeling**
The participant experienced frustration with buttons that lacked clear descriptive text, making it impossible to predict what actions they would perform.

## 🟢 What Worked Well

**Logical Main Menu Structure**
The participant found the main navigation intuitive, with Health, Benefits, Payments, and Profile sections making logical sense for organizing content.

**Clear Section Organization**
When elements were properly labeled, the participant could easily understand what each section contained and navigate effectively through the interface.

## 🟡 Key Opportunities

**High Impact - Implement Comprehensive Button Labeling Standards**
Address the critical accessibility issue where buttons lack descriptive text, ensuring all interactive elements clearly communicate their function to users relying on screen readers.

**High Impact - Relocate Primary Actions to Prominent Positions**
Move important functions like appointment scheduling to more discoverable locations rather than hiding them at the bottom of screens where users struggle to find them.

**Medium Impact - Establish Consistent Labeling Patterns**
Build on the successful main menu structure by applying the same clear, descriptive labeling approach throughout the entire application interface.

## 💡 Key Insights

**Mental Model - Logical Information Architecture Expectations**
The participant's positive response to the main menu structure reveals they expect VA services to be organized by benefit type, suggesting this mental model should be reinforced throughout the app.

**Accessibility Need - Screen Reader Dependency on Descriptive Labels**
The participant's reliance on proper labeling indicates they use assistive technology and require descriptive text to understand interface functionality, highlighting the critical importance of accessibility compliance.

**Navigation Strategy - Sequential Screen Reading Pattern**
The participant's difficulty finding buttons at the bottom of screens suggests they navigate by reading through content sequentially, missing elements placed in less prominent positions.

**Success Pattern - Clear Labeling Enables Confident Navigation**
When sections were properly labeled, the participant expressed confidence and ease, demonstrating how descriptive text directly impacts user success and satisfaction.

## ✅ Recommended Actions

- **Critical:** Audit and fix all unlabeled buttons and interactive elements to include descriptive text *(addresses: Unlabeled Interactive Elements)*

- **High Impact:** Relocate primary action buttons like appointment scheduling to prominent, easily discoverable positions *(addresses: Hidden Critical Actions)*

- **High Impact:** Establish and implement comprehensive accessibility labeling standards across the application *(addresses: Inconsistent Button Labeling)*

- **Quick Win:** Apply the successful main menu labeling pattern to other navigation elements throughout the app *(addresses: Clear Section Organization success)*
