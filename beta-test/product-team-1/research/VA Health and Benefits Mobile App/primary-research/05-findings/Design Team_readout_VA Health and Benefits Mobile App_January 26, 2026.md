# DESIGN TEAM READOUT
## VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research

**Date:** January 26, 2026 | **Participants:** 3 | **Lead:** Lapedra Tolson

---

### TL;DR FOR DESIGN TEAM

- **Critical accessibility failures** blocking users with assistive technology from completing basic tasks
- **Navigation structure works well** but key actions are hidden in poor locations
- **Performance issues** are driving users away from the app entirely
- **Search functionality** needs major improvements to serve as backup navigation

---

### FINDINGS RELEVANT TO DESIGN TEAM

**Critical Accessibility Barriers**

> "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text. That's not helpful at all."
> *— PT001*

- **The Problem:** Interactive elements lack proper labeling, creating barriers for screen reader users
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants (likely affects all assistive technology users)
- **Suggested Fix:** Audit all buttons and interactive elements to ensure descriptive labels; implement accessibility labeling standards

---

**Hidden Primary Actions**

> "I'm hearing a lot of different elements but nothing that clearly says 'Schedule new appointment.'"
> *— PT001*

- **The Problem:** Important functions like appointment scheduling are positioned at bottom of screens where users miss them
- **Severity:** High
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants explicitly, likely broader issue
- **Suggested Fix:** Relocate primary action buttons to prominent, easily discoverable positions above the fold

---

**Navigation Structure Success**

> "The main menu structure makes sense - Health, Benefits, Payments, Profile. That's logical."
> *— PT001*

- **The Problem:** This is actually working well - preserve this pattern
- **Severity:** Positive finding
- **Affected:** Multiple participants praised this approach
- **Suggested Fix:** Apply this successful categorization pattern throughout the app consistently

---

**Search Functionality Gaps**

> "The search is hard to find and the results aren't great. I'd probably give up and call someone instead."
> *— PT002*

- **The Problem:** Search is poorly positioned and returns inadequate results, failing as backup navigation
- **Severity:** High
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants, critical for task recovery
- **Suggested Fix:** Make search more prominent and improve result relevance; consider search as primary navigation aid

---

**Performance Impact on User Experience**

> "By the time this app loads what I need, I could have talked to a person and gotten my answer."
> *— PT003*

- **The Problem:** Loading times are driving users to abandon digital channels entirely
- **Severity:** High
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants, impacts overall app adoption
- **Suggested Fix:** Coordinate with engineering on performance optimization; consider loading states and progressive disclosure

---

### IMPLEMENTATION CHECKLIST

- [ ] **Accessibility audit** - Review all interactive elements for proper labeling (Critical - Week 1)
- [ ] **Button placement review** - Map primary actions and move to prominent positions (High - Week 2)
- [ ] **Search redesign** - Improve discoverability and result quality (High - Week 3)
- [ ] **Preserve successful patterns** - Document and standardize main menu approach (Quick win - Week 1)
- [ ] **Performance collaboration** - Work with engineering on loading optimization (High - Ongoing)

---

### QUESTIONS FOR DESIGN TEAM

- How can we systematically apply the successful main menu categorization pattern to sub-navigation?
- What's the best approach for making search more discoverable without cluttering the interface?
- Should we consider progressive disclosure to improve perceived performance while technical issues are addressed?
