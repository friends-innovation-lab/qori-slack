# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
## VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research

**Date:** January 26, 2026 | **Lead:** Lapedra Tolson

---

### KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Critical Accessibility Barriers** — Veterans encounter unlabeled buttons and hidden functions that prevent task completion, requiring immediate accessibility audit and remediation
2. **Navigation Complexity Drives Abandonment** — Veterans abandon tasks due to "maze-like" navigation for simple actions like messaging doctors, needing streamlined user flows  
3. **Performance Issues Undermine Digital Strategy** — Excessive loading times cause veterans to abandon the app for phone support, contradicting VA's digital-first objectives

---

### WHAT'S WORKING

- **Main Menu Structure** — Veterans find the Health, Benefits, Payments, Profile organization logical and intuitive
- **Prescription Refill Feature** — This functionality works smoothly with clear information display and obvious action buttons that veterans use regularly

---

### DECISIONS NEEDED

| Decision | Options | Recommendation | By When |
|----------|---------|----------------|---------|
| Accessibility Compliance | A) Comprehensive audit + fixes B) Gradual improvements | A) Immediate comprehensive audit | Week 1 |
| Navigation Redesign | A) Full restructure B) Targeted fixes to problem areas | B) Target messaging and search first | Week 2 |

### RESOURCE IMPLICATIONS

- **Timeline:** Navigation improvements can be implemented in 3 phases over 4 months without disrupting current roadmap
- **Staffing:** Requires dedicated accessibility specialist and UX designer for 8-12 weeks
- **Budget:** Performance optimization and accessibility fixes can be accomplished within existing development resources

---

**Supporting Evidence:**
- 3 veterans interviewed with consistent patterns across accessibility needs, navigation complexity, and performance expectations
- Direct quotes document specific pain points: "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text" (PT001)
