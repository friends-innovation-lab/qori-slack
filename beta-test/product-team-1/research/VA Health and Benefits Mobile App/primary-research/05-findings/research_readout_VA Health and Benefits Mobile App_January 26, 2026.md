## 🔍 Key Findings

### Finding 1: Critical Accessibility Barriers Block Task Completion

**What we observed:**
Participants encountered buttons with no descriptive text, including one that literally just said "Button," creating significant accessibility barriers and forcing users to guess functionality.

**Evidence:**
> "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text. That's not helpful at all."
> *— PT001*

> "I shouldn't have to guess what buttons do. That's basic accessibility."
> *— PT001*

**Frequency:** 1 of 3 participants
**Severity:** Critical

**Recommendation:**
Immediately audit and fix all unlabeled buttons and interactive elements to include descriptive text, ensuring compliance with accessibility standards.

---

### Finding 2: Search Functionality Fails as Navigation Backup

**What we observed:**
When primary navigation fails, participants turn to search as a backup strategy, but the search feature is difficult to locate and produces inadequate results, leading to task abandonment.

**Evidence:**
> "The search is hard to find and the results aren't great. I'd probably give up and call someone instead."
> *— PT002*

**Frequency:** 1 of 3 participants
**Severity:** High

**Recommendation:**
Improve search visibility and result quality to serve as effective backup navigation when primary pathways fail.

---

### Finding 3: Complex Messaging Workflows Create Friction

**What we observed:**
Participants experience significant friction when trying to contact healthcare providers, having to navigate through multiple categories before reaching their goal.

**Evidence:**
> "I just want to ask my doctor a simple question, but I have to navigate through all these categories first."
> *— PT002*

> "Sometimes I feel like I'm going through a maze just to do simple things."
> *— PT002*

**Frequency:** 1 of 3 participants
**Severity:** High

**Recommendation:**
Create a more direct path for users to contact healthcare providers without requiring navigation through multiple category layers.

## 🔬 Methodology

- **Type:** 60-minute remote video interviews
- **Duration:** Individual sessions with screen sharing
- **Focus:** Main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality
- **Recruitment:** Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month and those who have struggled to complete tasks
