# PRODUCT TEAM READOUT
## VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research

**Date:** January 26, 2026 | **Participants:** 3 | **Lead:** Lapedra Tolson

---

### TL;DR FOR PRODUCT TEAM

- **Critical accessibility violations** are blocking veterans from completing basic tasks (unlabeled buttons, hidden actions)
- **Navigation structure works well** but search functionality is failing as backup option
- **Performance issues** are driving veterans to abandon app and call support instead
- **Immediate fixes needed** before broader navigation redesign to prevent further user abandonment

---

### FINDINGS RELEVANT TO PRODUCT TEAM

**Critical Accessibility Barriers**

> "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text. That's not helpful at all."
> *— PT001*

- **The Problem:** Interactive elements lack descriptive labels, critical functions hidden at bottom of screens
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants (likely affects all screen reader users)
- **Suggested Fix:** Audit all buttons/interactive elements for proper labeling, relocate primary actions to prominent positions

---

**Search Functionality Inadequate**

> "The search is hard to find and the results aren't great. I'd probably give up and call someone instead."
> *— PT002*

- **The Problem:** Search is difficult to locate and returns poor results when navigation fails
- **Severity:** High
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants explicitly mentioned, likely broader issue
- **Suggested Fix:** Improve search visibility and result relevance algorithms

---

**Performance Issues Driving Channel Switching**

> "By the time this app loads what I need, I could have talked to a person and gotten my answer."
> *— PT003*

- **The Problem:** Excessive loading times throughout app causing task abandonment
- **Severity:** High
- **Affected:** 1 of 3 participants, described as "typical for this app"
- **Suggested Fix:** Performance audit and optimization across all features

---

**Navigation Structure Success Pattern**

> "The main menu structure makes sense - Health, Benefits, Payments, Profile. That's logical."
> *— PT001*

- **The Problem:** This is actually working well - preserve this structure
- **Severity:** Success to maintain
- **Affected:** Multiple participants praised main navigation
- **Suggested Fix:** Apply this successful pattern consistently throughout app

---

### IMPLEMENTATION CHECKLIST

- [ ] **Immediate:** Audit all buttons and interactive elements for accessibility compliance
- [ ] **Immediate:** Relocate critical actions (appointment scheduling) to prominent positions  
- [ ] **Week 1:** Conduct performance audit focusing on loading speeds
- [ ] **Week 2:** Improve search discoverability and result algorithms
- [ ] **Ongoing:** Document and protect successful navigation patterns from future changes
- [ ] **Before redesign:** Ensure prescription refill workflow (identified as highly successful) remains unchanged

---

### QUESTIONS FOR PRODUCT TEAM

- Should we delay broader navigation redesign until critical accessibility issues are resolved?
- What's the current performance baseline for loading times, and what's an acceptable target?
- How can we better leverage the successful prescription refill workflow patterns in other areas?
