## 📋 BACKGROUND

**Why this research was conducted:** To identify navigation and usability issues preventing veterans from successfully completing tasks in the VA's primary mobile application for healthcare and benefits access.

| Study Details | |
|:--------------|:--|
| **Methodology** | User interviews and usability testing |
| **Participants** | 3 veterans with diverse backgrounds and accessibility needs |
| **Focus Area** | Mobile app navigation, search functionality, and information architecture |

---

## 🔍 KEY FINDINGS

### Finding 1: Critical Accessibility Violations Block Core Functions

- **Veteran Impact:** Veterans using screen readers cannot complete basic tasks due to unlabeled buttons
- **Evidence:** "There's a button that just says 'Button' without any descriptive text. That's not helpful at all." — PT001
- **Status:** Requires immediate remediation

### Finding 2: Search Function Abandonment Forces Phone Support Reliance

- **Veteran Impact:** Veterans abandon digital tasks and call support centers when search fails
- **Evidence:** "The search is hard to find and the results aren't great. I'd probably give up and call someone instead." — PT002
- **Status:** Redesign needed for discoverability and relevance

### Finding 3: Performance Issues Exceed Rural User Tolerance

- **Veteran Impact:** Extended loading times (45+ seconds) make app unusable even for veterans adapted to slower connections
- **Evidence:** "By the time this app loads what I need, I could have talked to a person and gotten my answer." — PT003
- **Status:** Performance optimization required
