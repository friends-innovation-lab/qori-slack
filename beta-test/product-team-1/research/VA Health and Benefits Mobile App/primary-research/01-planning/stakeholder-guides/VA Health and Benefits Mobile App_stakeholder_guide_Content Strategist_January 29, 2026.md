## Interview Guide

### Opening (3-5 min)

> *Build rapport and set context*

- Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching the VA mobile app experience and wanted to understand content strategy and constraints better.
- Everything you share helps us design better solutions. No wrong answers.
- Okay to record? [If applicable]

**Opener:** "To start, can you tell me about your role and how you approach content for the mobile app?"

---

### Section 1: Content Constraints & Approval (12 min)

> *Understand what limits content decisions and changes*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | What are the biggest constraints you face when creating or updating mobile app content? | • Are these technical, legal, or resource constraints? • How do these differ from web content constraints? |
| 2 | Walk me through the approval process for new content or content changes. | • Who needs to sign off? • How long does this typically take? • Where do delays usually happen? |
| 3 | What policy or legal requirements shape how you write content for veterans? | • How do you balance compliance with user-friendly language? • What happens when legal and UX needs conflict? |
| 4 | How do technical limitations affect what content you can include or how you present it? | • Are there character limits or formatting restrictions? • What mobile-specific constraints do you work within? |

**Listen for:** Bottlenecks in approval workflows, competing priorities between compliance and usability, technical debt affecting content

---

### Section 2: Content Strategy & Processes (15 min)

> *Understand how content decisions are made and prioritized*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | How do you decide what content to prioritize for updates or creation? | • What data or feedback informs these decisions? • How do you balance different stakeholder requests? |
| 2 | Describe your process for ensuring content consistency across the mobile app. | • How do you coordinate with other content creators? • What tools or systems help maintain consistency? |
| 3 | How do you approach writing for veterans with different levels of familiarity with VA benefits? | • What guidelines do you follow for plain language? • How do you handle technical terms that can't be simplified? |
| 4 | What's your process for updating content when policies or benefits change? | • How quickly can you make these updates? • What coordination is required with other teams? |

**Listen for:** Content governance processes, collaboration challenges, user empathy in content decisions

---

### Section 3: Connecting to User Research (10 min)

> *Share relevant user findings and get stakeholder perspective*

"In our user research, we heard several things about content. From your perspective..."

| Finding | Question |
|---------|----------|
| Users report being confused by eligibility language and not knowing if benefits apply to them | "What constraints make it difficult to write clearer eligibility content? How do you currently approach this challenge?" |
| Veterans say they need more context about next steps after reading benefit information | "What prevents us from including more actionable guidance? Are there policy or resource limitations?" |
| Users mention inconsistent tone - some sections feel formal while others are conversational | "What drives these differences in voice? How do you balance brand consistency with different content needs?" |

---

### Section 4: Strategic Priorities & Future Vision (10 min)

> *Understand roadmap and strategic thinking*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | What are your team's main content priorities for the next 6-12 months? | • How do these align with broader VA digital strategy? • What would success look like? |
| 2 | If you could change one thing about how content works in the mobile app, what would it be? | • What's preventing this change now? • What would need to happen to make it possible? |
| 3 | How do you envision content strategy evolving as the mobile app grows? | • What capabilities or resources would you need? • How might user expectations change? |

**Listen for:** Strategic vision, resource needs, barriers to improvement

---

### Closing (3-5 min)

- "What haven't I asked about content strategy or constraints that you think I should know?"
- "Who else should I talk to to better understand content processes - maybe someone from legal or policy?"
- "Can I follow up if I have more questions as our research progresses?"

**Thank them for their time.**
