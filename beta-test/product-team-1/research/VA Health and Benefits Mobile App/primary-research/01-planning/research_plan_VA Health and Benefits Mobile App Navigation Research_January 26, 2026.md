# Research Plan — VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research

📦 **Product Area:** VA Mobile App — Provides Veterans with streamlined access to their benefits, health records, and VA services through an intuitive mobile interface with integrated search and organized navigation by service category.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digital Services

🗓️ **Date:** January 26, 2026 · **Timeline:** standard

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the VA Mobile App product team's decisions on restructuring the main navigation menu, enhancing search functionality, and reorganizing information hierarchy for the Q1 2026 redesign. The findings will help create a more intuitive app experience that allows Veterans to quickly find and access the services they need, reducing frustration and time spent navigating the app. For VA, these navigation improvements will increase app adoption and usage while reducing support calls and improving overall Veteran satisfaction with digital services.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Expand the flagship mobile app

**Explanation:** Improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality directly supports expanding the flagship mobile app by making it more usable and accessible for both active users and those who have struggled with task completion.

**Additional Priorities:**
- **Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience:** Better navigation and search capabilities will help Veterans more easily find and access the specific services and information most relevant to their individual needs and circumstances.
- **Improve the Veteran login experience:** Since navigation and information architecture issues often compound login frustrations, addressing these structural elements will create a smoother overall user journey from authentication through task completion.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App's navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality serve as the primary digital gateway that veterans use throughout their entire journey to access, discover, and manage their VA benefits and services across all life phases.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** The app's health-focused navigation sections and search capabilities enable veterans to quickly find and access medical appointments, prescription refills, secure messaging with healthcare providers, and health records, while the intuitive information architecture groups related health services together for easier discovery and management of ongoing care needs.

- **Putting down roots:** The navigation structure supports veterans transitioning to civilian life by organizing education benefits, home loan information, career services, and disability compensation in logical categories, while the search function helps veterans quickly locate specific programs like GI Bill benefits, vocational rehabilitation, or housing assistance as they establish their post-military lives.

- **Retiring:** The app's information architecture accommodates aging veterans by providing clear pathways to pension benefits, long-term care options, survivor benefits, and Medicare coordination, with search functionality that helps locate age-specific services and the navigation structure that prioritizes the most commonly needed retirement-related benefits and healthcare services.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Veterans struggle with inconsistent categorization and labeling within the navigation structure, making it difficult to predict where specific benefits or services might be located
- The search functionality often returns irrelevant results or fails to surface the most appropriate services for a veteran's specific situation or eligibility status
- The information architecture doesn't effectively surface related or complementary benefits that veterans might be eligible for but unaware of, leading to underutilization of available services

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and prioritize the specific navigation pathways, information architecture flaws, and search functionality issues within the VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search that directly contribute to the 45% task abandonment rate and 4.2/10 satisfaction score among veterans. Document the top 5 usability barriers with clear severity rankings and user impact metrics to guide immediate redesign priorities.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct in-depth interviews with 15-20 veterans who have abandoned tasks in the VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search to map their decision-making process, identify friction points in their user journey, and understand which navigation elements or search results caused confusion or frustration during critical tasks like benefits checking or appointment scheduling.

- Analyze veteran mental models and expectations for organizing health, benefits, and administrative information within the VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search to identify gaps between user expectations and current categorization, labeling, and content hierarchy that impact findability and task completion rates.

- Evaluate the effectiveness and discoverability of search functionality, menu structures, and information categorization in the VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure, information architecture, search to provide specific recommendations for navigation redesign, improved search algorithms, and content organization that will measurably reduce task abandonment and increase user satisfaction scores.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 
Interviews are ideal for understanding veterans' mental models of how VA services should be organized and their expectations for finding information within the mobile app, providing deep insights into navigation preferences and search behaviors that quantitative methods cannot capture. This qualitative approach reveals the reasoning behind user choices and uncovers pain points in the current information architecture that may not surface through other research methods.

**Implementation Approach:**

- **Session Format:** 
60-minute remote video interviews conducted individually with veterans, allowing for screen sharing to review current app navigation and discuss information architecture preferences without the pressure of task performance.

- **Key Activities:**
  - Card sorting exercises to understand how veterans categorize VA services and expect them to be grouped in navigation
  - Tree testing discussions where participants explain their expected paths to find specific services or information
  - Search behavior exploration through scenarios asking how they would look for specific benefits, appointments, or documents
  - Mental model mapping conversations about their understanding of VA service relationships and hierarchies
  - Comparative analysis discussions reviewing navigation patterns from other apps they successfully use

- **Evaluation Protocol:**
  - Audio recording with participant consent for detailed analysis of language patterns and terminology preferences
  - Real-time note-taking focused on participant reasoning, frustrations, and suggested improvements
  - Post-interview synthesis documenting navigation expectations, information architecture gaps, and search strategy preferences
  - Cross-participant pattern analysis to identify common mental models and divergent needs across veteran demographics

**VA Compliance Notes:**

- **Collaboration Cycle:** 
Research plan review required 2 weeks prior to recruitment; conversation guide approval needed 1 week before sessions; findings presentation scheduled within 2 weeks of completion to inform design iterations.

- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** 
Participants can skip any questions about specific VA experiences that cause discomfort; interviews focus on preferences and expectations rather than past negative experiences; researchers trained to recognize signs of distress and redirect conversations appropriately.

- **Accessibility:** 
Screen reader compatible materials provided in advance; alternative communication methods available for participants with hearing impairments; flexible scheduling to accommodate various disability-related needs and medical appointments.

## Top 4 Risks for VA Mobile App Navigation & Search Interview Research

**Recruitment Challenges:** Difficulty finding Veterans with diverse mobile app usage patterns who can provide meaningful feedback on navigation pain points, especially those who abandoned the app due to findability issues but are willing to participate in research sessions.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA contact centers to identify Veterans who called for help after struggling with the app; recruit through multiple channels including social media and veteran organizations; offer flexible scheduling and compensation; maintain a backup pool of 150% target participants.

**Execution Issues:** Participants may struggle to articulate navigation and search problems during remote interviews, leading to surface-level feedback that doesn't reveal underlying information architecture issues or mental model mismatches.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop specific task scenarios that naturally expose navigation challenges; use think-aloud protocols with gentle probing questions; prepare screen-sharing backup plans; create a standardized interview guide with follow-up prompts for IA-specific insights; conduct pilot interviews to refine approach.

**Timeline Risks:** Extended recruitment period due to the specific user criteria (active app users vs. strugglers) could delay analysis and push back critical navigation redesign decisions that impact the next app release cycle.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Begin recruitment 3 weeks before interview start date; run recruitment and interviews in parallel waves; prepare interim findings reports after every 5 interviews; establish clear decision points with stakeholders for proceeding with partial data if needed.

**Quality Concerns:** Interview findings may not accurately represent the full spectrum of navigation and search issues due to recall bias from infrequent users and social desirability bias when discussing government services, potentially leading to incomplete IA recommendations.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Combine interviews with live task observation using participants' own devices; triangulate findings with app analytics data on navigation patterns; include both moderated tasks and retrospective questions; validate key findings through follow-up card sorting or tree testing with a broader user group.

## 🚀 Implementation Plan

```diff
+ IMMEDIATE (Week 1 post-research)
```
- **Audit current navigation taxonomy** - UX Research team conducts comprehensive mapping of existing VA Mobile App navigation labels, categories, and user pathways by Friday Week 1
- **Stakeholder alignment workshop** - Product Owner schedules 2-hour session with VA leadership, IT, and user advocacy groups to review research findings and establish navigation priorities by Wednesday Week 1
- **Search functionality gap analysis** - Technical team documents current search capabilities, limitations, and integration points with VA backend systems by Thursday Week 1
- **User journey prioritization** - UX team identifies top 5 critical user flows (benefits, healthcare, claims, documents, profile) that must be preserved in new IA by Friday Week 1

```diff
! SHORT-TERM (Weeks 2-4)
```
- **Navigation prototype development** - Design team creates 3 alternative navigation structures with card sorting validation from 15+ veterans by end of Week 3
- **Search implementation roadmap** - Engineering team defines technical requirements, API integrations, and development timeline for enhanced search functionality by Week 2
- **Information architecture testing** - UX Research conducts moderated usability sessions with 20 veterans testing new navigation concepts by Week 4
- **Navigation decision milestone** - Cross-functional team finalizes primary navigation structure, search approach, and IA hierarchy based on user feedback by end of Week 4
- **Content migration strategy** - Content team maps existing app content to new IA structure and identifies gaps/redundancies by Week 3

```diff
- LONGER-TERM (Month 2+)
```
- **Phased rollout implementation** - Development team builds and deploys navigation changes in 3 phases: core structure (Month 2), enhanced search (Month 3), advanced filtering (Month 4)
- **Accessibility compliance validation** - Ensure new navigation meets Section 508 requirements and conducts testing with assistive technology users by Month 3
- **Search personalization features** - Implement user-specific search suggestions and recent activity integration by Month 4

**Success Metrics:**
- **Task completion rate improvement** - Measure 20%+ increase in successful completion of top 5 user tasks (benefits lookup, appointment scheduling, claim status, document upload, profile updates) within new navigation
- **Search success rate tracking** - Monitor search result click-through rates, query refinement frequency, and zero-result searches with target of <15% failed searches
- **User satisfaction scores** - Quarterly NPS surveys showing 15+ point improvement in navigation ease-of-use ratings compared to baseline
- **Follow-up assessment plan** - Monthly analytics reviews for 6 months post-launch, plus quarterly user interviews to identify emerging navigation pain points and optimization opportunities
