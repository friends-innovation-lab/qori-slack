# 📄 Research Brief – VA Health Benefits Mobile App Navigation

> **📦 Project Area:** Digital Services &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **👩🏽‍🔬 Prepared by:** Taylor Kim, Mobile UX Researcher &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **🗓️ Date:** September 23, 2025

## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues such as difficulty in finding necessary information and confusing navigation. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving access to essential services. Actionable insights are needed for the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- High customer churn rate is causing a decrease in revenue and market share
- Users are experiencing difficulty navigating the website, leading to decreased engagement and conversions
- Stakeholders want to address the lack of personalized recommendations, resulting in missed cross-selling opportunities and reduced customer satisfaction

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter barriers in accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app.
- Which features within the main dashboard are most frequently used and prioritized by veterans.
- Why veterans struggle with appointment scheduling and modification processes within the app.
- How veterans interact with search behavior and results to find relevant information within the app.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Download the app and create an account to begin the onboarding process.
2. Explore the initial features of the app, such as setting up your profile and connecting with healthcare providers.
3. Navigate the main dashboard to access different sections and complete tasks like viewing appointments and checking benefits status.
4. Access your health records and manage prescriptions through a designated section in the app.
5. Schedule appointments with healthcare providers and easily modify them if needed.
6. Check your benefits status and submit any required documents through the app.
7. Manage your profile information and adjust notification settings to stay updated on important information.
8. Use the search function to find specific information within the app and interact with the results to access relevant content.

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (e.g., Health, Benefits, Profile) leading to difficulty in finding desired information
- Poor discoverability of key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling due to unclear navigation paths
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app causing user confusion
- Search functionality that returns irrelevant or outdated results, hindering users from finding the information they need
- Information hierarchy that does not align with veteran mental models, making it harder for users to navigate the app effectively
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impacting the usability of the app for all users

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the app onboarding process and discover initial features, and what challenges do they face in understanding the app's functionality?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from efficiently accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app, and how do these barriers impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans struggle with the appointment scheduling and modification process within the app, and what specific pain points do they encounter during this task?
4. What causes veterans to experience difficulties in checking their benefits status and submitting required documents through the app, and how can these challenges be addressed to improve usability and efficiency?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Lack of access to affordable transportation increases missed medical appointments among low-income patients.
- Limited knowledge about mental health resources leads to underutilization of available services among college students.
- Inadequate communication between healthcare providers and patients results in medication non-adherence among elderly individuals.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 8 veterans. The research will focus on evaluating the current information architecture and identifying optimal navigation patterns for the mobile app, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users, and testing on both iOS and Android platforms to account for differences.

**Reasoning:** The refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and attitudes towards the product, providing valuable insights into their preferences and decision-making processes. This method aligns with the scope of the study by focusing on understanding consumer perceptions and behaviors within the constraints of the available resources and time frame.
