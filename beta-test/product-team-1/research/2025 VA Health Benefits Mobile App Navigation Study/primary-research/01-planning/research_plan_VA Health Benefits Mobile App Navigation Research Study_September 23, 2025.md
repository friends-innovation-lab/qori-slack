# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Health Benefits Mobile App Navigation Research Study

Essential tool for Veterans to easily access crucial services within the VA ecosystem."

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features. These decisions will directly impact Veterans by improving their ability to access crucial health and benefits information efficiently. By prioritizing these improvements, the VA can enhance the overall user experience and ensure Veterans can easily find the resources they need.

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Expand the flagship mobile app

**Explanation:** This priority aligns with the focus on improving the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for veterans who actively use the app.

**Additional Priorities:**

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in assisting Veterans/VA Employees/External Stakeholders in accessing health and benefits features seamlessly within their respective workflows.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Navigating to health resources for self-care
- **Daily operations:** Using the search functionality to find specific benefits information
- **Client acquisition:** Accessing VA system for initial consultation

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Inconsistencies in the main navigation structure may lead to difficulty in finding specific health and benefits information, impacting task completion efficiency.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing high user abandonment rates and low satisfaction ratings within the VA Mobile App.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most intuitive main navigation structure through cardsorting to improve user task completion rates.
- Analyze competitive apps to identify successful information architecture models for implementation within the VA Mobile App.
- Conduct usability testing to pinpoint specific pain points in search functionality and task completion flows for health and benefits features.

---

## ✅ Success Criteria & Expected Deliverables

**Success Criteria:**
1. **Decision Support:** By the end of the research project, provide actionable recommendations for improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows of the VA Mobile App. These recommendations should be implemented within 3 months of the research completion to address the identified barriers causing user abandonment and low satisfaction ratings.

2. **Quality Threshold:** Ensure that the research findings are comprehensive, insightful, and directly tied to the root causes of user dissatisfaction and abandonment. All recommendations should be supported by data from card sorting, competitive analysis, and usability testing. Stakeholders should be able to clearly see the impact of the research on improving the user experience of the VA Mobile App.

3. **Timeline Success:** Present initial findings and recommendations to stakeholders within 2 weeks of completing the research. Allow for a 1-week review period for stakeholders to provide feedback and make decisions on next steps. Finalize and implement recommendations within 3 months of research completion to start addressing the identified barriers and improving user satisfaction.

4. **Impact Measurement:** Measure the impact of the implemented recommendations on user satisfaction and task completion rates within 6 months of implementation. Aim to increase user satisfaction ratings by at least 1 point on a 10-point scale and reduce user abandonment rates by 20%. Regularly track and report on these metrics to ensure continuous improvement of the VA Mobile App user experience.

**Key Outputs:**
**Primary Deliverables:**
- User-validated main navigation structure and information architecture recommendations - delivered within 3 days of research completion
- Detailed task completion flows with identified pain points and proposed solutions - delivered within 4 days of research completion
- Improved search functionality design based on user feedback and competitive analysis - delivered within 5 days of research completion

**Supporting Materials:**
- Usability testing report highlighting key insights and recommendations for enhancing user satisfaction - delivered within 2 days of research completion

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting, competitive analysis, and usability testing are chosen to directly identify and address the specific barriers causing high task abandonment rates and low user satisfaction, providing actionable insights for immediate improvements.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Sessions will consist of 45-minute hybrid cardsorting sessions and 60-minute task-based usability testing on personal devices
- Activities will include sorting navigation elements, competitive app comparisons, and task completion scenarios, with data collected through observations, task success rates, and user feedback
- Protocols will focus on evaluating the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows within the VA Mobile App's health and benefits features

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met through regular updates and feedback sessions with stakeholders, with an approval timeline of 2 weeks
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be integrated into participant interactions and debriefing processes
- Accessibility accommodations will be provided for participants with assistive technologies or documented accessibility needs to ensure inclusive research practices.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding qualified participants who meet the specific inclusion criteria and accessibility focus
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA healthcare facilities to recruit participants directly from their patient populations, offer incentives such as gift cards or discounts on VA services to encourage participation

**Execution issues:** Potential difficulties in implementing the cardsorting, competitive, and usability research methodologies effectively
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Conduct pilot testing with a small group of participants to identify and address any issues with the research methodologies before full implementation, provide clear instructions and guidance to participants during the sessions

**Timeline risks:** Delays in the research process that could impact the standard timeline for project completion
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones and deadlines, regularly monitor progress and adjust resources or timelines as needed to ensure on-time delivery

**Quality concerns:** Risks related to data validity and stakeholder satisfaction with the research findings
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation processes, such as triangulation of data from multiple sources, ensure stakeholder involvement throughout the research process to validate findings and address any concerns promptly

**VA-specific risks:** Challenges related to delays in the Collaboration Cycle and compliance issues with VA regulations and policies
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Maintain open communication with VA stakeholders throughout the project, proactively address any compliance issues as they arise, and ensure all research activities align with VA guidelines and requirements.
