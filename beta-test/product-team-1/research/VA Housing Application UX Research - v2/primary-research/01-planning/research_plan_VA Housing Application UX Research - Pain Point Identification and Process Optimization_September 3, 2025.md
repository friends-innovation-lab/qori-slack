# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Housing Application UX Research - Pain Point Identification and Process Optimization

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal - facilitates online application, eligibility screening, document upload, and application status tracking for Veterans seeking housing benefits. This portal streamlines the application process, ensuring timely access to critical benefits within the VA ecosystem."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** Primary Team: VA Digital Experience Team
Collaborating Office: Office of Veterans Experience (VEO)
Support: VA Housing Benefits Administration
Technical Partner: VA.gov Platform Team
Accessibility Consultant: Section 508 Compliance Office  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Jordan Martinez, Senior UX Researcher  
**🗓️ Date:** September 3, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the product team's decision on redesigning the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal, focusing on the online application workflow, eligibility screening, document upload system, and application status tracking. This decision will simplify the application process for Veterans, reducing support calls and improving accessibility compliance. By streamlining these areas, Veterans will experience a more user-friendly and efficient application process.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims

**Explanation:** This priority directly impacts veterans applying for VA housing benefits by expediting the disability claims process, ensuring timely access to benefits for housing needs.

**Additional Priorities (if applicable):**
- **Improve the Veteran login experience:** Enhancing the login experience will make it easier for veterans to access and navigate the housing benefits application portal.
- **Solidify the VA.gov platform:** Ensuring the stability and reliability of the platform is crucial for a seamless application process for veterans.

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal plays a crucial role in the "Putting down roots" life stage for Veterans, assisting them in applying for housing benefits seamlessly.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Accessing the online application workflow for housing benefits
- **Putting down roots:** Completing eligibility screening for housing benefits
- **Retiring:** Uploading required documents for housing benefits application
- **Aging:** Tracking application status for housing benefits

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Veterans may face challenges in navigating the eligibility screening process and uploading necessary documents efficiently.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and prioritize the top 3-5 usability barriers contributing to the 34% application abandonment rate for veterans applying for housing benefits on VA.gov, uncovering actionable insights to improve the online application process.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Pinpoint specific pain points in the eligibility screening process that lead to application abandonment
- Understand the challenges veterans face when uploading required documents during the application
- Identify opportunities to streamline the application status tracking system for a more user-friendly experience

**Success Criteria:**

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, interviews, and competitive analysis are chosen for their ability to directly uncover usability barriers and root causes behind application abandonment rates.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Sessions will consist of 1-hour user interviews and 45-minute usability testing sessions
- Key activities include task-based scenarios, screen sharing, and think-aloud protocols
- Specific evaluation protocols will target the online application workflow, eligibility screening, document upload system, and application status tracking on the VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met with regular check-ins and approvals
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be integrated into participant interactions
- Accessibility accommodations will be provided for participants with assistive technologies or documented accessibility needs

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding qualified participants meeting the specific criteria
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA hospitals and disability organizations to access a pool of potential participants, offer incentives for participation to increase interest

**Execution issues:** Challenges in implementing usability testing, interviews, and competitive analysis smoothly
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct pilot testing with a small group of participants to identify and address any logistical issues before full implementation, provide clear instructions and training to research team members

**Timeline risks:** Potential delays that could impact the standard timeline
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones and deadlines, regularly monitor progress and adjust resources as needed to stay on track

**Quality concerns:** Risks related to data validity and stakeholder satisfaction
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation processes, involve stakeholders in the research process to ensure alignment with expectations, and regularly communicate progress updates to manage expectations and address any concerns early on
