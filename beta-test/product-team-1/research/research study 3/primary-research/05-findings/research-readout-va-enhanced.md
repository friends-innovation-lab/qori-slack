## 🔍 Key Insights Summary

1. **Eligibility Uncertainty:** Participants were unclear on whether they qualified for the benefit.  
2. **Overwhelming Layout:** Participants felt the content was dense and hard to navigate.  
3. **Redundant Document Uploads:** Several participants were confused by the upload process.  

---

## 🎯 Research Goals

- Understand how users determine eligibility for the benefit.
- Evaluate ease of use in the application flow.
- Identify opportunities to improve clarity and reduce burden.

---

## ❓ Research Questions

- Can participants find out if they are eligible?
- What are the most confusing parts of the application process?
- Are users able to complete the task without help?

## 🧠 Detailed Findings & Recommendations

### 🧭 Finding 1: Eligibility Uncertainty

**Summary:**  
Participants were not confident in knowing whether they qualified for benefits.

**Evidence:**  
> “I wasn’t sure if I even qualified.”

**Recommendation:**  
Add a plain-language yes/no eligibility screener before the form.

---

### 🧭 Finding 2: Overwhelming Page Layout

**Summary:**  
Several users commented that the application looked like a “wall of text.”

**Evidence:**  
> “There’s just so much to read. I didn’t know where to start.”

**Recommendation:**  
Use accordion sections and break form steps into bite-sized parts.

---

### 🧭 Finding 3: Confusion About Uploads

**Summary:**  
Participants weren’t sure which documents were required, or if they uploaded correctly.

**Evidence:**  
> “I uploaded the same file three times because I thought it didn’t work.”

**Recommendation:**  
Include upload confirmation messages and a checklist for required files.
