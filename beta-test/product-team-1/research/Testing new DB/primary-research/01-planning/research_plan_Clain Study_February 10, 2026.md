## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** Health serves as a foundational support system that spans the entire veteran journey, providing essential medical care, mental health services, and wellness resources from the moment veterans transition out of military service through their retirement years.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Health provides comprehensive medical care, mental health counseling, substance abuse treatment, and preventive care services that help veterans address service-connected conditions, manage chronic illnesses, and maintain overall physical and mental wellbeing as they adapt to civilian life.
- **Putting down roots:** Health supports veterans in establishing consistent healthcare relationships with local VA medical centers and community providers, accessing family healthcare services, and managing ongoing treatments that allow them to build stable lives in their chosen communities.
- **Retiring:** Health ensures continuity of care for aging veterans through specialized geriatric services, long-term care options, home healthcare services, and coordination with Medicare to address the complex medical needs that often increase with age.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Long wait times for appointments and specialty care that can delay critical treatment and worsen health conditions
- Complex navigation between VA healthcare systems, community care networks, and insurance coverage that creates confusion and gaps in care
- Inconsistent quality and availability of mental health services, particularly in rural areas where veterans may struggle to access trauma counseling and PTSD treatment

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing and user interviews are essential for Health research because they reveal how veterans actually interact with health tools and services in real-time, uncovering pain points that could impact medication adherence, appointment scheduling, or accessing critical health information. This combined approach captures both behavioral data through task observation and contextual insights through direct participant feedback about their health management needs.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** 90-minute hybrid sessions combining 45 minutes of moderated usability testing with health portal/app prototypes, followed by 45 minutes of semi-structured interviews exploring health management workflows, pain points, and preferences
- **Key Activities:** Task-based testing of core health functions (appointment booking, prescription refills, secure messaging), think-aloud protocols during navigation, post-task interviews about current health management practices, barriers to care access, and technology comfort levels
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Measure task completion rates, time-on-task, and error frequency for critical health actions; conduct thematic analysis of interview transcripts focusing on care coordination challenges, digital health literacy gaps, and preferred communication channels with providers

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Requires full Collaboration Cycle review including research plan approval, prototype review, and post-research readout; minimum 4-6 week timeline from initial submission to research execution
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Provide content warnings for health-related scenarios, allow participants to skip tasks that may trigger medical trauma, offer breaks during sessions, and ensure facilitators are trained in recognizing signs of distress related to health discussions
- **Accessibility:** Accommodate screen readers and assistive technologies during usability testing, provide large-print materials, offer phone-based participation options for mobility-limited veterans, and ensure testing environments meet ADA requirements for wheelchair accessibility

# Top 4 Risks for Health Usability Testing & User Interviews

**Recruitment Challenges:** Difficulty recruiting participants with diverse health conditions and varying levels of health literacy, especially vulnerable populations who may be hesitant to share sensitive medical information or participate in health-related research.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with healthcare providers, patient advocacy groups, and community health centers for trusted referrals. Offer flexible participation options (remote/in-person), provide clear privacy protections, and ensure compensation covers transportation/childcare barriers.

**Execution Issues:** Participants experiencing emotional distress or triggering responses when discussing health conditions during interviews, or struggling to complete usability tasks due to health-related cognitive or physical limitations.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Train moderators in trauma-informed research practices, prepare referral resources for mental health support, design accessible testing environments with assistive technology options, and create shorter session formats with breaks as needed.

**Timeline Risks:** Extended recruitment periods due to IRB/ethics review requirements for health research, plus potential delays from participant cancellations due to health emergencies or medical appointments.
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Begin IRB submission process early, build 2-3 week buffer into timeline, maintain waitlist of backup participants, and establish clear rescheduling policies that accommodate health-related cancellations.

**Quality Concerns:** Participants providing socially desirable responses about health behaviors rather than honest feedback, or self-censoring due to stigma around certain health conditions, leading to invalid insights for health product recommendations.
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Use anonymous feedback collection methods, employ indirect questioning techniques, create psychologically safe interview environments, and triangulate findings with behavioral observation data from usability tasks rather than relying solely on self-reported information.
