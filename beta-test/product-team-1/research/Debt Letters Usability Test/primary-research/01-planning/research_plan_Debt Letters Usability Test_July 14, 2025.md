# 🧭 Research Plan – Debt Letters Usability Test

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "Debt Letters" is a service provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to inform veterans of any outstanding debts owed to the VA. These letters are official notifications regarding any financial obligations that need to be addressed by the recipient. This service is part of the VA's commitment to ensuring transparency and accountability in financial matters related to veterans' benefits and services.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** The research goal is to analyze how Veterans interpret the current debt letter format to identify specific pain points and areas of confusion, with the aim of improving the clarity and effectiveness of communication in future correspondence.

**Supporting Goals:**

- Conduct usability testing with target users to identify specific pain points and areas of improvement in the current system. This will provide valuable insights into how users interact with the system and help inform design decisions to enhance usability.

- Analyze user feedback and behavior data to understand patterns and trends in user interactions. By examining user feedback and behavior data, we can gain a deeper understanding of user needs and preferences, which can guide the development of more user-friendly features and functionalities.

- Compare the usability of the current system with competitor products to identify strengths and weaknesses. By conducting a comparative analysis, we can benchmark the usability of our system against competitors and identify opportunities for differentiation and improvement.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Approach:** usability

**Session Structure and Duration:**
- The session format will consist of a one-on-one interaction between the researcher and the participant.
- Each session will last approximately 60 minutes to allow for in-depth exploration of usability issues.
- The session flow will include an introduction to the study, usability tasks, debriefing, and feedback collection.

**Specific Approach and Techniques:**
- The methodology will involve a think-aloud protocol where participants verbalize their thoughts while completing usability tasks.
- Data will be collected through audio recordings, screen recordings, and observational notes.
- Procedures will include pre-testing of usability tasks, task scenarios, and post-test interviews for feedback.

**VA Compliance Considerations:**
- IRB approval will be obtained prior to conducting the study, with a timeline of 4-6 weeks for approval.
- Informed consent procedures will involve providing participants with a detailed explanation of the study and obtaining their voluntary consent.
- Data privacy and security protocols will include storing data securely and anonymizing participant information.
- Veteran-specific ethical considerations will be taken into account, such as ensuring sensitivity to potential triggers related to military experiences.

**Trauma-Informed Design Elements:**
- To support veteran mental health, the research environment will be calm and welcoming, with options for breaks if needed.
- Accommodations for PTSD and anxiety will include providing a quiet space, allowing participants to take breaks, and offering support resources.
- The research environment will be designed to be safe and supportive, with trained staff who are knowledgeable about trauma-informed care.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** Military veterans who have received formal debt collection notices within the last six months. These individuals are crucial for our research as their experiences can provide valuable insights into the challenges faced by veterans in managing their finances post-service.

**📋 Recruitment Strategy:**

**Specific Recruitment Channels:**

- Utilize VA-specific recruitment channels such as VA job boards, VA career fairs, and VA social media platforms.
- Partner with veteran organizations and community groups to reach out to potential participants through their networks and events.
- Utilize digital outreach methods such as targeted social media advertising, email campaigns, and online job boards to reach the target audience effectively.

**Partnership Opportunities:**

- Collaborate with relevant VA programs and offices to leverage their resources and networks for recruitment efforts.
- Partner with external veteran service organizations such as the American Legion, Veterans of Foreign Wars, and Disabled American Veterans to tap into their membership base.
- Engage with community groups and advocacy organizations that focus on veteran issues to expand outreach efforts and increase participation.

**Screening Criteria and Process:**

- Clearly define inclusion/exclusion criteria based on specific qualifications and experience requirements for the study.
- Develop screening questions that are tailored to the target audience and align with the study objectives.
- Implement verification methods such as requesting military service records or conducting background checks to ensure participants meet the eligibility criteria.

**Accessibility Considerations:**

- Provide accommodations for participants with disabilities, such as offering sign language interpreters or accessible transportation options.
- Address language and communication needs by providing materials in multiple languages and offering translation services.
- Ensure technology access and support by providing technical assistance for participants who may require assistance with online platforms or virtual communication tools.

**👥 Demographic Requirements:**

**Demographic must-haves:** 
- Service era diversity: 2 participants from recent veterans (post-9/11), 2 participants from Vietnam era veterans, 2 participants from Gulf War era veterans
- Income diversity: 3 participants with household income $49,000 or less
- Racial/ethnic diversity: 3 participants identifying as Black, Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, Asian, or Native American
- Cognitive considerations: 2 participants with diagnosed cognitive disorders
- Gender diversity: 2 participants identifying as other than male
- LGBTQ+ representation: 2 participants identifying as LGBTQ+
- Education diversity: 2 participants without degrees
- Age diversity: 2 participants under 35 and 2 participants over 55
- Language diversity: 2 non-native English speakers
- Accessibility needs: 2 screen reader users

**Secondary criteria (nice-to-haves):**
- Geographic diversity: 2 participants from rural areas
- VA service usage patterns: 2 participants with frequent VA service usage
- Technology comfort levels: 2 participants with low technology comfort levels

Recruitment challenges may include finding participants who meet all the specified criteria within the limited participant count. Additional outreach efforts may be needed to ensure a diverse and representative sample.

**🧍 Participant Count:** 6-8 participants
