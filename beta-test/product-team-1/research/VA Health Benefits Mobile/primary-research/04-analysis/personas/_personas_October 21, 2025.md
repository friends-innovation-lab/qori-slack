## **Persona Characteristics**

**Persona: Sarah Thompson - Experienced User**

📝 **Context & Scenario:** Sarah is a Navy veteran who served as a hospital corpsman for 6 years and was discharged in 2021. She works night shifts and prefers to access the VA Health Benefits Mobile app in the afternoon after waking up. Sarah primarily uses the app for managing her prescriptions and checking upcoming appointments. Today, she needs to refill her prescription, send a message to her doctor, check her disability benefits, update her contact information, and use the search function to find specific information.

🎓 **Experience Level:** Experienced - Sarah has been using the VA Health Benefits Mobile app for over 2 years and is very familiar with the process of managing prescriptions and appointments. She understands the general layout of the app and knows where to find specific features.

🔧 **Approach Style:** Sarah is efficient and values simplicity in interface design. She prefers to avoid complex features due to past experiences of poor user experience. When faced with new or complex tasks, Sarah tends to be cautious and may seek alternative methods if the app interface is not intuitive.

💬 **Communication Preferences:** Sarah prefers step-by-step instructions and clear, concise information. She values straightforward navigation and easily accessible features. Visual cues and clear labels are important to her for efficient task completion.

🤝 **Trust Factors:** Sarah's confidence in the app is built on clear information display, obvious action buttons, and efficient workflows. She values when features are designed with user needs in mind and can be highly effective. However, her trust can be undermined by hidden or non-obvious interactive elements, poor search functionality, and confusing information hierarchy.

🏷️ **Demographic Details:** Navy veteran, hospital corpsman, discharged in 2021, night shift worker, Samsung Galaxy device user, 2+ years experience with the VA Health Benefits Mobile app.

💬 **Supporting Evidence:** Sarah's pragmatic approach to technology limitations, patient testing process despite frustrations, and clear articulation of user needs and expectations align with an experienced user who values efficiency and simplicity in interface design. Her interest in improved search functionality and willingness to adapt to app limitations further support her characteristics as an experienced user.

## **Goals & Frustrations**

**Persona: John**

🎯 **Goals & Motivations:**
• Schedule a medical appointment easily: John wants to be able to book appointments with his healthcare provider quickly and efficiently to manage his health effectively.
• Access his medical records: John values being able to view his medical history and test results to stay informed about his health status.
• Receive reminders for medication refills: John relies on reminders to ensure he doesn't run out of essential medications.

😤 **Frustrations & Barriers:**
• Complicated appointment scheduling process: The complex steps involved in booking an appointment make it difficult for John to navigate the system.
• Inaccessible medical records: John struggles to access his medical records easily, leading to frustration and confusion.
• Lack of timely medication refill reminders: Without reminders, John often forgets to refill his medications, impacting his health management.

⚡ **Top Frustration Impact:** The difficulty in scheduling appointments affects John's ability to receive timely medical care, potentially leading to delays in treatment and worsening health outcomes.
✅ **Success Factors:** Clear and intuitive appointment booking system, easy access to medical records, and automated medication refill reminders.

🎯 **Use Case Context:** John, a veteran with multiple health conditions, attempts to schedule an urgent appointment through the VA Health Benefits Mobile app but struggles due to the confusing interface and lack of clear instructions.

By mapping out John's goals, motivations, frustrations, and success factors, we can better understand his needs and tailor the mobile app to address his specific challenges and improve his overall experience.

## **Opportunities**

**Persona: Veteran John**

💡 **Primary Opportunity:** Provide personalized recommendations for health benefits based on John's specific needs and preferences.

🛠️ **Implementation Approach:**
- Develop a questionnaire to gather information on John's health history, preferences, and goals.
- Use AI algorithms to analyze the data and generate tailored recommendations for health benefits.
- Implement a notification system to alert John of new benefits or changes that may be relevant to him.

📈 **Success Metrics:** Increase in engagement with personalized recommendations, higher utilization of recommended benefits, positive feedback from John on the relevance of the suggestions.

⚡ **Quick Wins:** 
- Send a personalized welcome message to John upon login.
- Implement a "Recommended for You" section on the home screen.
- Provide easy access to a live chat feature for personalized assistance.

🎯 **Relevant Use Case:** Personalized recommendations can inform the design of the app's homepage layout and content organization, ensuring that John easily finds the information most relevant to him.

⭐ **Priority Level:** High - Personalization is key to improving John's experience and increasing his engagement with the app.

**Persona: Caregiver Sarah**

💡 **Primary Opportunity:** Streamline the process for accessing and managing health benefits on behalf of the veteran Sarah cares for.

🛠️ **Implementation Approach:**
- Create a caregiver portal where Sarah can easily access and manage the health benefits of the veteran she cares for.
- Implement a shared calendar feature to schedule appointments and reminders for both Sarah and the veteran.
- Provide educational resources specifically tailored to caregivers to support their role effectively.

📈 **Success Metrics:** Increase in caregiver engagement with the portal, improved coordination of care between Sarah and the veteran, positive feedback on the usability of the caregiver features.

⚡ **Quick Wins:** 
- Send a personalized email to Sarah introducing the caregiver portal.
- Include a step-by-step guide on how to navigate the caregiver features.
- Offer a feedback survey to gather insights on areas for improvement.

🎯 **Relevant Use Case:** The caregiver portal can serve as a central hub for Sarah to manage the veteran's health benefits, ensuring seamless coordination and communication between the caregiver and the veteran.

⭐ **Priority Level:** Medium - Simplifying the process for caregivers like Sarah can significantly enhance their experience and support their caregiving responsibilities.
