## 🔎 Background

This research will provide crucial insights for the VA Mobile App's redesign by informing decisions on main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features. By prioritizing navigation improvements and enhancing user experience, Veterans will benefit from a more intuitive and efficient app interface, ultimately improving their access to vital services and resources. This strategic redesign will not only enhance user satisfaction and engagement but also drive increased usage and adoption of the VA Mobile App, ultimately improving the delivery of essential services to Veterans.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing easy access to health and benefits information and resources, helping veterans take care of themselves, put down roots, and eventually retire with the support they need.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** The VA Mobile App allows veterans to easily access health information, schedule appointments, and track their wellness goals.
- **Putting down roots:** Veterans can use the app to explore and apply for benefits, access educational resources, and connect with community support services.
- **Retiring:** As veterans transition into retirement, the app provides tools for financial planning, accessing VA benefits, and staying connected to the VA healthcare system.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App may include confusing navigation, difficulty finding specific information or resources, and challenges with completing tasks related to health and benefits features. It is essential to ensure that the app's structure, search functionality, and task completion flows are user-friendly and intuitive to address these pain points effectively.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting, competitive analysis, and usability research are specifically right for the VA Mobile App as they provide insights into how users interact with the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features. Cardsorting helps understand how users categorize and prioritize information, competitive analysis provides insights into industry best practices, and usability research evaluates the overall user experience.

**Implementation Approach:**
- For cardsorting, we will conduct individual or group sessions with participants to organize content into categories that make sense to them. This will help inform the information architecture and navigation structure of the app.
- Competitive analysis will involve evaluating similar apps in the market to identify strengths and weaknesses in terms of search functionality and task completion flows.
- Usability research will involve task-based testing to evaluate how easily users can complete specific health and benefits-related tasks within the app.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline will be adhered to throughout the research process to ensure timely feedback and collaboration with VA stakeholders.
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be taken into account when recruiting veteran participants, ensuring a safe and respectful research environment.
- Accessibility accommodations will be made for participants with diverse needs, such as providing alternative formats for materials and ensuring the usability of the app for individuals with disabilities.

By combining cardsorting, competitive analysis, and usability research, we will be able to gather comprehensive insights into the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features of the VA Mobile App. This methodology will help us understand user preferences, industry standards, and usability issues to inform improvements and enhancements to the app.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios:
1. Find information on VA health benefits and navigate to the enrollment page.
2. Use the search functionality to find a specific medical facility near you.
3. Complete the process of scheduling an appointment with a healthcare provider through the app.
- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate the app.
- Data collection: Set up screen recording software, use an observation notes template to record participant actions and feedback, time each task completion.
- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using the app, any difficulties encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different sections and features of the VA Mobile App related to health and benefits.
- Sorting methodology: Conduct an open sort where participants group cards based on their own logic, then a closed sort where they categorize cards according to predetermined categories.
- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures to analyze card groupings, dendrograms to visualize relationships between categories, and similarity matrices to identify common themes.
- Results interpretation: Translate card sorting findings into recommendations for improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows within the app.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Use heuristics to assess the usability of the VA Mobile App compared to competitors, create feature comparison matrices to identify strengths and weaknesses.
- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of key features, document interaction flows, and record usability notes during the analysis.
- Analysis criteria: Identify specific areas where the VA Mobile App excels or falls short compared to competitors, extract best practices from competitors' apps.
- Synthesis approach: Translate competitive insights into actionable recommendations for enhancing the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows of the VA Mobile App.
