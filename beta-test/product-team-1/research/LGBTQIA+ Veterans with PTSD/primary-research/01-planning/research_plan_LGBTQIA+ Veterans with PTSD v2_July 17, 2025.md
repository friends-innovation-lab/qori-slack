# 🧭 Research Plan – LGBTQIA+ Veterans with PTSD v2

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services provide convenient and accessible mental health support for Veterans through online platforms and virtual appointments. This service is crucial in addressing the mental health needs of Veterans, especially those in remote areas or with limited mobility. Within the VA ecosystem, these digital tools and telehealth services help streamline mental health care delivery and improve overall access to crucial services for Veterans."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** VA Office of Mental Health and Suicide Prevention (OMHSP)  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson  
**🗓️ Date:** July 17, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

1. **Decision statement:** "This research will inform the redesign of VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services to better serve LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD."
2. **Impact statement:** "This decision will improve access to mental health support for LGBTQIA+ veterans, leading to better health outcomes and increased trust in VA services."
3. **Context:** Currently, there is a need to enhance inclusivity and effectiveness of digital tools and telehealth services for this specific veteran population.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify key barriers faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD in accessing and utilizing VA mental health services, with a focus on digital tools, telehealth platforms, and provider interactions, to drive improvements in service delivery.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Explore the unique needs and experiences of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD when using VA mental health digital tools and telehealth services.
- Understand the impact of provider interactions on the mental health treatment outcomes of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD within the VA system.
- Identify specific recommendations for enhancing the accessibility and effectiveness of VA mental health services for LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD.

---

## ✅ Success Criteria & Expected Deliverables

**Success Criteria:**
1. **Decision Support:** By the end of the research period, provide actionable insights on at least 3 key barriers faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD in accessing VA mental health services, including recommendations for improvements to digital tools, telehealth platforms, and provider interactions. These insights should be presented to the VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services team within 2 weeks of the research completion for consideration in their decision-making process.

2. **Quality Threshold:** Ensure that the research findings are based on a minimum of 15 in-depth interviews with LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD, conducted in a culturally sensitive and inclusive manner. The insights gathered should be specific, relevant, and actionable, with clear recommendations for service improvements. The research report should be reviewed by a cross-functional team within the VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services department to ensure its quality and relevance.

3. **Timeline Success:** Complete the research on the unique barriers, needs, and experiences of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD accessing VA mental health services within the standard timeline. Present the findings to the VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services team for review and decision-making within 2 weeks of the research completion. Any necessary follow-up actions or adjustments to the research plan should be communicated promptly to stakeholders.

4. **Impact Measurement:** Measure the success of the research by tracking the implementation of recommendations for service improvements based on the insights gathered from LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD. Monitor the adoption and effectiveness of these improvements over a 6-month period, using feedback from LGBTQIA+ veterans and VA mental health providers as indicators of success. Evaluate the impact of the research on enhancing the accessibility and effectiveness of mental health services for LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD.

**Key Outputs:**
**Primary Deliverables:**
- Analysis of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD experiences accessing VA mental health services, including barriers, needs, and preferences (delivered in 2 days)
- Recommendations for improving VA mental health digital tools and telehealth services for LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD (delivered in 1 day)
- Summary report highlighting key findings and actionable insights for decision-making (delivered in 1 day)

**Supporting Materials:**

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Interviews are optimal for this research as they allow for in-depth exploration of participants' barriers, needs, and experiences, providing rich qualitative data to inform service improvements.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Conduct semi-structured interviews with LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD who have utilized VA mental health services.
- Use open-ended questions to explore participants' experiences with digital tools, telehealth platforms, and provider interactions.
- Collect data on specific challenges faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans in accessing and utilizing VA mental health services.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Ensure compliance with VA Collaboration Cycle requirements and obtain necessary approvals before conducting interviews.
- Implement trauma-informed research practices to create a safe and supportive environment for participants.
- Provide accessibility accommodations, such as offering alternative communication methods or adjusting interview settings, to ensure all participants can fully engage in the research process.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** LGBTQIA+ veterans who have a PTSD diagnosis and have used (or attempted to use) VA mental health services, including digital tools, in-person care, or telehealth within the past 12 months.  

**🧍 Participant Count:** 12-15 participants

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era diversity: 2 participants from recent veterans and 2 participants from different service periods
- Income diversity: 4 participants with household income $49,000 or less
- Racial/ethnic diversity: 2 participants identifying as Black, 2 as Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, 2 as Asian, and 1 as Native American
- Gender diversity: 2 participants identifying as other than male
- Age diversity: 2 participants under 35 and 2 over 55
- Education diversity: At least 2 participants without degrees
- Accessibility needs: 1 assistive technology user if relevant to the study

**Secondary targets:**
- Geographic diversity: 2 participants from rural areas and 2 from urban areas
- VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services usage patterns and experience levels: 2 participants with high usage levels and 2 with low usage levels
- Technology comfort levels: 2 participants with high comfort levels and 2 with low comfort levels

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty finding qualified LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD who have used VA mental health services
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Collaborate with LGBTQIA+ veteran organizations, advertise through social media platforms, and offer incentives for participation to increase recruitment rates.

**Execution issues:** Problems with conducting interviews such as technical difficulties or participant no-shows
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct pilot interviews to identify and address any potential issues, provide clear instructions to participants, and have backup plans in place in case of technical difficulties.

**Timeline risks:** Delays in participant recruitment or data analysis that impact the project timeline
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Set clear milestones and deadlines, regularly track progress, and have contingency plans in place to address any potential delays.

**Quality concerns:** Data validity issues or dissatisfaction from stakeholders
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Use standardized interview protocols, ensure confidentiality and anonymity for participants, and involve stakeholders in the research process to address any concerns or feedback.
