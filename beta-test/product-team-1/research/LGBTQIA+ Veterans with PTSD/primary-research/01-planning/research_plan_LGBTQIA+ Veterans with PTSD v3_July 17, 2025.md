# 🧭 Research Plan – LGBTQIA+ Veterans with PTSD v3

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services provide convenient and accessible resources for Veterans to manage their mental health needs remotely. These services play a crucial role in extending mental health support to Veterans who may face barriers to in-person care within the VA healthcare system."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** VA Office of Mental Health and Suicide Prevention (OMHSP)  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson  
**🗓️ Date:** July 17, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the decision to redesign VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services to better serve LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD. This change aims to provide more inclusive and tailored support for this specific demographic, ultimately improving their mental health outcomes. By updating provider training protocols and creating more inclusive interface design, the VA can better meet the unique needs of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify specific barriers faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD in accessing and utilizing VA mental health services, including digital tools and telehealth platforms, to drive targeted service improvements.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Understand the unique needs and experiences of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD when interacting with VA mental health providers, both in-person and through telehealth.
- Explore the effectiveness and usability of VA mental health digital tools for LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD, and identify areas for enhancement.
- Determine the preferences and challenges of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD in utilizing telehealth services for mental health support within the VA system.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 
["Interviews"] are optimal for this research as they allow for in-depth exploration of participants' experiences, emotions, and perspectives, providing rich qualitative data to uncover unique barriers and needs faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD accessing VA mental health services.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format: Semi-structured interviews lasting approximately 60-90 minutes to allow for detailed discussions.
- Key activities: Probing questions to understand participants' experiences with VA mental health services, digital tools, telehealth platforms, and provider interactions. Use of open-ended questions to encourage participants to share their thoughts and feelings.
- Data collection techniques: Audio recording and note-taking to capture participants' responses accurately.
- Specific protocols: Focus on evaluating the usability, accessibility, and effectiveness of VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services in meeting the unique needs of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle: Ensure compliance with VA research protocols and obtain necessary approvals before conducting interviews.
- Trauma-informed research: Approach interviews with sensitivity to potential triggers and ensure participants feel safe and supported throughout the process.
- Accessibility accommodations: Provide options for participants to choose preferred communication methods, ensure materials are accessible, and offer support for any special needs.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** LGBTQIA+ veterans who have a PTSD diagnosis and have used (or attempted to use) VA mental health services, including digital tools, in-person care, or telehealth within the past 12 months.  

**🧍 Participant Count:** 12-15 participants

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era diversity: 2 recent veterans, 2 from different service periods
- Income diversity: 4 participants with household income $49,000 or less
- Racial/ethnic diversity: 2 Black, 2 Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, 2 Asian, 1 Native American
- Gender diversity: 2 participants identifying as other than male
- Age diversity: 2 participants under 35, 2 over 55
- Education diversity: At least 2 participants without degrees
- Accessibility needs: 1 assistive technology user

**Secondary targets:**
- Geographic diversity: 2 participants from rural areas, 2 from urban areas
- VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services usage patterns and experience levels: 2 high usage, 2 low usage
- Technology comfort levels: 2 high comfort, 2 low comfort

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty finding LGBTQIA+ veterans who meet the specific criteria for participation
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Work closely with VA mental health providers and LGBTQIA+ veteran support organizations to identify and reach out to potential participants. Utilize targeted social media advertising and outreach to relevant online communities.

**Execution issues:** Technical difficulties or scheduling conflicts impacting the quality of the interviews
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough pilot testing of the interview process with a diverse group of participants to identify and address any potential issues. Provide participants with clear instructions and technical support to ensure smooth interview experiences.

**Timeline risks:** Unexpected delays in participant recruitment, scheduling, or data analysis
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with specific milestones and deadlines, and regularly track progress against these targets. Build in buffer time for potential delays and communicate any issues or changes to stakeholders proactively.

**Quality concerns:** Data validity and stakeholder satisfaction may be compromised if the research process is not rigorous or thorough
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement a robust data validation process, including member checking and triangulation of data sources. Regularly engage with stakeholders throughout the research process to gather feedback and ensure alignment with their needs and expectations.
