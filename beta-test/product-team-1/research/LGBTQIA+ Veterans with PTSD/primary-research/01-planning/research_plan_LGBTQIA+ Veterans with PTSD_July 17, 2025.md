# 🧭 Research Plan – LGBTQIA+ Veterans with PTSD

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services provide convenient and accessible resources for Veterans to access mental health support remotely. This offering is crucial within the VA ecosystem as it enhances Veterans' ability to receive timely care and support, ultimately improving their overall well-being."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** VA Office of Mental Health and Suicide Prevention (OMHSP)  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson  
**🗓️ Date:** July 17, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the decision to redesign VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services to better serve LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD. This change will improve access to inclusive mental health resources, leading to better outcomes for veterans. By updating provider training protocols and creating more inclusive interface designs, the VA can enhance the overall experience for this specific population.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Understand the unique barriers, needs, and experiences of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD accessing VA mental health services, including digital tools, telehealth platforms, and provider interactions, to drive service improvements.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Identify specific pain points and challenges faced by LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD when using VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services.
- Uncover opportunities to enhance the accessibility and inclusivity of VA mental health services for LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD.
- Gain insights into preferences and expectations of LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD regarding digital tools, telehealth services, and provider interactions within the VA system.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** 
["Interviews"] offer a personalized approach to gather in-depth insights from LGBTQIA+ veterans with PTSD, allowing for a nuanced understanding of their experiences and needs within the VA mental health system.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Conduct semi-structured interviews with LGBTQIA+ veterans, focusing on their experiences with VA mental health services.
- Use open-ended questions to explore barriers, needs, and preferences related to digital tools, telehealth, and provider interactions.
- Implement a trauma-informed approach, providing a safe and supportive environment for participants to share their experiences.
- Utilize VA-approved protocols for data collection and ensure compliance with Collaboration Cycle requirements.
- Offer accessibility accommodations, such as ASL interpreters or closed captioning, to ensure all participants can fully engage in the research process.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Submit the research plan for Collaboration Cycle approval at least 4 weeks prior to the proposed start date.
- Prioritize trauma-informed research practices, including informed consent processes and sensitivity to potential triggers.
- Ensure all research materials and sessions are accessible to participants with disabilities, following VA guidelines for accommodation.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** LGBTQIA+ veterans who have a PTSD diagnosis and have used (or attempted to use) VA mental health services, including digital tools, in-person care, or telehealth within the past 12 months.  

**🧍 Participant Count:** 12-15 participants

**👥 Demographic Targets:**
**Demographic targets:**
- Service era diversity: Include participants from recent veterans and different service periods when possible (at least 2-3 participants)
- Income diversity: Aim for participants with household income $49,000 or less (target ~30% of sample, at least 4-5 participants)
- Racial/ethnic diversity: Include participants identifying as Black, Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, Asian, or Native American when possible (at least 2-3 participants)
- Gender diversity: Include participants identifying as other than male when possible (at least 1 participant)
- Age diversity: Include both under 35 and over 55 when possible (at least 2-3 participants in each age group)
- Education diversity: Include participants without degrees when possible (at least 1 participant)
- Accessibility needs: Include assistive technology users if relevant to the study (at least 1 participant)

**Secondary targets:**
- Geographic diversity: Rural and urban participants when possible (at least 1 participant from each setting)
- VA Mental Health Digital Tools & Telehealth Services usage patterns: Mix of high and low usage levels when possible (at least 2-3 participants in each category)
- Technology comfort levels: Mix of high and low comfort when possible (at least 2-3 participants in each category)

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding LGBTQIA+ veterans who meet the specific criteria for participation
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Collaborate with LGBTQIA+ veteran organizations and support groups to identify potential participants, offer incentives for participation, and utilize targeted social media advertising to reach the desired demographic.

**Execution issues:** Potential technical difficulties or scheduling conflicts during the interview process
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough pilot testing of the interview process, provide clear instructions to participants, offer flexible scheduling options, and have backup plans in place in case of any unforeseen issues.

**Timeline risks:** Delays in participant recruitment, scheduling, or data analysis that impact the project timeline
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Establish a detailed project timeline with built-in buffers for potential delays, closely monitor progress throughout the research process, communicate any issues promptly with stakeholders, and adjust timelines as needed to ensure timely completion.

**Quality concerns:** Data validity issues due to participant bias or stakeholder dissatisfaction with research outcomes
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation techniques, such as triangulation with multiple data sources, member checking with participants, and peer debriefing with other researchers. Keep stakeholders involved and informed throughout the research process to address any concerns or feedback promptly.
