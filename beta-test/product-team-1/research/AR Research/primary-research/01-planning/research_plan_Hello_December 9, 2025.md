# Research Plan — Hello

📦 **Product Area:** Hello — A virtual platform connecting Veterans with resources, support, and a community of fellow service members.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Abdul Rehman · no

🗓️ **Date:** December 9, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform a decision about implementing a new program to improve mental health services for Veterans. By providing better access to mental health resources, Veterans will experience improved well-being and quality of life. The VA will see increased efficiency and effectiveness in addressing mental health needs, leading to better overall outcomes for Veterans.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Improve the Veteran login experience

**Explanation:** Enhancing the login experience for Veterans will ensure a smoother and more efficient interaction with the VA.gov platform.

**Additional Priorities:**
- Solidify the VA.gov platform: Ensuring the stability and reliability of the VA.gov platform is crucial for providing a seamless online experience for Veterans.
- Enhance Veterans' personalized online experience: Customizing the online experience for Veterans will help them access relevant information and services more easily.

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** Hello can be a valuable tool for veterans in the "Taking care of myself" phase of their journey, helping them manage their health and wellbeing.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Hello provides veterans with resources and tools to monitor and improve their physical and mental health, promoting overall wellbeing.
- **Putting down roots:** Hello offers support and guidance for veterans as they transition into civilian life, helping them establish routines and connections in their new community.
- **Retiring:** Hello assists veterans in retirement by providing access to healthcare services, financial planning resources, and social support networks.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Limited access to healthcare services
- Difficulty adjusting to civilian life
- Lack of support in retirement planning

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Measure the impact of the new user interface design on user engagement with Hello by analyzing the increase in daily active users over a 3-month period.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to identify pain points in the current Hello interface and provide recommendations for improvement.
- Survey Hello users to understand their preferences and expectations for features to be included in future updates.
- Analyze user feedback data to determine the most requested features for prioritization in the product roadmap for Hello.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for Hello research as it focuses on evaluating the user experience and interaction with the product, which is crucial for understanding how well Hello meets the needs of its users.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting approximately 45-60 minutes to allow for in-depth exploration of user interactions with Hello.
- **Key Activities:** Use of task-based scenarios to observe how participants navigate through Hello, think-aloud protocols to capture user thought processes, and post-task interviews to gather feedback on user experience.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Utilize a combination of quantitative metrics (such as task completion rates and time on task) and qualitative feedback (such as user comments and suggestions) to evaluate the usability of Hello.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Coordinate with VA stakeholders to ensure alignment with research goals and timelines, providing regular updates on progress and findings.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Prioritize participant comfort and safety, offering breaks and support as needed, and ensuring that all interactions are conducted in a respectful and non-triggering manner.
- **Accessibility:** Provide accommodations such as screen readers, large font sizes, and alternative input methods to meet the diverse needs of participants.
