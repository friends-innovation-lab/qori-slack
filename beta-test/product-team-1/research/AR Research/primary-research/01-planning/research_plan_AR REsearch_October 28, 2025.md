## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** Research fits in the veteran journey during the phase of "Taking care of myself" as veterans may need to conduct research on healthcare options, benefits, and resources available to them.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans may need to conduct research on healthcare options, benefits, and resources available to them to ensure they are taking care of their physical and mental well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** Research may also be necessary during this phase as veterans look into housing options, employment opportunities, and community resources to establish stability and support.
- **Retiring:** Research becomes critical during retirement as veterans navigate their benefits, financial planning, and healthcare options for their post-military life.

**Critical Pain Points:** 
- Lack of access to reliable information and resources for veterans.
- Overwhelming amount of information to sift through and understand.
- Difficulty in navigating the complex systems and processes related to veteran benefits and resources.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Cardsorting is particularly suitable for Research research as it allows participants to organize and categorize information related to the topic, providing insights into how users conceptualize and navigate through research content.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Conducting multiple cardsorting sessions with participants to gather diverse perspectives and insights on how they categorize and prioritize research topics.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Using open and closed cardsorting techniques to understand how participants group and label research topics, as well as collecting qualitative feedback on their reasoning behind their choices.
- Specific protocols for evaluating Research user experience: Analyzing the results of the cardsorting sessions to identify common themes, patterns, and discrepancies in how participants organize research topics, and using this information to inform the design and structure of research content.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring that the cardsorting research aligns with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline for conducting user research and incorporating feedback into the design process.
- Trauma-informed research considerations for veteran participants: Implementing trauma-informed research practices to create a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants during the cardsorting sessions, and providing resources for those who may need additional support.
- Accessibility accommodations for diverse participant needs: Making accommodations for participants with diverse needs, such as providing materials in alternative formats, ensuring physical accessibility of the research sessions, and offering support for participants with disabilities to fully participate in the cardsorting research. 

By utilizing cardsorting as a methodology for Research research, we can gain valuable insights into how users perceive and interact with research content, informing the design and organization of research materials to better meet the needs and preferences of our target audience.
