# 🧭 Research Plan – Veterans Using Assistive Technology on the VA Mobile App

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Mobile Application" 

The VA Mobile Application is a secure and user-friendly tool developed by the Department of Veterans Affairs to provide veterans with convenient access to essential services and resources. This mobile app is designed to streamline communication and access to VA benefits, healthcare information, appointments, and other important features. It serves as a valuable extension of the comprehensive services offered by the VA, allowing veterans to easily manage their healthcare needs and stay connected with their care team.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Investigate the utilization of assistive technology by veterans with disabilities within the VA Mobile app to pinpoint key obstacles and possibilities for enhancing accessibility, usability, and user satisfaction.

**Supporting Goals:**

- Conduct usability testing with Veterans who use assistive technology to access the VA Mobile App to identify specific barriers and challenges they face in navigating the app. This will provide insights into how the app can be improved to better accommodate their needs and enhance their user experience.

- Explore the preferences and needs of Veterans who rely on assistive technology when using the VA Mobile App through surveys or interviews. By understanding their unique requirements and expectations, we can tailor the app's features and functionalities to better meet their needs and improve overall usability.

- Evaluate the effectiveness of accessibility features within the VA Mobile App for Veterans using assistive technology. This research goal will help determine if the current accessibility features are sufficient and identify areas for improvement to ensure a more seamless and inclusive user experience for all Veterans.

## ❓ Research Questions

1. How does the VA Mobile App interface accommodate the needs of Veterans who rely on assistive technology to access digital services, such as screen readers or voice recognition software, in terms of usability and accessibility?
- This question directly relates to the VA Mobile App and its usability for Veterans who use assistive technology, focusing on how well the app meets their needs and allows them to access digital services effectively.

2. What specific features or functionalities within the VA Mobile App enhance the user experience for Veterans who rely on assistive technology, and how can these be further optimized to improve usability and accessibility?
- This question delves into the specific aspects of the VA Mobile App that cater to Veterans using assistive technology, aiming to identify areas for improvement to enhance usability and accessibility for this user group.

3. How do Veterans who rely on assistive technology perceive the overall usability of the VA Mobile App in comparison to other digital services provided by the VA, and what factors contribute to their decision-making process in choosing to use the app?
- This question explores the comparative usability of the VA Mobile App for Veterans using assistive technology and seeks to understand the key factors influencing their decision to utilize the app over other digital services offered by the VA.

4. In what ways can the VA Mobile App be optimized to better support Veterans who rely on assistive technology, based on their feedback and preferences for usability and accessibility features?
- This question focuses on gathering insights from Veterans using assistive technology to identify opportunities for enhancing the VA Mobile App's usability and accessibility, aligning with their specific needs and preferences.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Approach:** usability

**Session Structure and Duration:**
- The session format for VA Mobile App evaluation will include a combination of individual usability testing sessions and focus group discussions.
- Each individual usability testing session will last approximately 60 minutes, with a 10-minute break in between sessions.
- The focus group discussions will be conducted for 90 minutes, allowing for in-depth feedback and discussion among participants.
- The session flow will begin with a brief introduction to the study, followed by a demonstration of the VA Mobile App. Participants will then be asked to complete specific tasks using the app, while researchers observe and take notes. The session will conclude with a debriefing and feedback session.

**Specific Approach and Techniques:**
- The ["usability"] implementation for VA Mobile App will involve a combination of think-aloud protocols, task analysis, and cognitive walkthroughs.
- Data collection methods will include video recording of participants using the app, as well as post-session surveys and interviews to gather qualitative feedback.
- Specific protocols will be followed for each task, including clear instructions and prompts for participants, as well as standardized criteria for evaluating usability.

**VA Compliance Considerations:**
- IRB approval will be obtained prior to conducting the research, with a timeline of 4-6 weeks for review and approval.
- Informed consent procedures will include a detailed explanation of the study purpose, procedures, risks, and benefits, as well as the right to withdraw at any time.
- Data privacy and security protocols will be strictly followed, including encryption of data, secure storage, and limited access to personal information.
- Veteran-specific ethical considerations will be taken into account, including potential triggers for PTSD and anxiety, and the need for sensitivity and empathy in interactions with participants.

**Trauma-Informed Design Elements:**
- The research team will be trained in trauma-informed care principles, including creating a safe and supportive environment for participants.
- Accommodations will be made for participants with PTSD and anxiety, including breaks, relaxation techniques, and the option to have a support person present during sessions.
- The research environment will be designed to be calming and non-threatening, with soft lighting, comfortable seating, and minimal distractions to support veteran mental health during the research process.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** This study is seeking participation from military veterans who utilize the VA Mobile app and depend on assistive technology to access digital services. These participants are crucial for research on improving the accessibility and usability of the VA Mobile app for individuals with disabilities.

**📋 Recruitment Strategy:**

**Specific Recruitment Channels:**

- Utilize VA-specific recruitment channels such as the VA Careers website, VA job fairs, and VA social media platforms to reach Veterans who use the VA Mobile app.
- Partner with veteran organizations such as the American Legion, Veterans of Foreign Wars, and Disabled American Veterans to promote research opportunities to their members.
- Utilize digital outreach methods such as targeted social media advertising, email campaigns, and online forums frequented by Veterans who use assistive technology.

**Partnership Opportunities:**

- Collaborate with relevant VA programs and offices such as the Office of Connected Care and the VA Mobile Health program to access their networks of VA Mobile app users.
- Partner with external veteran service organizations that specialize in assisting Veterans with disabilities and technology access.
- Engage with community groups and advocacy organizations focused on disability rights and assistive technology to reach Veterans who may benefit from the VA Mobile app.

**Screening Criteria and Process:**

- Develop specific inclusion criteria such as being a Veteran who uses the VA Mobile app and relies on assistive technology to access digital services.
- Include screening questions related to the Veterans' experience with the VA Mobile app, their preferred assistive technology, and any challenges they face in accessing digital services.
- Verify qualifications by requesting proof of VA Mobile app usage or assistive technology dependence through documentation or demonstration.

**Accessibility Considerations:**

- Provide accommodations for disabilities such as offering alternative formats for communication, ensuring website and survey accessibility, and providing support for assistive technology users.
- Address language and communication needs by offering multilingual resources, providing interpreters or translation services, and ensuring clear communication throughout the recruitment process.
- Ensure technology access and support by offering technical assistance for using the VA Mobile app, providing guidance on assistive technology compatibility, and offering alternative methods for participation if needed.

**👥 Demographic Requirements:**

**Demographic must-haves:**
- Service era diversity: 2 participants from recent veterans (post-9/11), 2 participants from Vietnam era veterans, 2 participants from other service periods
- Income diversity: 3 participants with household income $49,000 or less
- Racial/ethnic diversity: 3 participants identifying as Black, Hispanic/Latina/Latinx, Asian, or Native American
- Cognitive considerations: 1 participant with diagnosed cognitive disorders
- Gender diversity: 2 participants identifying as other than male
- LGBTQ+ representation: 2 participants identifying as LGBTQ+
- Education diversity: 3 participants without degrees
- Age diversity: 2 participants under 35, 2 participants over 55
- Language diversity: 1 non-native English speaker
- Accessibility needs: 1 screen reader user

**Secondary criteria (nice-to-haves):**
- Geographic diversity: 2 participants from rural areas, 4 participants from urban areas
- VA Mobile App usage patterns and experience levels: 4 participants with previous experience using the VA Mobile App
- Technology comfort levels: 5 participants comfortable with technology

Recruitment challenges may include finding participants with specific cognitive disorders, LGBTQ+ identification, and non-native English speakers within a small participant count of 6-8. Additional efforts may be needed to recruit individuals with these characteristics.

**🧍 Participant Count:** 6-8 participants
