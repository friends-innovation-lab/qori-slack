## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions about the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. The findings will improve the user experience for Veterans accessing health and benefits features, ensuring easier navigation, enhanced search capabilities, and streamlined task completion processes.

By prioritizing navigation improvements based on research findings, Veterans will benefit from a more user-friendly and efficient mobile app experience when accessing crucial health and benefits information. This will ultimately lead to increased user satisfaction, improved access to vital services, and a more seamless interaction with VA resources.

For the VA, implementing research-informed navigation improvements will result in a more effective mobile app that better serves the needs of Veterans. This will lead to increased engagement with VA services, higher user retention rates, and ultimately, improved overall satisfaction with the VA Mobile App.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in the veteran journey by providing easy access to health and benefits information and services, helping veterans take care of themselves, put down roots, and transition into retirement.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the VA Mobile App to schedule appointments, access medical records, and receive important health information to take care of themselves.
- **Putting down roots:** The app allows veterans to explore benefits and resources available to them, helping them establish stability and support in their post-military life.
- **Retiring:** As veterans transition into retirement, the VA Mobile App can assist them in understanding and accessing their benefits, making the process smoother and more manageable.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App could include confusing navigation, difficulty finding specific information or services, and technical glitches that hinder task completion. Improving the app's usability and search functionality could help alleviate these issues and enhance the user experience for veterans.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Conduct usability testing and interviews to identify the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that cause 45% of veterans to abandon mobile app tasks, and understand the root causes behind the current 4.2/10 user satisfaction rating.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing with 30 veterans to pinpoint specific pain points in the app's navigation and information architecture that lead to task abandonment.
- Analyze user feedback from interviews to identify key areas of improvement in the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features.
- Develop a comprehensive report outlining actionable recommendations to address the identified barriers and improve user satisfaction rating to at least 7/10.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability interviews are specifically right for researching the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows because they allow for in-depth exploration of user interactions and experiences with the app, providing valuable insights into how veterans navigate and utilize its health and benefits features.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Conduct 1-on-1 usability interviews with veteran participants, lasting approximately 60 minutes each, to allow for detailed discussions and observations.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Use task scenarios to assess participants' ability to navigate the main features, analyze information architecture, test search functionality effectiveness, and evaluate task completion flows. Collect qualitative data through verbal feedback, screen recordings, and observation notes.
- Specific protocols: Develop interview guides with specific questions related to the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows. Use standardized metrics like task success rates, time on task, and user satisfaction ratings to evaluate user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements: Ensure all research activities align with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and adhere to the specified timeline for data collection and reporting.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: Approach interviews with sensitivity to potential trauma triggers for veteran participants, providing a safe and supportive environment for sharing their experiences with the app.
- Accessibility accommodations: Offer accessibility accommodations such as screen readers, enlarged text options, and alternative input methods to accommodate diverse participant needs and ensure inclusivity in the research process.

By implementing usability interviews with a focus on the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows, this methodology allows for a comprehensive evaluation of the app's user experience from the perspective of veteran users. Through detailed data collection techniques and compliance with VA requirements, this approach ensures that research findings are relevant, meaningful, and actionable for improving the app's usability and effectiveness in delivering health and benefits services to veterans.
