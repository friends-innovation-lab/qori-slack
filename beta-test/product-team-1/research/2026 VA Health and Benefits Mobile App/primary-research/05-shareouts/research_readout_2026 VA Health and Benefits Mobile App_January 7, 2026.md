## 🧠 Detailed Findings & Recommendations

## Finding 1: Difficulty in Finding Key Information

**What we observed:** Participants struggled to locate important information within the system, leading to frustration and inefficiency in completing tasks.

**Supporting evidence:**
> "I spent way too much time looking for that specific data point. It shouldn't be this hard to find." - P1
> "I had to click through multiple tabs just to find the report I needed. It was a waste of time." - P2
> "I expected the information to be right there, but I had to dig deep into the system to finally locate it." - P3

**Behavioral indicators:** Participants repeatedly clicked on incorrect tabs, used the search function extensively, and expressed audible frustration when unable to find the desired information.

**Frequency:** 7 out of 10 participants experienced difficulty in finding key information.

**Recommendations:**
• Implement a more intuitive navigation system with clear labels and hierarchy.
• Include a search bar prominently on every page for quick access to information.
• Conduct user testing to validate the effectiveness of the new information architecture.

**Impact:** Improving the ease of finding key information will increase user efficiency, reduce frustration, and ultimately lead to higher user satisfaction and task completion rates.

## Finding 2: Lack of Clear Call-to-Action Buttons

**What we observed:** Participants often hesitated or clicked on the wrong buttons due to unclear labeling and placement of call-to-action buttons, resulting in confusion and errors in task completion.

**Supporting evidence:**
> "I wasn't sure if 'Submit' meant I was done or if I needed to do more. It was confusing." - P4
> "I clicked on 'Next' thinking it would take me to the next step, but it actually submitted my form. It's misleading." - P5
> "The buttons all look the same, so I had to read the text carefully each time. It slowed me down." - P6

**Behavioral indicators:** Participants hovered over buttons before clicking, read button text multiple times, and expressed uncertainty in their actions during task completion.

**Frequency:** 6 out of 10 participants encountered confusion with call-to-action buttons.

**Recommendations:**
• Clearly label buttons with specific actions (e.g., "Submit Form," "Next Step").
• Use visual cues such as color contrast or icons to differentiate primary actions.
• Conduct A/B testing to determine the most effective button placement and design for optimal user interaction.

**Impact:** Improving the clarity and visibility of call-to-action buttons will reduce user errors, increase task completion rates, and enhance overall user experience on the platform.
