## Interview Guide

### Opening (3-5 min)

> *Build rapport and set context*

- Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching mobile app navigation and wanted to understand the design perspective better.
- Everything you share helps us design better solutions. No wrong answers.
- Okay to record? [If applicable]

**Opener:** "To start, can you tell me about your role and how you approach design decisions for the VA mobile app?"

---

### Section: History (15 min)

> *Understanding past decisions and lessons learned*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | Walk me through the evolution of the mobile app's navigation — how did we get to where we are today? | • What were the biggest turning points? • Which decisions would you make differently now? |
| 2 | What navigation patterns or approaches have you explored or tested in the past? | • What did you learn from those experiments? • Which ones were ruled out and why? |
| 3 | Tell me about any previous research or data you've used to inform navigation decisions. | • What patterns did you notice in user behavior? • How did that research influence your design choices? |

**Listen for:** Past constraints that still apply, lessons learned from failed approaches, research gaps

---

### Section: Priorities (15 min)

> *Current focus areas and success metrics*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | What are your top design priorities for the mobile app right now? | • How do you balance competing priorities? • What's driving these priorities? |
| 2 | What would "success" look like for a navigation redesign from your perspective? | • How would you measure that success? • What metrics matter most to your team? |
| 3 | What's your hypothesis about why veterans struggle with the current navigation? | • What evidence supports that hypothesis? • How does this align with what other teams are seeing? |

**Listen for:** Alignment with business goals, measurable outcomes, design team's understanding of user pain points

---

### Section: Requirements (20 min)

> *Constraints and must-haves for any design solution*

| # | Question | Follow-ups |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1 | What design constraints do you work within — technical, brand, accessibility, or otherwise? | • Which constraints are hardest to work around? • How do these constraints impact user experience? |
| 2 | Walk me through your accessibility requirements and how they shape navigation design. | • What are the biggest accessibility challenges? • How do you test for compliance? |
| 3 | How does the VA brand and design system influence navigation decisions? | • Where do you have flexibility vs. strict requirements? • How do mobile patterns differ from web? |
| 4 | What technical limitations affect what's possible with navigation design? | • How do you work with engineering to understand feasibility? • What would you change if there were no technical constraints? |

**Listen for:** Non-negotiable constraints, areas of flexibility, collaboration challenges with other teams

---

### Closing (3-5 min)

- "What haven't I asked about that you think I should know?"
- "Who else should I talk to about navigation design decisions?"
- "Can I follow up if I have more questions?"

**Thank them for their time.**
