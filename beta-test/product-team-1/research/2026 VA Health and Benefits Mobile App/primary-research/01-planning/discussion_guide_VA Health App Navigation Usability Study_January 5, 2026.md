# 💬 Discussion Guide — VA Health App Navigation Usability Study

**Session Length:** 60 minutes

**Participants:** Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times monthly for health and benefits management. Mixed demographics including age ranges 25-65+, various service branches, and different geographic locations (urban/suburban/rural). Technology comfort levels from basic to advanced. Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies (VoiceOver, TalkBack, large text settings.

**Date Created:** January 5, 2026

**Testing URL:** https://mobile.va.gov/app-testing/navigation-study

## 🎯 Research Objectives

1. Conduct a card sorting exercise with veterans to understand how they mentally organize health and benefits information, with a goal of identifying common patterns and preferences that can inform the redesign of the VA Health and Benefits mobile app.
2. Implement a usability testing study comparing the effectiveness of bottom tabs versus a hamburger menu for navigation, measuring task completion times and success rates to determine which pattern enables faster and more efficient completion of common tasks for veterans.
3. Conduct in-depth interviews with veterans who have experienced difficulty finding key features such as prescription refills, appointment scheduling, and benefits status within the current app, aiming to identify specific barriers and pain points that can be addressed in the redesign process.
4. Conduct usability testing sessions with veterans who have accessibility needs (e.g., screen readers, large text, motor impairments) to assess how these needs impact navigation success within the app, with a focus on identifying areas for improvement to ensure equitable access for all users.
5. Utilize a combination of card sorting exercises and tree testing to determine where veterans expect to find specific functions within the app structure, aiming to align the app's information architecture with user expectations and reduce confusion and frustration during navigation.

## 🗣️ Warm-up Questions

1. Can you tell me about your experience using the VA mobile app for managing your health and benefits? 
   - *Why this matters:* This question helps to understand the participant's familiarity with the app and sets the stage for discussing their interactions with it.

2. How often do you find yourself using the search function within the app to locate specific features or information?
   - *Why this matters:* This question helps to gauge the participant's reliance on the search function, which can indicate potential issues with the app's navigation and information architecture.

3. Can you walk me through the process of completing a common task within the app, such as scheduling an appointment or checking your benefits?
   - *Why this matters:* This question allows the participant to demonstrate their navigation process and highlights any pain points they may encounter.

4. Have you ever experienced any difficulties or frustrations while using the VA mobile app? If so, can you describe a specific example?
   - *Why this matters:* This question helps to uncover specific usability barriers that the participant has encountered, providing valuable insights for improving the app's design.

5. How do you think the VA mobile app could be improved to better meet your needs as a veteran managing your health and benefits?
   - *Why this matters:* This question encourages the participant to share their thoughts on potential enhancements, guiding the conversation towards identifying areas for improvement in the app's functionality and user experience.

## 🎯 Main Research Tasks

### Task 1: Finding Prescription Refills
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You need to refill your prescription medication before it runs out.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please navigate to the section of the app where you can refill your prescription medication."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully locates the prescription refill feature within 2 minutes.

**What to Observe:**
- How easily the participant finds the prescription refill section
- Any confusion in the navigation process
- Whether the participant uses search or navigates through menus

**Probing Questions:**
- How did you decide where to look for the prescription refill feature?
- What could have made this task easier for you?

---

### Task 2: Scheduling an Appointment
**Time:** 7 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You need to schedule an appointment with your healthcare provider for a routine check-up.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please find and schedule an appointment with your healthcare provider through the app."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully schedules an appointment within 5 minutes.

**What to Observe:**
- How the participant navigates to the appointment scheduling feature
- Any challenges faced during the process
- Use of any accessibility features

**Probing Questions:**
- What was your thought process when looking for the appointment scheduling feature?
- Did you encounter any difficulties during this task?

---

### Task 3: Checking Benefits Status
**Time:** 6 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You want to check the current status of your benefits application.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please locate and check the status of your benefits application within the app."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully finds and checks the benefits status within 4 minutes.

**What to Observe:**
- How the participant navigates to the benefits status section
- Any confusion or frustration expressed during the task
- Use of assistive technologies, if applicable

**Probing Questions:**
- How did you know where to find the benefits status information?
- Were there any features or labels that were unclear to you during this task?

--- 

### Task 4: Using VoiceOver to Navigate
**Time:** 8 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You have turned on VoiceOver on your device and need to navigate through the app to find information about your recent medical history.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please use VoiceOver to navigate through the app and find information about your recent medical history."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully navigates to the medical history section using VoiceOver within 6 minutes.

**What to Observe:**
- How the participant interacts with VoiceOver to navigate
- Any challenges faced while using VoiceOver
- Effectiveness of VoiceOver in aiding navigation

**Probing Questions:**
- How comfortable are you with using VoiceOver to navigate through the app?
- Did VoiceOver help you in finding the information you needed easily?

## 💭 Post-Session Interview

1. How would you describe your overall experience using the VA Health and Benefits mobile app for navigation and completing tasks?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Can you elaborate on any specific positive or negative aspects that stood out to you?

2. Can you walk me through a task you attempted to complete on the app and any pain points you encountered along the way?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Were there any particular features or information that were difficult to locate or understand?

3. What were your expectations for how the app should function in terms of finding health and benefits information, and how did these expectations align with your actual experience?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Were there any specific discrepancies between what you expected and what you encountered in the app?

4. How do you typically organize and navigate through health and benefits information in your mind when using apps like this?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Did the app's structure and organization align with your mental model, or did you find it confusing?

5. Based on your experience with the app, do you have any suggestions for how the navigation and information architecture could be improved to better assist veterans in completing tasks efficiently?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Are there any specific features or functions you feel should be prioritized for improvement?

6. On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to use the VA Health and Benefits mobile app in its current state? What would make it a 10?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Can you explain what factors would significantly improve your likelihood to use the app and recommend it to others?
