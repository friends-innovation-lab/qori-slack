## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on restructuring the main menu, enhancing search functionality, and making changes to the information hierarchy for the mobile app redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements based on research findings, Veterans will benefit from a more user-friendly and intuitive mobile app experience. The business impact for VA includes increased user engagement, improved satisfaction, and potentially higher adoption rates of the mobile app, leading to better access to VA services for Veterans.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The Main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality of the VA Journey of Veterans Map help veterans easily navigate resources and information relevant to their specific needs and experiences.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** The Main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality enable veterans to find healthcare services, mental health resources, and wellness programs to support their overall well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** Veterans can use the Main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality to access employment resources, housing assistance, and community integration programs as they transition to civilian life.
- **Retiring:** The Main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality provide retirees with access to benefits information, financial planning resources, and retirement support services.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Inconsistent labeling or organization of resources may make it difficult for veterans to find the information they need.
- Lack of intuitive search functionality could lead to frustration and hinder veterans' ability to quickly locate relevant resources.
- Poor information architecture may result in important resources being buried or overlooked by users.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, interviews, and competitive research are ideal for evaluating the effectiveness of main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality as they provide valuable insights into user behavior, preferences, and expectations.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting 45-60 minutes, followed by in-depth interviews to gather qualitative feedback on the main navigation structure, information architecture, and search functionality.
- **Key Activities:** Observing participants interact with the website to assess ease of navigation, conducting interviews to understand user preferences and pain points, and analyzing competitors' websites to identify best practices and areas for improvement.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Utilizing task-based scenarios to evaluate the effectiveness of the main navigation structure, conducting card sorting exercises to assess information architecture, and analyzing search query data to evaluate the search functionality user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Regular check-ins with VA stakeholders to ensure alignment with project requirements and timelines.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Providing a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants, being mindful of potential triggers, and offering resources for additional support if needed.
- **Accessibility:** Ensuring all research materials and sessions are accessible to participants with diverse needs, including providing alternative formats for materials and accommodating any specific accessibility requirements.
