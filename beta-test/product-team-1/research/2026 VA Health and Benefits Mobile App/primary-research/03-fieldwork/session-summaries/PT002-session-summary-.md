# 📋 Session Summary - PT002

**Study:** 2026 VA Health and Benefits Mobile App
**Date:** 

---

## 👤 Participant Context

PT002 is a former Navy hospital corpsman with six years of military service. They have been using the VA app for about two years and primarily access healthcare services through the app. PT002 mentioned working night shifts and using an Android device, specifically a Samsung Galaxy.

---

## 🔴 Top Pain Points

**High - Message Confusion**
PT002 struggled to find the button to start a new message thread, leading to confusion about existing conversation threads and new topics.  
*Quote:* "I see there's a compose button at the bottom, let me try that..."  
*Timestamp:* 00:01:53

**High - Benefits Navigation Disaster**
PT002 faced challenges navigating the Benefits section, particularly in finding information about disability ratings and filing new claims.  
*Quote:* "This just shows '40%' but doesn't tell me what conditions that includes..."  
*Timestamp:* 00:02:54

**Medium - Search Functionality Test**
PT002 struggled to locate the search function within the app, leading to unsatisfactory search results.  
*Quote:* "It found some results but they're not very helpful..."  
*Timestamp:* 00:04:55

**Low - Clearer Labels Needed**
PT002 expressed the need for clearer labels and fewer subcategories in the app's navigation to simplify tasks.  
*Quote:* "Sometimes I feel like I'm going through a maze just to do simple things."  
*Timestamp:* 00:05:31

---

## 🟢 What Worked Well

**Prescriptions Refill Process**
PT002 found the prescription refill process straightforward and efficient, with clear information and easy access to the refill button.  
*Quote:* "It shows when I last refilled it, how many refills I have left, and the request button is obvious."  
*Timestamp:* 00:04:08

*Timestamp:* 00:02:13
