**Study:** 2026 VA Health and Benefits Mobile App

## 🔴 Top Pain Points
1. **Critical - Login Process**
The participant struggled with the login process due to the lack of clear instructions on password requirements, leading to multiple failed attempts and frustration.

2. **High - Navigation Complexity**
Participant found the navigation within the app to be confusing, especially when trying to locate specific benefits information, causing delays in accessing essential services.

3. **Medium - Error Messages**
Encountering vague error messages during form submissions caused confusion for the participant, who had difficulty understanding what went wrong and how to proceed.

## 🟡 Key Opportunities
1. **High Impact - Simplified Login Instructions**
Providing clear and concise instructions on password requirements during the login process can help users like the participant avoid frustration and multiple failed attempts.

2. **Medium Impact - Streamlined Navigation**
Improving the app's navigation by reorganizing menu items and providing clearer labels can enhance the user experience and help users locate information more efficiently.

## 💡 Key Insights
1. **Mental Model - Security Concerns**
The participant's focus on password complexity during the login process indicates a strong concern for security, suggesting that emphasizing data protection measures could increase user trust.

2. **Behavior Pattern - Trial and Error**
The participant's approach of trial and error when faced with unclear error messages highlights a common user behavior of attempting different actions to resolve issues, indicating a need for clearer feedback.

## ✅ Recommended Actions
- **Critical:** Provide clear instructions on password requirements to streamline the login process.
- **High Impact:** Revise error messages to offer specific guidance on resolving issues.
- **Quick Win:** Conduct a usability test to gather feedback on navigation improvements.
- **Quick Win:** Implement a password strength indicator to assist users in creating secure passwords.
