## 🔴 Top Pain Points

**Critical - Unlabeled Button Discovery (Minute 12)**
Participant encountered a button announced only as "Button" by VoiceOver, with no way to determine its function. This represents a fundamental barrier for blind and visually impaired users. His reaction: "That's not helpful at all" highlighted the frustration of navigating interfaces designed without accessibility consideration.

**High - Hidden Edit Button (Minute 42)**
Poor interface design affecting screen reader navigation. Edit button placed at the bottom of a long page was nearly missed entirely. Participant's systematic search revealed how screen reader users must exhaustively explore interfaces when standard patterns aren't followed. Demonstrated extra time and effort required for non-standard layouts.

**Medium - Difficulty Scheduling Appointments**
Participant struggled to find a clear way to schedule a new appointment due to unclear button labels and placement. The lack of descriptive text on buttons led to confusion and frustration during the task.

## 🟡 Key Opportunities

**High Impact - Improve Button Labeling**
The participant expressed the need for every button to have a proper label to avoid confusion and enhance accessibility. Implementing clear and descriptive button labels throughout the app will significantly improve the user experience for individuals relying on screen readers.

## 💡 Key Insights

**Accessibility Need - Importance of Proper Labels**
The participant emphasized the critical need for proper labels on all buttons to ensure a smooth and accessible user experience. Lack of descriptive text led to frustration and hindered task completion, highlighting the essential role of accessibility features in app design.
