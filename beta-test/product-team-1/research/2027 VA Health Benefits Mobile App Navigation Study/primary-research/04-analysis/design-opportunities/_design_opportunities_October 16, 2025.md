## **Problems Identified**

1. **Problem [1]: Inconsistent Interactive Element Sizing**
- **Problem Statement:** Users struggle with interactive elements like edit buttons that do not scale properly with text size settings, leading to accessibility barriers.
- **User Impact:** This inconsistency hinders users' ability to interact effectively with the app, especially for those relying on assistive technologies.
- **Evidence Strength:** Strong - supported by direct observations of users needing workarounds to locate and activate undersized elements.
- **Design Potential:** High - addressing this issue can significantly improve accessibility and user experience by ensuring consistent and scalable interactive elements.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "Edit buttons don't scale properly with text size settings" 

2. **Problem [2]: Lack of Loading Feedback for Screen Reader Users**
- **Problem Statement:** Screen reader users face challenges with extended loading times that provide no programmatic information, causing uncertainty and frustration.
- **User Impact:** Users with visual impairments or cognitive disabilities struggle to navigate effectively due to the lack of feedback during loading states.
- **Evidence Strength:** Moderate - highlighted by the absence of progress indicators for screen reader users during extended waits.
- **Design Potential:** Medium - implementing accessible loading states and progress indicators can enhance user confidence and reduce cognitive load.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "Loading states provide no programmatic information for assistive technologies"

3. **Problem [3]: Insufficient Error Guidance in Form Validation**
- **Problem Statement:** Users encounter difficulties with form validation errors not being programmatically associated with input fields, leading to confusion and usability issues.
- **User Impact:** This lack of clear error feedback affects users' ability to correct mistakes efficiently, impacting task completion and user satisfaction.
- **Evidence Strength:** Strong - evidenced by users struggling with error messaging that is not properly linked to form elements.
- **Design Potential:** High - improving error guidance in form validation can enhance user understanding, reduce errors, and improve overall usability.
- **Supporting Evidence:** "Form validation errors not programmatically associated with input fields"

## **How Might We Opportunities**

**Opportunity 1: Confusing Navigation**
**Problem:** Users struggle to navigate the VA Health Benefits Mobile App due to confusing menu structures and unclear labels.
**HMW:** How might we simplify the app navigation to make it intuitive for users of all tech literacy levels?
**Why This Matters:** Clear navigation is essential for users to access important health benefits information efficiently, reducing frustration and increasing engagement with the app.
**Design Challenge:** Design a new navigation system that is user-friendly and intuitive, ensuring easy access to key features and information.
**Success Vision:** Users can easily find the information they need within a few clicks, leading to increased app usage and satisfaction.
**Creative Inspiration:** Think about how physical spaces like airports or museums use clear signage and wayfinding to guide visitors effectively.
**Supporting Quote:** "I often get lost in the app trying to find what I need. It's frustrating and makes me not want to use it." 

**Opportunity 2: Lack of Personalization**
**Problem:** The VA Health Benefits Mobile App does not provide personalized recommendations or content based on individual user needs and preferences.
**HMW:** How might we tailor the app experience to provide personalized recommendations and resources for each user's unique health benefits needs?
**Why This Matters:** Personalization can enhance user engagement, satisfaction, and outcomes by delivering relevant information and support tailored to each individual.
**Design Challenge:** Develop a personalized content recommendation system that takes into account users' health benefits history, preferences, and goals.
**Success Vision:** Users receive personalized recommendations for relevant health benefits resources and services, leading to improved health outcomes and user satisfaction.
**Creative Inspiration:** Consider how streaming platforms like Netflix or Spotify use algorithms to recommend content based on user behavior and preferences.
**Supporting Quote:** "I wish the app could recommend resources or services that are specifically relevant to my health needs. It would make it more valuable to me." 

**Opportunity 3: Limited Accessibility Features**
**Problem:** The VA Health Benefits Mobile App lacks robust accessibility features, making it difficult for users with disabilities to navigate and use the app effectively.
**HMW:** How might we enhance the app's accessibility features to ensure all users, including those with disabilities, can access and utilize health benefits information easily?
**Why This Matters:** Improving accessibility ensures inclusivity and equal access to health benefits information for all users, regardless of their abilities.
**Design Challenge:** Implement accessibility features such as screen reader compatibility, voice commands, and customizable text sizes to enhance usability for users with disabilities.
**Success Vision:** Users with disabilities can navigate and use the app independently, improving their overall experience and access to essential health benefits information.
**Creative Inspiration:** Look at how inclusive design principles are applied in products like Microsoft's Xbox Adaptive Controller to make technology more accessible for users with disabilities.
**Supporting Quote:** "As a visually impaired user, I struggle to use the app effectively. More accessibility features would make a huge difference for me."
