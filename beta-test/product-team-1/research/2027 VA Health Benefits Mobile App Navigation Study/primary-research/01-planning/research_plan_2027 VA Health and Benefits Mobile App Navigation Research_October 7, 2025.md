## 🔎 Background

This research will provide valuable insights to the product team regarding the prioritization of navigation improvements for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. Specifically, it will inform decisions on main menu restructuring, search enhancement, and information hierarchy changes, as well as the implementation of progressive disclosure, dashboard layout redesign, and improvement of core feature discoverability.

By optimizing the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features, this research will ultimately benefit Veterans by providing them with a more user-friendly and efficient mobile app experience. This will lead to increased user satisfaction, improved access to essential services, and a more seamless interaction with VA resources.

From a business standpoint, implementing the findings from this research will result in a more intuitive and effective mobile app design, potentially leading to higher user engagement, increased usage of VA services, and overall improved operational efficiency for the VA.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App plays a crucial role in helping veterans navigate their health and benefits resources, providing easy access to important information and services.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to schedule appointments, refill prescriptions, and access mental health resources to take care of their physical and mental well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** The app can assist veterans in finding housing resources, employment opportunities, and educational benefits as they transition to civilian life.
- **Retiring:** Veterans can use the app to access information on retirement benefits, financial planning, and long-term care options as they enter retirement.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App could include difficulty navigating the app, locating specific information, or accessing certain features related to health and benefits. Improving the user experience and streamlining the app's functionality could help alleviate these pain points for veterans.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing 45% of veterans to abandon mobile app tasks, leading to an improvement in user satisfaction rating to at least 7/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to identify specific pain points in the navigation and information architecture that contribute to task abandonment among veterans.
- Implement card sorting exercises to reorganize the main navigation structure and information architecture based on user preferences and needs.
- Conduct competitive analysis to benchmark the VA Mobile App against similar apps in terms of navigation, information architecture, and user satisfaction.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research are specifically right for the VA Mobile App as they provide valuable insights into how users interact with the app, how they navigate through the main features, and how they complete tasks related to health and benefits. These methods will help identify pain points, areas for improvement, and best practices in the design of the app.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration: Conducting usability testing sessions with real users for 1-2 hours each, card sorting activities to understand how users categorize information, and competitive research to benchmark against other similar apps.
- Key activities and data collection techniques: Observing users as they navigate the main features, analyze the information architecture, test the search functionality, and complete tasks related to health and benefits. Collecting quantitative and qualitative data on user interactions, feedback, and preferences.
- Specific protocols: Using task scenarios to evaluate task completion flows, conducting card sorting exercises to inform information architecture, and comparing the app's features with competitors to identify strengths and weaknesses.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring that all research activities align with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline for approval and implementation.
- Trauma-informed research considerations: Being sensitive to the needs of veteran participants who may have experienced trauma and ensuring that research activities are conducted in a supportive and understanding manner.
- Accessibility accommodations: Providing accommodations for diverse participant needs, such as screen readers, alternative text, and other accessibility features to ensure all participants can fully engage in the research.

By implementing usability testing, card sorting, and competitive research specifically for the VA Mobile App, we can gather valuable insights into the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features. These methodologies will help us identify areas for improvement, optimize the user experience, and ensure that the app meets the needs of its veteran users effectively.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios:
1. Find information on how to schedule a medical appointment using the VA Mobile App.
2. Navigate to the benefits section and locate details about eligibility for disability compensation.
3. Use the search functionality to find nearby VA medical facilities.
4. Complete the process of requesting prescription refills through the app.

- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate the app and complete tasks.

- Data collection: Set up screen recording software to capture participant interactions with the app. Use an observation notes template to record key observations and issues. Time each task completion to measure efficiency.

- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience using the app, difficulties encountered, and suggestions for improvement related to the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different sections and features of the VA Mobile App related to health and benefits. Include cards for main navigation items, search functionality, and task completion flows.

- Sorting methodology: Conduct open card sorting sessions where participants group cards based on their understanding of the app's structure. Use hybrid approaches if needed to gather more specific insights.

- Analysis procedures: Analyze the results using statistical measures to identify common patterns and groupings. Use dendrograms and similarity matrices to visualize relationships between cards.

- Results interpretation: Translate the card sorting findings into recommendations for improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows of the app.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Evaluate competitor apps using established heuristics and compare features to those of the VA Mobile App. Create matrices to highlight strengths and weaknesses in navigation, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows.

- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of key features and interactions in competitor apps. Make notes on usability issues and innovative design elements.

- Analysis criteria: Identify common trends in competitor apps and extract best practices that could be applied to the VA Mobile App. Highlight areas where the VA app excels and where improvements can be made.

- Synthesis approach: Translate competitive insights into actionable recommendations for enhancing the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows of the VA Mobile App.
