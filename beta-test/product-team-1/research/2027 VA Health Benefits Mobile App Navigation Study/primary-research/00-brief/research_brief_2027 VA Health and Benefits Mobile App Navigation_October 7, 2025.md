## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight issues such as difficulty in finding necessary features and confusing navigation. With 78% of veteran interactions occurring on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving veteran access to essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights to inform the redesign of the mobile app scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential services, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is impacting VA operations by increasing call volumes to customer service for assistance, resulting in inefficiencies and higher costs.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are preventing veterans from efficiently accessing the services they need, leading to decreased user satisfaction and potentially hindering their overall healthcare experience.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter confusion in the main menu structure and how it affects their ability to navigate the app effectively.
- Which key features veterans prioritize in the app and how easily they can discover and utilize them.
- Why the current navigation patterns in different app sections are inconsistent and how they impact user experience.
- How the search functionality in the app is failing to meet veterans' needs and what improvements can be made to provide more relevant results.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users are confused by the main menu structure due to overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile.
- Key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling are difficult to discover within the app.
- Navigation patterns vary inconsistently between different sections of the app.
- The search functionality often returns irrelevant or outdated results, impacting user experience.
- The information hierarchy does not align with veteran mental models, causing confusion.
- Accessibility issues exist with touch targets and screen reader navigation, hindering user interaction.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health and Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans encounter inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to efficiently complete tasks such as accessing health records or managing appointments?
4. What causes veterans to experience accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation within the app, and how does this impact their ability to effectively use the mobile platform for their healthcare needs?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
- "Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories leads to decreased user engagement with key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling."
- "Veterans with visual impairments face more accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation, resulting in lower app usage."
- "Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections lead to increased user frustration and longer task completion times."

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

| 🔬 **Aspect** | 📝 **Details** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Method** | This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 8 veterans who regularly use the mobile app. The focus will be on evaluating the current information architecture and identifying optimal navigation patterns, ensuring representation across age groups and assistive technology users. Testing will be conducted on both iOS and Android platforms to account for platform differences, in line with VA research standards. |
| **Rationale** | The refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives because it allows for in-depth exploration of consumer behavior and preferences, providing valuable insights into the factors influencing purchasing decisions. By combining qualitative and quantitative methods, it ensures a comprehensive understanding of both attitudinal and behavioral aspects within the scope and constraints of the study. |
