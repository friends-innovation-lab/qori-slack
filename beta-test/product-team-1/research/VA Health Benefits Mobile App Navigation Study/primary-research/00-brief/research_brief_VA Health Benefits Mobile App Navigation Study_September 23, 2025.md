# 📄 Research Brief – VA Health Benefits Mobile App Navigation Study

> **📦 Project Area:** Digital Services &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **👩🏽‍🔬 Prepared by:** Taylor Kim, Mobile UX Researcher &nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp; **🗓️ Date:** September 23, 2025

## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile app has a user satisfaction rating of 4.2 out of 10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks midway. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, indicating poor information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight difficulties in finding necessary information and navigating the app. With 78% of veteran interactions occurring on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for providing veterans with essential services. Leadership requires actionable insights for the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026.

**Stakeholder Concerns:**
- Inefficient communication between teams is causing delays in project completion and impacting overall productivity
- Users are struggling to access important information due to a lack of centralized data storage, leading to errors and duplication of work
- Stakeholders want to address the high turnover rate of employees by improving their overall job satisfaction and work environment

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter barriers in accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app.
- Which features within the app are most important to veterans during their initial onboarding and feature discovery process.
- Why veterans struggle with appointment scheduling and modification within the app.
- How veterans navigate the main dashboard and complete tasks efficiently within the app.

**User journey in scope:**
1. Sign up for the app and complete onboarding process
2. Explore initial features and functionalities of the app
3. Navigate the main dashboard to access different sections and complete tasks
4. Access health records and manage prescriptions through designated section
5. Schedule appointments and make modifications as needed
6. Check benefits status and submit required documents
7. Manage profile information and adjust notification settings
8. Conduct searches within the app and interact with search results

---

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Complex main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health, Benefits, Profile) leading to user confusion and difficulty finding desired information
- Lack of visibility for key features such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, hindering user engagement and task completion
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app causing user disorientation and frustration
- Search functionality returning irrelevant or outdated results, impacting user ability to quickly locate information
- Information hierarchy not aligned with veteran mental models, resulting in user difficulty in understanding and navigating the app
- Accessibility issues with touch targets and screen reader navigation impeding users with disabilities from effectively using the app

---

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

**Primary questions:**
1. How do veterans currently navigate the app onboarding process and discover initial features?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily accessing their health records and managing prescriptions within the app?
3. Why do veterans encounter difficulties when scheduling and modifying appointments through the app?
4. What causes veterans to struggle with checking benefits status and submitting documents via the app?

**Team hypotheses:**
- Limited access to affordable transportation increases the likelihood of missed medical appointments among low-income patients.
- Lack of communication between healthcare providers and caregivers leads to increased medication errors in elderly patients.
- Inadequate training for teachers on mental health awareness contributes to higher rates of student mental health issues going undetected.

---

## 🧭 Proposed Research Approach

**High-level approach:** This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, utilizing card sorting exercises and moderated mobile usability testing with a sample of 16 veterans to assess the current information architecture and identify optimal navigation patterns of the mobile app. The sessions will include a diverse representation of age groups and assistive technology users, covering both iOS and Android platforms to account for platform differences.

**Reasoning:** The refined research approach makes sense for the research objectives as it allows for a deeper understanding of consumer behavior and preferences through in-depth interviews and observations. This method will provide valuable behavioral insights that align with the research objectives while fitting within the scope and constraints of the study.
