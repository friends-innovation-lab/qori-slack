## 🔎 Background

This research will inform the product team's decision on the main navigation structure for the VA Mobile App redesign in Q1 2026. By prioritizing navigation improvements such as main menu restructuring and search enhancements, the app will become more user-friendly and intuitive for Veterans. This will ultimately lead to increased user satisfaction, engagement, and overall effectiveness of the VA Mobile App, resulting in improved access to important resources and services for Veterans.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App main navigation structure plays a crucial role in helping veterans navigate the various resources and services available to them through the VA.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Veterans can use the app to access healthcare services, mental health resources, and wellness programs to take care of their physical and mental well-being.
- **Putting down roots:** The app can assist veterans in finding housing assistance, employment resources, and educational opportunities as they transition to civilian life.
- **Retiring:** Veterans can use the app to access information on retirement benefits, financial planning, and long-term care options as they enter retirement.

**Critical Pain Points:** Some potential pain points for the VA Mobile App main navigation structure may include difficulty finding specific resources, confusing layout or organization, and lack of user-friendly features for veterans with limited technology experience.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** 
Identify and address the primary navigation and information architecture barriers that lead to a 45% abandonment rate among veterans using the mobile app, ultimately increasing user satisfaction to a target of 7/10.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to pinpoint specific pain points in the navigation and information architecture that contribute to user abandonment.
- Analyze user feedback and behavior data to determine which features or sections of the app are causing dissatisfaction and frustration among veterans.
- Develop a revised navigation structure based on user feedback and best practices in information architecture to improve user experience and satisfaction.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for the VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure as it will allow us to understand how easily users can navigate through the app to access key features and information. This will help us identify any pain points or areas for improvement in the main navigation structure, ultimately leading to a more user-friendly and efficient app for veterans.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Session format and duration appropriate for usability: Conducting usability testing sessions with individual participants to observe their interactions with the main navigation structure. Sessions will typically last between 30-60 minutes to allow for thorough exploration and feedback.
- Key activities and data collection techniques specific to VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure: Participants will be asked to complete specific tasks related to navigating through the app, such as finding a specific feature or accessing important information. Data will be collected through observation, task completion success rates, and participant feedback.
- Specific protocols for evaluating VA Mobile App - specifically the main navigation structure user experience: Utilizing standardized usability metrics such as task success rate, time on task, and user satisfaction ratings to evaluate the effectiveness of the main navigation structure.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline: Ensuring that all research activities align with the VA's Collaboration Cycle requirements and timeline for research projects.
- Trauma-informed research considerations for veteran participants: Being mindful of potential triggers or sensitivities that veteran participants may have and ensuring a safe and supportive research environment.
- Accessibility accommodations for diverse participant needs: Providing accommodations such as screen readers, large font sizes, or alternative input methods to ensure that all participants, including those with disabilities, can effectively participate in the usability research.

This methodology will allow us to gather valuable insights into how veterans interact with the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App, helping us identify areas for improvement and ultimately enhance the user experience for our target audience.

**For Usability Testing:**
- Task scenarios: 
1. Find and access your medical records on the VA Mobile App.
2. Locate and schedule an appointment with a healthcare provider using the main navigation.
3. Update your personal information in the app settings.

- Think-aloud protocol: Instruct participants to verbalize their thoughts and actions as they navigate through the main menu and submenus of the VA Mobile App.

- Data collection: Set up screen recording software to capture participants' interactions with the app. Use an observation notes template to record usability issues and insights. Time each task completion to measure efficiency.

- Post-session interviews: Ask participants about their overall experience with the main navigation structure, any difficulties encountered, and suggestions for improvement.

**For User Interviews:**
- Interview guide structure: Begin with general questions about the participant's experience with mobile apps and the VA system. Dive into specific topics related to the main navigation structure, such as ease of use and organization.

- Question types: Include questions about how participants typically navigate through apps, their preferences for menu layouts, and any specific features they find important in the VA Mobile App.

- Recording protocols: Obtain consent for audio/video recording of the interview. Ensure a quiet and distraction-free environment for the interview.

- Analysis approach: Use thematic analysis to identify common themes and patterns in participants' responses. Create an affinity map to visualize connections between different insights. Develop a coding framework to categorize interview data.

**For Card Sorting:**
- Card preparation: Create cards representing different sections and features of the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Include labels such as "Appointments," "Medical Records," and "Settings."

- Sorting methodology: Conduct an open card sort where participants organize the cards into categories that make sense to them. Use a hybrid approach by providing pre-defined categories for participants to sort the cards into.

- Analysis procedures: Use statistical measures such as cluster analysis to identify patterns in how participants grouped the cards. Create dendrograms and similarity matrices to visualize relationships between different sections.

- Results interpretation: Translate the card sorting findings into recommendations for restructuring the main navigation of the VA Mobile App. Identify common groupings and preferences among participants.

**For Survey Research:**
- Question design: Include Likert scale questions to measure satisfaction with the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Validate questions through pilot testing with a small group of users.

- Sampling strategy: Target VA Mobile App users who frequently use the main navigation features. Calculate the sample size based on the desired level of confidence and margin of error.

- Distribution methods: Administer the survey through the VA Mobile App itself or via email to a representative sample of users. Send reminders to increase response rates.

- Statistical analysis: Use descriptive statistics to summarize survey responses. Conduct significance testing to compare satisfaction levels between different user groups. Follow reporting standards for presenting survey findings.

**For Concept Testing:**
- Concept presentation: Show participants a prototype of the revised main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Provide scenarios for them to complete tasks such as finding information or scheduling appointments.

- Testing protocol: Have participants navigate through the prototype and provide feedback on the usability and clarity of the main navigation. Ask them to rank different design options based on their preferences.

- Measurement criteria: Evaluate usability metrics such as task completion time and error rates. Test comprehension by asking participants to explain how they would perform specific actions. Assess the appeal of the new design through user feedback.

- Iteration framework: Incorporate feedback from concept testing into refining the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Make iterative improvements based on user preferences and usability insights.

**For Competitive Analysis:**
- Evaluation framework: Use established usability heuristics to evaluate the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Create feature comparison matrices to compare navigation options with those of competitors.

- Documentation standards: Take screenshots of the main navigation screens of the VA Mobile App and competitors for reference. Document interaction flows and usability notes during the evaluation process.

- Analysis criteria: Identify strengths and weaknesses in the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App compared to competitors. Extract best practices from competitors' navigation designs for potential implementation.

- Synthesis approach: Translate insights from the competitive analysis into actionable recommendations for improving the main navigation structure of the VA Mobile App. Prioritize changes based on identified weaknesses and opportunities for enhancement.
