# 🧭 Research Plan – VA Health Benefits Mobile App Navigation Study

**📦 Product/Feature Area:** "VA Mobile App - A user-friendly platform for Veterans to easily navigate health and benefits features. This tool ensures efficient access to critical resources within the VA ecosystem, improving overall user experience and task completion."

**🏛️ Team/Office:** Primary Team: VA Mobile Development Team
Collaborating Office: Digital Experience Office
Support: Veterans Health Administration (VHA) Digital Services
Technical Partner: VA.gov Platform Team
Accessibility Consultant: Digital Accessibility Specialist
Analytics Support: VA Mobile Analytics Team  
**👩🏽‍🔬 Lead Researcher:** Taylor Kim, Mobile UX Researcher  
**🗓️ Date:** September 23, 2025

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions regarding the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows across health and benefits features in the VA Mobile App. The outcome will directly benefit Veterans by improving their ability to access essential services efficiently. This strategic shift will enhance user experience, ensuring Veterans can easily navigate and utilize key features to meet their needs effectively.

---

## 🎯 OCTO Priorities

**Primary OCTO Priority:** Expand the flagship mobile app

**Explanation:** This priority directly aligns with improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows for Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app.

**Additional Priorities (if applicable):**
- **Improve the Veteran login experience:** Enhancing the login experience will streamline access to the app for frequent users.
- **Fast-track disability claims, starting with PACT Act claims:** Prioritizing disability claims will improve task completion flows for users seeking benefits through the app.

---

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** The VA Mobile App serves as a crucial tool for Veterans/VA Employees/External Stakeholders to access health and benefits features seamlessly within their respective life stages/work phases/service phases.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** Navigating to health resources and benefits information
- **Daily operations:** Using search functionality to find relevant health services
- **Client acquisition:** Accessing VA system for benefits eligibility

**Critical Pain Points (if applicable):** Confusing main navigation structure leading to difficulty in finding specific health and benefits information.

---

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify and address the main navigation and information architecture issues causing high user abandonment rates and low satisfaction scores within the VA Mobile App.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most common pain points in task completion flows related to health and benefits features through usability testing.
- Improve user engagement and satisfaction by optimizing the search functionality based on card sorting results.
- Enhance the overall user experience by benchmarking against competitors and identifying areas for improvement in the VA Mobile App.

---

## ✅ Success Criteria & Expected Deliverables

**Success Criteria:**
1. **Decision Support:** By the end of the research period, provide actionable recommendations for improving the main navigation structure, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows in the VA Mobile App. These recommendations should be implemented within 3 months of the research completion to address the identified barriers that cause 45% of veterans to abandon tasks and improve the current user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10.

2. **Quality Threshold:** Ensure that research findings are robust and actionable, with clear insights into the root causes behind the navigation and information architecture barriers. The recommendations should be based on user feedback and validated through usability testing and card sorting exercises. Stakeholders should be confident in the validity and reliability of the research findings to make informed decisions for improving the VA Mobile App.

3. **Timeline Success:** Present preliminary findings and recommendations to stakeholders within 2 weeks of completing the research, allowing for timely decision-making and implementation planning. Finalize the research report and recommendations within 4 weeks of completion, with a detailed timeline for implementing the proposed changes in the VA Mobile App. Stakeholders should review and approve the recommendations within 6 weeks of the research completion to ensure timely action.

4. **Impact Measurement:** Measure the impact of the implemented changes on user satisfaction and task completion rates within 3 months of implementation. Conduct follow-up usability testing and user feedback surveys to assess the effectiveness of the improvements in addressing the identified barriers and increasing user satisfaction. Aim to achieve a noticeable improvement in the user satisfaction rating and a decrease in task abandonment rates to demonstrate the success of the research and recommendations.

**Key Outputs:**
**Primary Deliverables:**
- Usability report highlighting primary navigation and information architecture barriers causing task abandonment - delivered within 2 days of research completion
- Card sorting analysis identifying optimal main navigation structure - delivered within 1 day of research completion
- Competitive analysis report on search functionality and task completion flows across health and benefits features - delivered within 2 days of research completion

**Supporting Materials:**
- User journey map illustrating key pain points and opportunities for improvement in task completion flows - delivered within 3 days of research completion
- Recommendations presentation outlining actionable steps to address root causes of low user satisfaction rating - delivered within 2 days of research completion

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability testing, card sorting, and competitive analysis are optimal for identifying navigation and information architecture barriers in the VA mobile app, providing concrete insights into user satisfaction and task completion issues.

**Implementation Approach:**
- Hybrid sessions for card sorting and task-based usability testing, focusing on main navigation, information architecture, search functionality, and task completion flows
- Activities include card sorting exercises, task-based scenarios, competitive analysis, and user feedback collection
- Protocols will be tailored to evaluate specific features of the VA Mobile App, ensuring a comprehensive assessment of user experience and accessibility considerations

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- Collaboration Cycle requirements will be met through regular check-ins with stakeholders and timely reporting
- Trauma-informed research considerations will be integrated into participant recruitment and session facilitation
- Accessibility accommodations will be provided for participants with visual/motor accessibility needs, ensuring inclusivity in the research process.

## 🧲 Recruitment Plan

**🎯 Target Participants:** Primary: Veterans who actively use the VA mobile app at least 2-3 times per month
Secondary: Veterans who have downloaded but struggled to complete tasks in the mobile app
Inclusion criteria: Current smartphone users (iOS/Android), completed at least one transaction via VA mobile app
Exclusion criteria: VA employees, mobile app developers, UX professionals
Accessibility focus: Minimum 50% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented visual/motor accessibility needs  

**🧍 Participant Count:** Card Sorting: 8 participants (45-minute hybrid sessions)
Mobile Usability Testing: 8 participants (60-minute task-based sessions on personal devices)

**Secondary targets:**
- VA Mobile App Usage Patterns: 4 frequent users (3+ times per week), 4 infrequent users (1-2 times per month)

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

**Recruitment challenges:** Difficulty in finding qualified participants who meet the inclusion criteria and accessibility focus
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Partner with VA hospitals and clinics to recruit participants, offer incentives for participation, and utilize social media platforms to reach a wider audience of potential participants.

**Execution issues:** Potential problems with the implementation of usability testing, card sorting, and competitive analysis methodologies
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct pilot testing with a small group of participants to identify and address any issues with the research methodologies before the main study, provide clear instructions and guidance to participants, and have a backup plan in case of technical difficulties.

**Timeline risks:** Delays that may impact the standard timeline for the research project
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project plan with clear milestones and deadlines, regularly monitor progress against the plan, and proactively address any potential delays by reallocating resources or adjusting the timeline as needed.

**Quality concerns:** Risks related to data validity and stakeholder satisfaction
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation processes, ensure that all research findings are accurately documented and reported, and regularly communicate with stakeholders to gather feedback and address any concerns promptly.
