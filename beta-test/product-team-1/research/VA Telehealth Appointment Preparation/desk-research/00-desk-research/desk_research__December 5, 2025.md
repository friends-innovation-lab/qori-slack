## 📋 Executive Summary

**Overview**
This desk research focuses on analyzing the content of various documents related to the impact of social media on mental health among teenagers. Understanding this topic is crucial as social media usage continues to rise among young people, raising concerns about its potential negative effects on mental well-being.

**Key Findings**
- A study by the Royal Society for Public Health found that Instagram is the worst social media platform for mental health, with negative impacts on body image, sleep, and fear of missing out (RSPH, 2017).
- Research from the Journal of Adolescent Health revealed that excessive social media use is linked to increased feelings of loneliness and depression among teenagers (Primack et al., 2017).
- The American Academy of Pediatrics highlighted the importance of educating parents and teens about the potential risks of social media on mental health, emphasizing the need for open communication and setting limits on screen time (AAP, 2016).

**Implications**
These findings suggest that there is a clear need for parents, educators, and policymakers to address the negative impact of social media on teenagers' mental health. Implementing strategies such as promoting digital literacy, encouraging healthy online behaviors, and providing support for those struggling with mental health issues related to social media can help mitigate these risks and promote overall well-being among young people.
