## 🎯 Business Context

Telehealth appointments have become a significant component of VA care for Veterans. However, many Veterans face uncertainty and challenges with pre-appointment tasks such as setting up devices, conducting connection tests, and navigating permissions. Support teams have identified recurring issues stemming from unclear instructions, mismatched captions, and confusion regarding whether to join appointments through a browser or app. These challenges highlight the need for improved guidance and support for Veterans engaging in telehealth services within the VA system.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages is causing confusion and uncertainty for Veterans, leading to potential missed appointments or delays in care.
- Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore are preventing them from properly preparing for their telehealth appointments, potentially leading to misunderstandings or technical difficulties during the session.
- Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone are causing frustration and delays for Veterans trying to join their appointments, impacting the efficiency and effectiveness of VA telehealth operations.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most in locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages.
- Which prep instructions are most commonly ignored or skimmed by Veterans.
- Why Veterans experience delays in comprehension due to caption issues during video-based guidance.
- How Veterans currently navigate through permission prompts and device compatibility expectations during appointment setup.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users struggle to locate appointment instructions across various communication channels (email, SMS, portal messages), leading to confusion and missed appointments.
- Veterans often overlook or skim through lengthy and complex preparation instructions, resulting in potential errors or missed information.
- Delays in captions during video-based guidance hinder user comprehension and engagement with the content.
- Unclear expectations regarding device and browser compatibility create frustration and uncertainty for users trying to access the platform.
- Confusing and inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone usage create barriers for users trying to participate in video-based interactions.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate through email/SMS reminders and appointment detail pages for their telehealth appointments?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from successfully completing pre-visit setup steps such as camera, microphone, and internet tests?
3. Why do veterans experience delays and reduced comprehension during video-based guidance due to caption delays?
4. What causes confusion and frustration among veterans when encountering unclear device and browser compatibility expectations and permission prompts for camera and microphone during telehealth appointments?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages increases the likelihood of missed appointments among Veterans.
2. Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore lead to decreased engagement and understanding during telehealth appointments.
3. Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone result in frustration and technical difficulties for Veterans during telehealth appointments.
