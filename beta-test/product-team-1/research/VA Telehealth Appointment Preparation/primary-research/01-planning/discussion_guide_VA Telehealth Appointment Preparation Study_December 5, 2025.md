# 💬 Discussion Guide — VA Telehealth Appointment Preparation Study

**Session Length:** 60 minutes

**Participants:** Veterans who completed at least one VA telehealth visit in the last year. Include a mix of ages, service histories, and device preferences. Half of participants should rely on accessibility tools such as screen readers, magnification, captions, or adaptive input. Include at least two Veterans who receive help from a caregiver during telehealth visits. Recruit across time zones to capture scheduling differences and morning versus afternoon appointment behaviors.

**Date Created:** December 5, 2025

**Testing URL:** https://telehealth.va.gov/appointment-join-test

## 🎯 Research Objectives

1. Identify the specific steps in the telehealth appointment preparation process that Veterans find unclear or overwhelming, and determine the frequency with which these issues occur.
2. Determine the factors that influence Veterans' decision-making process when choosing whether to join their telehealth appointment from a phone, laptop, or tablet, and assess the impact of these choices on their overall experience.
3. Analyze the information that Veterans consider most important right before their telehealth appointment begins, and evaluate how this information influences their level of preparedness and engagement.
4. Investigate when and where Veterans typically seek out instructions for their telehealth appointment, and assess whether the availability and accessibility of these instructions impact their ability to successfully join the appointment.
5. Examine how the use of assistive technologies affects Veterans' experience when joining their telehealth appointment, and identify any barriers or challenges that may arise as a result of using these technologies.

## 🗣️ Warm-up Questions

1. Can you tell me about your experience with VA telehealth appointments and what led you to start using this service?
   - *Why this matters:* Understanding the motivations and experiences of participants can provide valuable context for their telehealth journey.

2. How do you typically prepare for a telehealth appointment, especially in terms of setting up your device and ensuring a smooth connection?
   - *Why this matters:* Exploring participants' preparation routines can uncover common pain points and areas where guidance may be lacking.

3. Can you walk me through the steps you take to join a VA telehealth appointment, from receiving the invitation to connecting with your healthcare provider?
   - *Why this matters:* Mapping out the participant's process can help identify specific areas where confusion or difficulties arise.

4. Have you encountered any challenges or barriers when using telehealth services, particularly in terms of device accessibility or understanding the instructions provided?
   - *Why this matters:* Understanding the specific challenges faced by participants using accessibility tools can inform improvements in the telehealth experience for all Veterans.

5. How does having a caregiver assist you during telehealth visits impact your overall experience and comfort level with the technology?
   - *Why this matters:* Exploring the role of caregivers in telehealth appointments can shed light on the support systems that Veterans rely on and potential areas for caregiver involvement in the redesign process.

## 🎯 Main Research Tasks

### Task 1: Device Selection
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You have a VA telehealth appointment scheduled for tomorrow morning. You need to decide which device to use for the video visit.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please think about your upcoming telehealth appointment and choose which device you would like to use for the video visit."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant selects a device (phone, laptop, or tablet) and explains their reasoning behind the choice.

**What to Observe:**
- Decision-making process for selecting a device
- Factors influencing their choice (e.g., convenience, familiarity, screen size)
- Any hesitation or uncertainty in making the decision

**Probing Questions:**
- Why did you choose that particular device?
- What factors did you consider when deciding on the device?

---

### Task 2: Finding Instructions
**Time:** 7 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You have just received a reminder for your VA telehealth appointment later today. You need to find the instructions on how to join the video visit.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please locate the instructions on how to join your telehealth appointment. You can use any resources you would normally use, such as emails, text messages, or the VA website."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully locates the instructions on how to join the telehealth appointment.
- Participant explains where they found the instructions and how they accessed them.

**What to Observe:**
- Search behavior for finding instructions
- Ease of access to the instructions
- Any confusion or frustration during the process

**Probing Questions:**
- Where did you find the instructions?
- Was it easy for you to locate the instructions? 

---

### Task 3: Joining the Video Visit
**Time:** 10 minutes

**Scenario:** 
It's time for your VA telehealth appointment. You need to join the video visit and connect with your healthcare provider.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please go through the steps to join your telehealth appointment. Pretend that your healthcare provider is waiting for you on the other end."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant successfully joins the video visit with their healthcare provider.
- Participant follows the correct steps to connect to the appointment.

**What to Observe:**
- Ability to navigate the joining process
- Any errors or difficulties encountered during the connection
- Confidence in using the technology to join the appointment

**Probing Questions:**
- Did you encounter any challenges while joining the video visit?
- How did you feel about the process of connecting to the appointment?

--- 

### Task 4: Assistance from Caregiver
**Time:** 8 minutes

**Scenario:** 
You have a VA telehealth appointment coming up, and your caregiver will be helping you with the setup. Your caregiver is not familiar with the process and needs guidance.

**Instruction to Participant:**
"Please guide your caregiver through the steps to set up and join your telehealth appointment. Pretend that your caregiver is assisting you with the process."

**Success Criteria:**
- Participant effectively guides their caregiver through the setup and joining process.
- Participant communicates clearly and provides necessary information to their caregiver.

**What to Observe:**
- Communication and collaboration between participant and caregiver
- Clarity of instructions provided by the participant
- Any challenges faced during the guidance process

**Probing Questions:**
- How did you decide what information to share with your caregiver?
- Did you encounter any difficulties while guiding your caregiver through the setup process?

## 💭 Post-Session Interview

1. Can you walk me through your experience preparing for your VA telehealth appointment? What steps did you take and how did you feel during the process?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Were there any specific moments that stood out to you as particularly challenging or confusing?

2. How did you decide whether to join your telehealth appointment from a phone, laptop, or tablet? What factors influenced your decision?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Did you encounter any difficulties with the device you chose to use?

3. What information did you find most important to have right before your telehealth appointment began? Were there any key pieces of information that you felt were missing or unclear?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* How did the availability of information impact your overall experience?

4. When did you first look for instructions on how to join your telehealth appointment, and where did you expect to find them? Did you encounter any challenges in accessing the instructions?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* How do you think the location and clarity of instructions could be improved?

5. How did any assistive technologies you used impact your experience when joining your telehealth appointment? Did they make the process easier or more difficult for you?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* Were there any specific features of the assistive technologies that you found particularly helpful or hindering?

6. What typically triggers you to make a last-minute call to support lines for assistance with joining your telehealth appointment? Are there common issues that lead to this need for additional help?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* How do you think the support process could be improved to minimize the need for last-minute calls?

7. Are there any parts of the appointment workflow that you tend to skip, misunderstand, or repeat because you didn't trust the initial instructions given? What factors contribute to this lack of trust?
   - *Follow-up if needed:* How do you think the clarity and reliability of instructions could be enhanced to address this issue?

End with: "On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to use this VA telehealth service again in the future? What improvements would make it a 10?"
