# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect — Allows Veterans to connect with their healthcare providers through secure video appointments on their mobile devices or desktop computers, improving access to care and reducing the need for in-person visits.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digital HCD Team

🗓️ **Date:** December 5, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop platforms. By guiding Veterans through appointment scheduling, providing timely technical checks and instructions, and coordinating messages effectively, the goal is to make the telehealth process easier and more user-friendly for Veterans. This improved experience will benefit Veterans by reducing confusion and frustration, ultimately leading to increased utilization of telehealth services. From a business perspective, simplifying the telehealth experience can result in higher patient satisfaction, increased appointment attendance, and overall improved efficiency for the VA.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in providing convenient and accessible healthcare services to veterans throughout their journey.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop enable veterans to easily connect with healthcare providers, attend virtual appointments, and manage their health conditions from the comfort of their own homes.
- **Putting down roots:** These telehealth services support veterans as they transition into civilian life by ensuring continuity of care, access to mental health services, and assistance in navigating the healthcare system.
- **Retiring:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop offer retired veterans the convenience of receiving medical care without the need to travel to a VA facility, helping them maintain their health and well-being in retirement.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Limited access to technology or internet connectivity may hinder some veterans from utilizing VA Video Connect and telehealth services.
- Lack of awareness or education about how to use these virtual healthcare options could prevent veterans from taking advantage of the benefits they offer.
- Technical difficulties or issues with the platform may lead to frustration and barriers in accessing necessary healthcare services.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify the specific moments where Veterans encounter difficulties or drop off while using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with a target of reducing drop-off rates by 15% within the next 6 months.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Conduct usability testing to determine which specific content, touchpoints, and interaction patterns are causing Veterans to get stuck or drop off while using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.
- Identify the key user needs and pain points related to VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop to inform the development of more user-friendly features and interfaces.
- Provide actionable recommendations based on research findings to improve user confidence, reduce last-minute technical issues, and enhance the overall user experience of VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for a systematic evaluation of the user experience, identifying pain points and areas for improvement to enhance the overall usability and effectiveness of the platform for veterans.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one usability testing sessions lasting approximately 45-60 minutes to allow for in-depth exploration of user interactions with VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop.
- **Key Activities:** Utilizing task-based scenarios to observe how participants navigate the platform, conducting cognitive walkthroughs to understand user thought processes, and collecting feedback through surveys and interviews to gather qualitative insights.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Using a combination of think-aloud protocols, observation of user interactions, and post-task debriefings to capture user feedback on usability, functionality, and overall user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Ensuring compliance with VA regulations and guidelines for research, including obtaining necessary approvals and coordinating with VA stakeholders throughout the research process.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Implementing trauma-informed practices to create a safe and supportive environment for veteran participants, acknowledging potential triggers and providing resources for support if needed.
- **Accessibility:** Making accommodations for diverse participant needs, such as providing alternative communication methods for individuals with disabilities and ensuring the usability testing environment is accessible to all participants.

## ⚠️ Risk Management

> [!WARNING]
> **High-Risk Areas Requiring Immediate Mitigation**

> [!CAUTION] 
> **Medium-Risk Areas to Monitor Closely**

**Recruitment Challenges:** 
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Collaborate with VA healthcare providers to identify and reach out to eligible Veterans who have used VA Video Connect in the past. Offer incentives for participation to increase interest and engagement.

**Execution Issues:** 
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Conduct thorough testing of the usability research tools and platforms prior to the study to identify and address any potential technical issues. Provide clear instructions and support for participants to ensure smooth execution of the research tasks.

**Timeline Risks:** 
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** Develop a detailed project timeline with clear milestones and deadlines. Regularly monitor progress and adjust the timeline as needed to ensure that the research stays on track.

**Quality Concerns:** 
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** Implement rigorous data validation processes, such as cross-checking participant responses and conducting follow-up interviews to verify information. Utilize experienced researchers and experts in the field of telehealth to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the research findings.
