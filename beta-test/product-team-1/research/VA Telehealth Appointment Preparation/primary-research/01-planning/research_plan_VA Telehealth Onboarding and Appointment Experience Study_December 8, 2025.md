# Research Plan — VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

📦 **Product Area:** VA Video Connect — Allows Veterans to have virtual medical appointments with their healthcare providers through their mobile devices or desktop computers.

👩🏽‍🔬 **Lead Researcher:** Lapedra Tolson · VA Digitl

🗓️ **Date:** December 8, 2025 · **Timeline:** standard

---

## 🔎 Background

This research will inform decisions on how to streamline the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans using VA Video Connect and other touchpoints across mobile and desktop platforms. By improving the appointment scheduling process, providing timely technical support, and coordinating messages effectively, Veterans will have a smoother and more user-friendly telehealth experience. This will ultimately lead to increased usage of telehealth services by Veterans, improving their access to healthcare and overall satisfaction. Additionally, the business impact for VA will include increased efficiency in delivering telehealth services, potentially reducing costs and improving overall patient outcomes.

## 🧭 User Journey

**User Journey Connection:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop play a crucial role in connecting veterans with healthcare providers remotely, ensuring they have access to necessary medical care regardless of their location.

**Key Moments/Phases:**
- **Taking care of myself:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop allow veterans to easily schedule virtual appointments with healthcare professionals, monitor their health conditions, and receive medical advice without the need to travel to a VA facility.
- **Putting down roots:** By offering convenient telehealth services, VA Video Connect and supporting touchpoints on mobile and desktop help veterans establish a routine of regular healthcare check-ins and consultations as they transition into civilian life.
- **Retiring:** For veterans entering retirement, VA Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop provide a seamless way to continue managing their health and accessing medical services without disruptions.

**Critical Pain Points:**
- Limited access to technology or internet connectivity may hinder some veterans from utilizing VA Video Connect and telehealth touchpoints on mobile and desktop.
- The learning curve associated with using virtual healthcare platforms could pose challenges for older veterans who are less familiar with technology.
- Privacy and security concerns regarding personal health information shared through telehealth services may be a barrier for some veterans in utilizing VA Video Connect and supporting touchpoints.

## 🎓 Research Goals

**Primary Goal:** Identify specific moments where Veterans encounter technical issues or drop off while using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with the aim of reducing user frustration and increasing successful telehealth visits by 20% within the next 6 months.

**Supporting Goals:**
- Determine the most common content and touchpoints that lead to user confusion or frustration during the onboarding process of VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, in order to streamline the user experience and reduce drop-off rates by 15%.
- Identify the interaction patterns that are most effective in building user confidence and reducing last-minute technical issues for Veterans using VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, with the goal of increasing successful telehealth visits by 25%.
- Evaluate user feedback and behavior data to pinpoint areas of improvement in VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop, providing actionable recommendations to enhance the overall user experience and reduce technical barriers for Veterans accessing telehealth services.

## 🛠️ Methodology

**Methodology Rationale:** Usability research is specifically right for VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile and desktop because it allows for the evaluation of user experience, efficiency, and satisfaction with the technology, which are crucial for ensuring successful implementation and adoption by veterans.

**Implementation Approach:**
- **Session Format:** One-on-one moderated usability testing sessions lasting approximately 60 minutes each.
- **Key Activities:** Task-based scenarios to evaluate navigation, functionality, and user interface design. Think-aloud protocol for participants to verbalize their thoughts and reactions during interactions with the technology.
- **Evaluation Protocol:** Use of standardized usability metrics such as task success rate, time on task, and user satisfaction ratings. Post-session interviews to gather qualitative feedback on user experience.

**VA Compliance Notes:**
- **Collaboration Cycle:** Regular check-ins with VA stakeholders to ensure alignment with project requirements and timelines.
- **Trauma-Informed Approach:** Sensitivity to potential triggers for veteran participants, providing a safe and supportive environment for feedback.
- **Accessibility:** Providing options for participants with diverse needs, such as screen reader compatibility, adjustable font sizes, and alternative input methods.

## ⚠️ Risk Management

> [!WARNING]
> **High-Risk Areas Requiring Immediate Mitigation**

> [!CAUTION] 
> **Medium-Risk Areas to Monitor Closely**

**Recruitment Challenges:** 
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** To address recruitment challenges, we will work closely with VA healthcare providers to identify and reach out to eligible Veterans who have recently used telehealth services. Additionally, we will leverage social media and online platforms frequented by Veterans and caregivers to promote the study and encourage participation.

**Execution Issues:** 
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** To ensure smooth execution of the usability research, we will conduct thorough training sessions for the research team on the VA Video Connect platform and supporting telehealth touchpoints. We will also conduct pilot testing to identify and address any potential technical issues before the actual research sessions.

**Timeline Risks:** 
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Mitigation:** To stay on schedule, we will establish clear milestones and deadlines for each phase of the research project. Regular check-ins and progress updates will be conducted to monitor the progress and address any delays promptly. Flexibility in the timeline will also be built in to accommodate any unforeseen challenges.

**Quality Concerns:** 
- **Likelihood:** High
- **Mitigation:** To ensure the validity and quality of the research findings, we will employ a diverse range of research methods, including surveys, interviews, and usability testing. Data collection and analysis will be conducted rigorously, and findings will be cross-validated with multiple sources to ensure accuracy. Additionally, regular peer reviews and feedback sessions will be held to maintain research quality throughout the project.
