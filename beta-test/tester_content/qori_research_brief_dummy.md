## Research Brief Fields

**Project Title/Focus:**
VA Housing Application Process - Identifying Usability Barriers and Accessibility Gaps

**Study context and motivation:**
Current VA housing application completion rate is only 66%, with high abandonment at the eligibility screening and document upload stages. Veterans are calling the support line 3x more than other VA benefits, indicating significant usability issues. Recent accessibility audit flagged 12 WCAG violations. Leadership wants to prioritize improvements for Q4 2025 roadmap, but we need data on which pain points have the highest impact on veteran success.

**Target barriers to identify:**
- Navigation confusion between different housing program types (loans vs. grants vs. rental assistance)
- Document upload failures and unclear requirements 
- Eligibility screening questions that are too complex or confusing
- Mobile experience issues (68% of veterans start applications on mobile)
- Status tracking system that doesn't provide meaningful updates
- Accessibility barriers for veterans using assistive technologies

**User flows/touchpoints to evaluate:**
- Initial program selection and eligibility screening
- Document gathering and upload process
- Application form completion (sections 1-4)
- Application review and submission
- Status checking and follow-up communication
- Support-seeking behavior when stuck

**Stakeholders Consulted:**
- Maria Rodriguez (Housing Benefits Program Manager) - provided completion rate data and top support call reasons
- James Kim (VA.gov Product Owner) - shared analytics showing drop-off points
- Dr. Sarah Chen (Accessibility Lead) - reviewed recent audit findings
- Michael Torres (Veterans Service Representative) - insights from veteran support calls
- Lisa Johnson (UX Design Lead) - previous research findings and design constraints

**Research Approach:**
Mixed-methods study combining user interviews (5 veterans) and moderated usability testing (5 veterans) to understand both attitudinal and behavioral barriers. Focus on veterans who attempted applications in the last 6 months, with emphasis on including participants who use assistive technologies. Sessions will be conducted remotely to accommodate participants across different geographic locations.
