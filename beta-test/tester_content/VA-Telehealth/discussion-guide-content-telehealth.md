# Discussion Guide Form Content

## What are you researching?

We're studying how Veterans prepare for and enter their VA telehealth appointments. Many Veterans report that they feel unsure about device setup, connectivity, and the steps needed to join their video visit. Some use multiple channels for instructions, which creates confusion. The goal is to map out where the process breaks down and where guidance should change. This work will support the Telehealth Clinic redesign planned for early 2026.

## What specific questions do you need answered?

Which steps feel unclear or overwhelming when Veterans prepare for a telehealth visit? How do Veterans decide whether to join from a phone, laptop, or tablet? What information matters most to them right before the appointment begins? When do they first look for instructions and where do they expect to find them? How do assistive technologies change the joining experience? What triggers last-minute calls to support lines? Which parts of the appointment workflow Veterans skip, misunderstand, or repeat because they didn't trust the first instruction?

## Who are your participants?

Veterans who completed at least one VA telehealth visit in the last year. Include a mix of ages, service histories, and device preferences. Half of participants should rely on accessibility tools such as screen readers, magnification, captions, or adaptive input. Include at least two Veterans who receive help from a caregiver during telehealth visits. Recruit across time zones to capture scheduling differences and morning versus afternoon appointment behaviors.
