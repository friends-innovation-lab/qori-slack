# Research Brief

## Research Brief Fields

**Project Title/Focus:** VA Telehealth Appointment Preparation and Join Experience

**Study context and motivation:**  
Telehealth appointments now represent a significant share of VA care, yet many Veterans report uncertainty during pre-appointment tasks such as device setup, connection tests, and permissions. Support teams have logged repeated issues related to unclear instructions, mismatched captions, and confusion about whether to join through browser or app. Appointment no-show rates remain higher for first-time telehealth users, and clinicians report delays due to Veterans joining late or encountering technical barriers. Improving clarity, accessibility, and predictability across the “prep and join” workflow is essential for the Telehealth Services redesign planned for early 2026.

**Target barriers to identify:**  
* Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages  
* Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore  
* Caption delays that reduce comprehension during video-based guidance  
* Unclear device and browser compatibility expectations  
* Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone  
* Accessibility gaps for screen reader, magnification, and caption users  
* Uncertainty about what to expect after clicking the visit link (loading states, waiting room, connection checks)

**User flows/touchpoints to evaluate:**  
* Email/SMS reminders and appointment detail pages  
* Pre-visit setup steps (camera, microphone, internet tests)  
* Browser vs. in-app join pathways  
* Permission prompts and device compatibility checks  
* Loading and waiting room states  
* Troubleshooting guidance when tests fail  
* Profile updates that affect telehealth readiness (contact info, device preferences)

**Stakeholders Consulted:**  
* Dr. Mia Thompson (Telehealth Clinical Lead) – clarified provider-side delays and common Veteran issues  
* Jason Wu (Telehealth Product Manager) – shared roadmap priorities for upcoming redesign  
* Linda Harper (Support Center Supervisor) – provided top categories of telehealth technical support calls  
* Samuel Ortiz (Digital Accessibility Engineer) – identified caption, screen reader, and magnification issues  
* Caroline Bright (Communications Lead) – reviewed message templates for reminders and prep steps  

**Research Approach:**  
Moderated telehealth appointment simulations with 10 Veterans representing a range of device types, assistive technology usage, and telehealth familiarity. Sessions include reviewing reminder communications, running device tests, and joining a simulated visit. Complemented by contextual inquiry with 4 participants to observe home environments, connectivity constraints, and real-life device switching. Study includes iOS, Android, desktop/laptop, and assistive technology scenarios (screen readers, magnification, captions, and TalkBack/VoiceOver).
