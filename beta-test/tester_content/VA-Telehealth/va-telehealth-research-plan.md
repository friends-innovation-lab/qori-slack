# Research Plan

## Study Planning Details

**Project Title:** VA Telehealth Onboarding and Appointment Experience Study

**Product / Feature Area:** VA Video Connect and supporting telehealth touchpoints across mobile, desktop, and in-clinic workflows

**What decision will this research inform?**  
This research will inform how the product team simplifies the first-time telehealth experience for Veterans. Findings will drive decisions about how to:

- Guide Veterans from appointment scheduling to joining their video visit
- Surface the right technical checks and instructions at the right time
- Coordinate messages between My HealtheVet, VA.gov, VA mobile apps, and email/SMS reminders

The team will use this research to choose between three competing approaches for the onboarding flow:  
1) a single guided checklist,  
2) a series of short, embedded tips along the journey,  
3) a hybrid model that blends both.

**Primary research goal:**  
Understand the specific moments where Veterans get stuck or drop off while preparing for and joining telehealth visits. Identify which content, touchpoints, and interaction patterns most effectively build confidence and reduce last-minute technical issues.

---

## Methodology Selection

**Selected Methods:**

* ✅ **Journey Mapping Sessions**  
  To co-create a detailed view of how Veterans move from “I have an appointment” to “I joined my visit successfully” across devices and channels.

* ✅ **Remote Moderated Usability Testing**  
  To observe Veterans trying to schedule, prepare for, and join a telehealth visit using realistic tasks and test accounts.

* ✅ **Contextual Inquiry (Virtual)**  
  To see how telehealth fits into the Veteran’s real environment, including device setup, internet constraints, and privacy needs at home or work.

* ⚪ Survey Research  
  Potential phase 2 to measure changes in confidence, completion rates, and perceived ease of use after improvements ship.

* ⚪ Concept Testing  
  Reserved for later rounds if new onboarding concepts emerge from early findings.

---

## Target Participants

**Primary:**  
- Veterans who have completed at least one telehealth appointment in the last 6 months  
- Veterans who prefer video visits due to mobility, transportation, or caregiving responsibilities  

**Secondary:**  
- Veterans who scheduled a telehealth appointment but never joined the visit  
- Caregivers or family members who regularly help Veterans join video visits  

**Inclusion criteria:**  
- Uses a smartphone, tablet, or laptop at least three times per week  
- Has an active My HealtheVet or VA.gov account  
- Has access to a stable internet connection for remote sessions  

**Exclusion criteria:**  
- VA employees working directly on telehealth products  
- Professional UX researchers or designers  
- Participants who cannot consent independently  

**Accessibility focus:**  
At least 40% of sessions will include Veterans who:  

- Rely on screen readers, screen magnification, or captioning  
- Have limited hand mobility or dexterity  
- Report anxiety or stress around using video technology for health care  

---

## Sample Size and Session Details

- **Journey Mapping:** 6–8 participants in small-group virtual workshops (90 minutes each)  
- **Usability Testing:** 10–12 individual remote sessions (60 minutes each)  
- **Contextual Inquiry:** 4 in-depth follow-up visits with selected participants (75–90 minutes)

---

## Timeline

**Timeline Preference:** ✅ Standard (4–6 weeks)

**High-level breakdown:**

- **Week 1:** Finalize research questions, create test scripts, align with Telehealth and Clinical stakeholders  
- **Week 2–3:** Recruit participants, schedule sessions, prepare test accounts and devices  
- **Week 3–4:** Run journey mapping workshops and usability sessions

- **Week 6:** Deliver findings, journey maps, and prioritized recommendations to product and clinical leadership
