# Session Information
Participant: Kelly Browning (TH003)  
Date: September 5, 2025  
Session Time: 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM EST  
Moderator: Jordan Reyes  
Observer: Taking live notes via /take-notes

Kelly joined at noon and was calm and precise from the beginning. She uses a laptop for telehealth and has strong familiarity with magnification tools—she zooms intentionally and knows exactly how she needs content to appear. But she's highly sensitive to layout compressions, which became the recurring theme of this session.
By minute 3, she was navigating smoothly and said "The telehealth link is easy to find once I zoom in" and "The main appointment info is clear." But around minute 11, frustration surfaced when the text compressed under magnification. "Zoom makes everything bunch together." The layout collapsed and she lost her reading position. She said "I can't tell which part of the instructions I'm on after zooming" and "Is this the right page? Everything looks different when magnified." This was a clear moment of disorientation.
Around minute 18, she expressed concern about tapping the wrong item because the spacing had overlapped under magnification. By minute 27, she was scrolling carefully through dense instructions, her pace noticeably slower. "The text is so dense it's hard to keep my place." The paragraphs lack any step structure, which makes navigation difficult for someone relying on zoom.
The visit link task was partially completed—she located it easily but zoom caused layout distortion. The instruction review was also partial—dense paragraphs and lost position multiple times. For the join simulation, she got in successfully, but around minute 37 she noted visual distortion when an input field didn't resize with her screen zoom. "The input fields don't resize well." This is a high-importance accessibility gap.
For updating contact information, the same pattern emerged that we saw with other participants. Around minute 49, she finally located the Edit button and smiled—"There it is"—but it had taken lateral scrolling to find it. "I didn't know the edit button was off to the side." Zoom made spatial orientation harder, and the button wasn't visible in her magnified view until she scrolled horizontally.
She ended around minute 54 with a relaxed tone, appreciative but emphatic about the need for spacing improvements. Throughout, she was careful and methodical, providing detailed visual feedback in a steady tone.
The three key moments: First, the instruction disorientation at minute 11 when zoom collapsed the layout and she lost her place. Second, the input field resizing failure at minute 37—a critical accessibility gap. Third, the hidden Edit button at minute 49, confirming that profile areas don't accommodate magnification users.
Kelly's needs are clear: larger tap targets, spaced and numbered instructions, and responsive input components that scale properly. This session makes a strong case for responsive layout improvements, reformatting instructions with clear structure, and expanding tap targets throughout the interface. She said "I like that the waiting page is simple"—so we know clean, well-spaced layouts work for her. The rest of the experience needs to match that standard.
