Thomas joined at 4:00 PM and was confident from the start. He's an Android tablet user who relies on telehealth primarily for convenience—his work schedule makes in-person visits difficult. He doesn't use a screen reader but depends heavily on text clarity and layout to navigate interfaces efficiently.
He started out strong. By minute 4, he was navigating appointment details with ease and said "The text message link is the fastest way for me." The SMS workflow clearly works well for him. But around minute 10, frustration crept in when he tried to locate the prep steps. He scrolled for nearly 20 seconds before finding them. "Where are the prep steps? I almost missed them—they're so far down the page." The prep details just aren't visually prominent enough.
For the device test task, the camera check went fine—"The camera test was straightforward"—but the mic test was a different story. Around minute 16, his tone became uncertain. "The mic test doesn't tell you if the volume is actually good." There's no decibel indicator, no visual feedback, nothing to confirm whether the mic is actually working at an acceptable level. His confidence dropped noticeably at this point. He completed the task, but only partially—camera OK, mic unclear.
Around minute 22, he was back in his comfort zone using the text message link to join the visit. He felt more confident with the SMS path than the portal workflow. But then at minute 29, the browser opened unexpectedly and he lost that confidence. "Sometimes I end up in the browser instead of the app and I get lost." There's no clear messaging explaining why the context switched, and it broke his expectation of staying in one place. He said "Do I need the app or browser? It changes each time." By minute 41, he had recovered and completed the join workflow smoothly, and he liked that the waiting screen was "simple and clear."
For the platform guidance review, he noted real inconsistencies. "The instructions don't match what I see on Android." He also mentioned "I'm not sure if my device is compatible every time. It doesn't tell me." There's no compatibility indicator, and Android-specific flows are poorly represented in the instructions. This was another partial completion.
He ended the session around minute 55 expressing a desire for "straightforward answers" in the prep steps. Throughout, he was efficient and direct, openly sharing his sources of confusion. He kept coming back to wanting "simple steps."
The three standout moments: First, the buried prep steps at minute 10—he had to scroll way too long to find critical information. Second, the mic test ambiguity at minute 16, where the lack of feedback immediately dropped his confidence. Third, the browser switch confusion at minute 29, where breaking his expectation caused real disorientation.
This session makes a strong case for Android-specific refinement, elevating prep steps visually, and adding proper feedback to the mic test. Thomas needs short checklists with priority actions, clear device compatibility guidance, and a predictable entry flow that doesn't switch between app and browser without explanation.
