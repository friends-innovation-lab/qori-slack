# VA Office of Inspector General: Telehealth Access and Quality Review

**Report Number:** 2024-TELE-0847
**Date:** September 15, 2024
**Author:** Office of Healthcare Inspections

---

## Executive Summary

This report examines the Department of Veterans Affairs' telehealth program implementation across 12 VA Medical Centers (VAMCs) from January 2023 through June 2024. The review focused on access, quality of care, and Veteran satisfaction with video-based healthcare services.

### Key Findings

1. **Access Improvements:** Telehealth appointments increased 47% year-over-year, with VA Video Connect (VVC) sessions growing from 2.1 million to 3.1 million annually.

2. **Technical Barriers Persist:** 34% of Veterans reported difficulty joining video appointments on their first attempt. Common issues included:
   - Audio/video permissions (41% of technical issues)
   - Browser compatibility (28% of technical issues)
   - Network connectivity (31% of technical issues)

3. **Rural Veteran Impact:** Veterans in rural areas showed 62% higher telehealth utilization than urban counterparts, but also reported 23% more technical difficulties.

4. **Caregiver Involvement:** 18% of telehealth sessions involved caregiver assistance, rising to 41% for Veterans over age 75.

5. **No-Show Rates:** Telehealth no-show rates averaged 12.3%, compared to 8.7% for in-person appointments. However, same-day rescheduling was 3x more common for telehealth.

---

## Methodology

The OIG conducted:
- Chart reviews of 2,400 telehealth encounters
- Surveys of 1,850 Veterans who used telehealth services
- Interviews with 156 VA clinical and IT staff
- Technical assessment of VVC platform across devices

---

## Detailed Findings

### Finding 1: Pre-Appointment Preparation Gaps

Veterans who received pre-appointment technical check communications were 67% more likely to successfully join their first appointment without assistance. However, only 43% of Veterans reported receiving such communications.

**Recommendation 1:** Implement standardized pre-appointment communication protocols including device testing links and step-by-step joining instructions.

### Finding 2: Device Preference Patterns

| Device Type | Usage Rate | Success Rate | Avg. Setup Time |
|-------------|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Smartphone | 52% | 78% | 4.2 min |
| Laptop/Desktop | 31% | 89% | 2.8 min |
| Tablet | 14% | 82% | 3.5 min |
| VA-issued device | 3% | 94% | 1.9 min |

Veterans using smartphones experienced more audio issues but appreciated the convenience. Desktop users had highest success rates but lower adoption.

### Finding 3: Accessibility Gaps

Veterans using assistive technologies reported:
- Screen reader compatibility issues with VVC interface (56% reported problems)
- Insufficient captioning accuracy (average 71% accuracy, below 85% target)
- Limited support for cognitive accessibility needs

---

## Recommendations

1. **Immediate (0-90 days):**
   - Deploy automated pre-appointment device testing
   - Expand technical support hours to include evenings/weekends
   - Create simplified joining instructions in multiple formats

2. **Short-term (90-180 days):**
   - Improve VVC accessibility for screen reader users
   - Implement caregiver-specific access features
   - Develop mobile-first onboarding experience

3. **Long-term (180+ days):**
   - Integrate telehealth technical support into MyHealtheVet
   - Establish telehealth digital literacy training programs
   - Create Veteran peer support network for technology assistance

---

## Conclusion

VA telehealth services have significantly expanded access to care, particularly for rural Veterans. However, technical barriers during appointment joining remain a significant pain point. Targeted improvements to the pre-appointment experience and enhanced accessibility features would substantially improve Veteran outcomes.
