## 🎯 Business Context

This user research project aims to assess the navigation patterns and information architecture of the VA mobile application, with a specific focus on improving access for veterans to their health records, appointment scheduling, and benefits tracking on various mobile devices. The goal is to streamline the user experience and enhance the efficiency of accessing crucial services for veterans through the mobile platform. This study addresses the unique challenges faced by veterans in navigating the VA mobile application and aims to optimize their interaction with key features essential to their healthcare and benefits management.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing navigation and information architecture in the VA mobile application is causing veterans to struggle with accessing their health records, scheduling appointments, and tracking benefits, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks.
- The low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10 is impacting VA operations by potentially hindering veterans' ability to efficiently manage their health care needs and benefits, which could result in decreased engagement with VA services.
- Technical limitations within the mobile app, such as slow loading times, lack of intuitive design, and difficulty in finding key features, are contributing to the overall poor user experience and dissatisfaction among veterans.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter the most frustration in navigating the VA Mobile App's main navigation structure and information architecture.
- Which features within the VA Mobile App are most frequently accessed by veterans and should be prioritized for improvement.
- Why the current user satisfaction rating for the VA Mobile App is low at 4.2/10, by understanding the root causes behind navigation and information architecture barriers.
- How veterans currently interact with the search functionality and task completion flows within the VA Mobile App, specifically in relation to health records, appointment scheduling, and benefits tracking.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Lack of intuitive navigation elements leading to confusion and frustration for veterans trying to locate specific features or information within the mobile app.
- Inconsistent information architecture causing difficulties in accessing critical resources or services, resulting in high abandonment rates among users.
- Poor visual hierarchy and organization of content leading to a disjointed user experience and contributing to the low satisfaction rating.
- Limited customization options and personalization features hindering veterans from tailoring the app to their individual needs and preferences.
- Complex menu structures and nested navigation paths making it challenging for users to quickly find and complete tasks within the app.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the VA Health Benefits Mobile App, and what specific barriers do they encounter that lead to task abandonment?
2. What causes the low user satisfaction rating of 4.2/10 for the VA Health Benefits Mobile App, specifically in relation to the information architecture and search functionality?
3. What barriers prevent veterans from efficiently completing health and benefits tasks within the VA Mobile App, and how do these barriers impact their overall user experience?
4. Why do veterans struggle to find and access core features within the VA Health Benefits Mobile App, and how can improvements in information hierarchy and discoverability address these challenges?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Inadequate labeling and organization of menu options increases user confusion and frustration, leading to higher rates of task abandonment among veterans using the VA Health Benefits Mobile App.
2. Veterans with visual impairments face more difficulty navigating the app due to lack of accessibility features, resulting in lower user satisfaction ratings.
3. The current design of the app's search function leads to irrelevant search results and difficulty finding specific information, contributing to the overall low user satisfaction rating.
