## 🎯 Business Context

The current VA mobile application has a user satisfaction rating of 4.2 out of 10, with 45% of veterans abandoning tasks before completion. Navigation analytics reveal that users heavily rely on the search function to locate basic features, suggesting inadequate information architecture. App store reviews consistently highlight difficulties in finding necessary services and navigating the app. Given that 78% of veteran interactions occur on mobile devices, enhancing navigation is crucial for improving access to essential services. Actionable insights are needed to inform the redesign of the mobile app, scheduled for Q1 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories is causing veterans to struggle to find essential services like prescription refills and appointment scheduling, leading to frustration and abandonment of tasks mid-completion.
- Poor discoverability of key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling is impacting VA operations by increasing the workload on call centers and in-person service centers as veterans seek assistance in accessing these services.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections are preventing veterans from efficiently accessing the information they need, resulting in decreased user satisfaction and negative app store reviews.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter confusion in the main menu structure and how it affects their ability to navigate the app effectively.
- Which key features, such as prescription refills and appointment scheduling, are most difficult for veterans to discover and use.
- Why there are inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections and how it impacts user experience.
- How the search functionality's irrelevant or outdated results contribute to user dissatisfaction and task abandonment.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users are confused by the main menu structure due to overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile, leading to difficulty in finding relevant information.
- Key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling are not easily discoverable, causing frustration for users trying to access essential functionalities.
- Inconsistent navigation patterns across different sections of the app create confusion and hinder users' ability to move seamlessly between tasks.
- The search functionality returns irrelevant or outdated results, making it challenging for users to find the information they are looking for efficiently.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the main menu structure of the 2026 VA Health Benefits Mobile App, and what challenges do they face with overlapping categories such as Health, Benefits, and Profile?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily discovering key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling within the app, and how does this impact their overall user experience?
3. Why do veterans experience inconsistent navigation patterns between different sections of the app, and how does this affect their ability to efficiently complete tasks such as accessing health records or managing prescriptions?
4. What causes the search functionality in the app to return irrelevant or outdated results, and how does this impact veterans' ability to find the information they need quickly and accurately?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Confusing main menu structure with overlapping categories (Health vs. Benefits vs. Profile) increases user frustration and decreases overall app engagement.
2. Veterans with limited technological proficiency face more difficulty in navigating the app and accessing key features like prescription refills and appointment scheduling.
3. Inconsistent navigation patterns between different app sections lead to user confusion and decreased efficiency in completing tasks within the app.
