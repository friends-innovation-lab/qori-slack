## 🎯 Business Context

This study aims to address the challenges faced by Veterans in utilizing telehealth services, specifically focusing on reducing appointment no-shows, decreasing technical failure rates, and enhancing Veteran confidence in telehealth as a viable option for receiving care. By improving these aspects, we can better support Veterans in accessing and utilizing telehealth services effectively within the VA healthcare system.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Veterans facing uncertainty during pre-appointment tasks such as device setup and connection tests is causing a lack of confidence in using telehealth, potentially leading to appointment no-shows and decreased utilization of the service.
- Repeated issues related to unclear instructions and confusion about joining through browser or app are impacting the efficiency of VA operations, potentially leading to increased technical failure rates and decreased overall satisfaction with telehealth services.
- Mismatched captions and confusion about permissions are preventing Veterans from successfully navigating the telehealth platform, hindering the desired outcome of increased Veteran confidence and successful utilization of telehealth appointments.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter the most confusion and frustration during pre-appointment tasks such as device setup, connection tests, and permissions.
- Which aspects of the pre-visit setup steps, browser vs. in-app join pathways, and permission prompts are the highest priority for improving clarity and ease of use for Veterans.
- Why Veterans are unsure about whether to join through a browser or app, and how this impacts their overall confidence and success in using telehealth appointments.
- How Veterans currently navigate loading and waiting room states, troubleshoot technical issues, and update their profiles for telehealth readiness.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Veterans report uncertainty during pre-appointment tasks such as setting up devices, conducting connection tests, and granting permissions due to unclear instructions provided by support teams.
- Support teams have documented repeated issues related to mismatched captions, leading to confusion among Veterans about the necessary steps to join a telehealth appointment through a browser or app.
- Veterans express confusion about the proper procedures for device setup, connection testing, and permission granting, resulting in a barrier to accessing telehealth appointments efficiently.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the pre-appointment setup tasks such as device setup, connection tests, and permissions for VA Telehealth appointments?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from easily understanding instructions, captions, and join pathways (browser vs. app) when preparing for a Telehealth appointment?
3. Why do veterans experience confusion and uncertainty when encountering loading and waiting room states during the Telehealth appointment process?
4. What causes veterans to struggle with troubleshooting guidance and profile updates that impact their readiness for Telehealth appointments within the VA system?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Unclear instructions and mismatched captions during VA Telehealth Onboarding increase user frustration and decrease successful appointment completion rates.
2. Veterans with limited technology literacy face more difficulties with device setup and connection tests, leading to higher rates of missed appointments.
3. The current approach of offering multiple options for joining telehealth appointments (browser or app) leads to confusion and delays in appointment start times.
