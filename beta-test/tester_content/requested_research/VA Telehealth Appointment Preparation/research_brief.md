## 🎯 Business Context

Telehealth appointments have become a significant component of VA care, yet many Veterans face challenges with pre-appointment tasks such as device setup, connection tests, and permissions. Support teams have identified recurring issues related to unclear instructions, mismatched captions, and confusion regarding the preferred method of joining appointments. This has resulted in higher appointment no-show rates for first-time telehealth users and delays for clinicians due to late arrivals and technical difficulties experienced by Veterans.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages is causing confusion for Veterans, leading to higher appointment no-show rates and delays for clinicians.
- Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore are preventing Veterans from properly setting up their devices and joining appointments on time.
- Unclear device and browser compatibility expectations are causing technical barriers for Veterans, leading to frustration and delays in accessing telehealth appointments.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most in locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages.
- Which prep instructions veterans find most challenging to follow due to length or density.
- Why veterans experience delays in comprehension during video-based guidance due to caption issues.
- How veterans currently navigate through permission prompts and device compatibility expectations during telehealth appointments.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users struggle to locate appointment instructions across multiple communication channels such as email, SMS, and portal messages, leading to confusion and missed appointments.
- Veterans often ignore or skim through lengthy and dense preparation instructions, resulting in incomplete or incorrect preparation for appointments.
- Delays in captions during video-based guidance reduce user comprehension and engagement with the content.
- Unclear expectations regarding device and browser compatibility make it difficult for users to access and use the platform effectively.
- Confusing and inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone usage create barriers for users trying to engage in video-based interactions.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate through email, SMS, and portal messages to locate appointment instructions for their telehealth appointments?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from fully engaging with and following overly long or dense prep instructions provided for their telehealth appointments?
3. Why do veterans experience caption delays that reduce comprehension during video-based guidance for their telehealth appointments?
4. What causes confusion or inconsistency in permission prompts for camera and microphone usage during telehealth appointments, particularly in relation to device and browser compatibility expectations?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages increases the likelihood of missed or late appointments among Veterans.
2. Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore lead to lower engagement and comprehension during telehealth appointments.
3. Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone result in technical difficulties and frustration for Veterans during telehealth appointments.
