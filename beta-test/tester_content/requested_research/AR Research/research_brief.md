## 🎯 Business Context

"Exploring the Efficacy of Testing Methods in Addressing Challenges Faced by Veterans Affairs and Veterans"

This study aims to investigate the effectiveness of testing methods in addressing unique challenges encountered within the Veterans Affairs (VA) system and by veterans themselves. By examining the impact of various testing approaches, this research seeks to provide valuable insights for improving services and support for VA and veteran populations.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Lack of user-friendly interfaces for AR applications is causing frustration and confusion for Veterans trying to access important information or services.
- Inefficient AR technology integration is hindering the VA's ability to provide timely and accurate support to Veterans, impacting overall service delivery.
- Limited accessibility features in AR products are preventing Veterans with disabilities from fully benefiting from the technology, leading to exclusion and unequal access to resources.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans encounter the most significant barriers in accessing testing resources during their journey.
- Which aspects of the user flow for testing services are most critical for veterans to have a positive experience.
- Why veterans may be experiencing difficulties in navigating the testing process and accessing necessary resources.
- How veterans currently interact with testing services and what pain points they experience throughout the process.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Lack of standardized testing protocols across research teams leads to inconsistent results and difficulties in comparing findings.
- Limited access to user testing tools and resources hinders the ability to conduct thorough research.
- Insufficient user recruitment strategies result in a narrow pool of participants, potentially skewing research outcomes.
- Inadequate training for researchers on how to conduct effective user testing sessions leads to inaccurate data collection.
- Poorly designed user interfaces on testing platforms create confusion and frustration for both researchers and participants.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate the testing research process for augmented reality technologies?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from effectively engaging with AR testing research initiatives?
3. Why do veterans encounter challenges in accessing and participating in AR testing research opportunities?
4. What causes veterans to experience difficulties in understanding and utilizing user flows within AR testing research platforms?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. The lack of user-friendly interface in AR Research tools increases user frustration and decreases overall engagement with the technology.
2. Veterans with PTSD face more difficulties navigating AR Research platforms due to the overwhelming sensory stimuli triggering anxiety and stress.
3. The current design approach in AR Research focusing on complex features leads to decreased user retention and adoption rates.
