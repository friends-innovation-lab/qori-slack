## 🎯 Business Context

Telehealth appointments have become a significant component of VA care for Veterans. However, many Veterans face uncertainties with pre-appointment tasks like device setup, connection tests, and permissions. Support teams have noted recurring issues stemming from unclear instructions, mismatched captions, and confusion regarding whether to join through a browser or an app. These challenges highlight the need for improved guidance and support for Veterans navigating telehealth services within the VA system.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages is causing confusion and uncertainty for Veterans during pre-appointment tasks.
- Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore are impacting the effectiveness of the telehealth appointments and potentially leading to missed information or misunderstandings.
- Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone are hindering Veterans from successfully connecting to their telehealth appointments, potentially leading to missed or delayed appointments.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans struggle the most in locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages.
- Which prep instructions are causing the most confusion for Veterans, leading them to skim or ignore important information.
- Why Veterans are experiencing comprehension issues due to caption delays during video-based guidance.
- How Veterans are currently navigating unclear device and browser compatibility expectations and permission prompts for camera and microphone.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users struggle to locate appointment instructions scattered across multiple channels (email, SMS, portal messages).
- Veterans often ignore or skim through lengthy and dense preparation instructions provided.
- Delays in captions during video-based guidance hinder user comprehension.
- Unclear expectations regarding device and browser compatibility create confusion for users.
- Inconsistent and confusing permission prompts for camera and microphone usage impact user experience.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate and locate appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from fully engaging with and comprehending overly long or dense prep instructions for telehealth appointments?
3. Why do veterans experience delays and reduced comprehension during video-based guidance due to caption delays?
4. What causes confusion and frustration for veterans regarding device and browser compatibility expectations, as well as permission prompts for camera and microphone during telehealth appointments?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages increases the likelihood of Veterans missing or being late to their telehealth appointments.
2. Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore lead to decreased understanding of how to properly prepare for their telehealth appointments.
3. Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone result in Veterans experiencing technical difficulties and delays during their telehealth appointments.
