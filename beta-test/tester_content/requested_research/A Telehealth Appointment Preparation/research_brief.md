## 🎯 Business Context

Telehealth appointments have become a significant component of VA care, however, many Veterans experience uncertainty during pre-appointment tasks such as device setup, connection tests, and permissions. Support teams have identified recurring issues with unclear instructions, mismatched captions, and confusion regarding whether to join via browser or app. With higher appointment no-show rates for first-time telehealth users and clinicians facing delays due to late arrivals and technical difficulties, enhancing clarity, accessibility, and predictability in the "prep and join" workflow is crucial for the upcoming Telehealth Services redesign in early 2026.

> [!WARNING]
> **Critical Issues Driving This Research**
> 
> - Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages is causing confusion for Veterans, leading to higher appointment no-show rates and delays for clinicians.
- Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore are preventing Veterans from properly preparing for their telehealth appointments, resulting in a less efficient and effective healthcare delivery process.
- Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone are causing technical barriers for Veterans, leading to frustration and potential disengagement with the telehealth service.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

What we want to learn:
- Where veterans are experiencing the most difficulty in locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages.
- Which prep instructions are overly long or dense, causing veterans to skim or ignore them.
- Why veterans are experiencing delays in comprehension during video-based guidance due to caption delays.
- How veterans are currently navigating unclear device and browser compatibility expectations and permission prompts for camera and microphone.

## 🎯 Target Barriers

- Users struggle to locate appointment instructions spread across multiple channels such as email, SMS, and portal messages.
- Veterans often ignore or skim through overly long and dense preparation instructions.
- Delayed captions in video-based guidance hinder user comprehension.
- Users face uncertainty regarding device and browser compatibility requirements.
- Confusing and inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone add to user confusion.

## 🤔 Key Questions & Hypotheses

### 🔍 Primary Research Questions
1. How do veterans currently navigate through email/SMS reminders and appointment detail pages to locate appointment instructions?
2. What barriers prevent veterans from fully understanding and following overly long or dense prep instructions provided for telehealth appointments?
3. Why do veterans experience caption delays that reduce comprehension during video-based guidance for telehealth appointments?
4. What causes confusion or frustration among veterans when encountering unclear device and browser compatibility expectations during telehealth appointments?

### 💭 Team Hypotheses
1. Difficulty locating appointment instructions across email, SMS, and portal messages increases the likelihood of Veterans missing important information and appointments.
2. Overly long or dense prep instructions that Veterans skim or ignore lead to decreased understanding of the telehealth appointment process and potential confusion during the appointment.
3. Confusing or inconsistent permission prompts for camera and microphone result in Veterans experiencing technical difficulties and frustration during telehealth appointments.

| 🔬 **Aspect** | 📝 **Details** |

This approach aligns with VA research standards for user-centered design and usability testing. |
