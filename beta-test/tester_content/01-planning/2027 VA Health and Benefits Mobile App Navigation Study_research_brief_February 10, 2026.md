# Research Brief: 2027 VA Health and Benefits Mobile App Navigation Study

**Lead Researcher:** Taylor Kim | **Date:** February 10, 2026 | **Project Area:** Digital Services

**Requested by:** Lapedra Tolson, CEO | **Timeline:** 4 weeks weeks

---

## Business Context

The VA mobile app has a 4.2/10 user satisfaction rating with 45% task abandonment rate. 78% of veteran interactions now happen on mobile. App store reviews consistently mention "can't find what I need." Support calls show 34% relate to navigation issues. Leadership needs insights for Q1 2026 mobile redesign.

---

## Research Objectives

- **Where** veterans encounter navigation barriers in the mobile app
- **Which** navigation patterns (bottom tabs vs hamburger menu) enable faster task completion
- **How** accessibility needs affect navigation success
- **Why** veterans abandon tasks and resort to phone support

---

## Research Questions

1. What mental models do veterans use to organize health and benefits information?

2. Which critical barriers prevent veterans from finding prescription refills, appointments, and benefits status?

3. How do screen reader users navigate the current app structure?

4. What causes the 45% task abandonment rate?

---

## User Journeys in Scope

1. App onboarding → Dashboard → Feature discovery
2. Health menu → Prescriptions → Refill request
3. Health menu → Appointments → Schedule new
4. Benefits menu → Claims status → Claim details
5. Profile → Settings → Notification preferences

---

## Target Barriers

| Category | Barrier |
|:---------|:--------|
| Information Architecture | Confusing menu structure (Health vs Benefits vs Profile overlap) |
| Navigation | Poor discoverability of prescription refills and appointment scheduling |
| Navigation | Inconsistent navigation patterns between sections |
| Accessibility | Unlabeled buttons blocking screen reader users |
| Information Architecture | Information hierarchy not matching veteran mental models |

---

## Hypotheses

We believe veterans abandon tasks because: (1) key features are buried too deep in menus, (2) category labels don't match their mental models, (3) screen reader users can't identify unlabeled buttons.

---

## Methodology

| Aspect | Details |
|:-------|:--------|
| **Method** | usability_testing |
| **Rationale** | Need to observe real-time task completion and identify specific friction points. Usability testing reveals where veterans get stuck, how they recover, and what causes abandonment. Think-aloud protocol captures mental models and expectations. |
| **Sample Size** | 8-10 participants |

---

## Participant Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Veterans who used VA mobile app in past 90 days
- Mix of iOS (5) and Android (5) users
- At least 3 participants using assistive technology (VoiceOver, TalkBack, large text)

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Range of ages (25-34, 35-54, 55+)
- Urban, suburban, and rural locations
- Mix of tech comfort levels

**Exclude:**
- VA employees
- Participants in previous VA mobile studies (past 6 months)

## Success Criteria

• We will have succeeded if we identify and document at least 5 specific navigation barriers that cause veterans to abandon tasks, with clear evidence of which app sections and user flows are most problematic.

• We will have succeeded if we can definitively determine which navigation pattern (bottom tabs vs hamburger menu) results in faster task completion times, with quantifiable data showing at least a 20% difference in completion rates for key tasks like prescription refills and appointment scheduling.

• We will have succeeded if we map out veterans' mental models for organizing health and benefits information and can demonstrate how the current app structure aligns or conflicts with these models, providing actionable recommendations for information architecture improvements.

• We will have succeeded if we uncover the root causes behind the 45% task abandonment rate and identify specific accessibility barriers that prevent screen reader users from successfully navigating the app, with concrete solutions for each barrier discovered.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Research will inform:
- **Information Architecture Redesign** — Restructuring app navigation hierarchy and content organization based on identified pain points where veterans get lost or abandon tasks
- **Navigation Pattern Selection** — Choosing between bottom tabs vs hamburger menu implementation for the Q1 2026 redesign based on task completion speed and success rate data
- **Accessibility-First Design Standards** — Developing navigation requirements that accommodate diverse veteran accessibility needs while reducing the 34% of support calls related to navigation issues
