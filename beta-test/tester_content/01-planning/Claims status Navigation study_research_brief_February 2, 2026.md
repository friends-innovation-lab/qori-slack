## Participant Criteria

**Must-haves:**
- Currently have an active VA disability claim or have filed a claim within the past 12 months
- Have experience navigating the VA claims process

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Mix of claim types (initial claims, appeals, supplemental claims)
- Variety of claim statuses (pending, in progress, recently decided)
- Range of experience levels with VA digital tools

**Exclude:**
- VA employees or contractors
- Participants in VA research studies within the past 6 months

## Success Criteria

• We will have succeeded if participants can locate their claims status information within 2 minutes without assistance in at least 80% of test scenarios.

• We will have succeeded if we identify and document at least 3 specific navigation pain points that prevent users from efficiently accessing their claims status.

• We will have succeeded if participants rate the overall ease of finding claims status information as 4 out of 5 or higher on a satisfaction scale.

• We will have succeeded if we can provide actionable recommendations for improving the claims status navigation flow based on observed user behavior patterns and feedback.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Research will inform:
- **User Interface Design** — Optimization of claims status page layout, navigation flows, and information hierarchy to reduce user confusion and support task completion
- **Information Architecture** — Restructuring of claims data presentation and categorization to align with users' mental models and expected journey through the claims process
- **Digital Experience Strategy** — Development of proactive communication touchpoints and self-service capabilities that address common navigation pain points and reduce support channel volume
