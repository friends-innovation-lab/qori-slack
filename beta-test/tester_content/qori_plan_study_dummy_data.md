# Research Plan - `/plan-study` Command

## Study Planning Details

**Project Title:** VA Housing Application UX Research - Pain Point Identification and Process Optimization

**Product / Feature Area:** VA.gov Housing Benefits Application Portal - specifically the online application workflow, eligibility screening, document upload system, and application status tracking

**What decision will this research inform?** 
This research will inform the product team's decision on which areas of the housing application process to prioritize for redesign in Q4 2025. Findings will directly influence the roadmap for improving application completion rates, reducing support calls, and enhancing accessibility compliance. The research will also guide decisions about whether to implement a new application wizard, redesign the status tracking system, or focus on mobile optimization first.

**Primary research goal:** 
Identify the top 3-5 usability barriers that prevent veterans from successfully completing housing benefit applications online, and understand the root causes behind the current 34% application abandonment rate.

## Methodology Selection

**Selected Methods:**
- ✅ **User Interviews** - To understand veteran mental models and pain points
- ✅ **Usability Testing** - To observe task completion and identify friction points  
- ✅ **Competitive Analysis** - To benchmark against other government and housing platforms
- ⚪ Concept Testing - Not applicable for this study
- ⚪ Survey Research - May follow as phase 2 for quantitative validation
- ⚪ Card Sorting - Not needed for current research questions

**Target Participants:**
- Primary: Veterans who have attempted to apply for VA housing benefits within the last 12 months (completed or abandoned)
- Secondary: Veterans currently receiving disability compensation (30%+ rating preferred)
- Inclusion criteria: Basic computer/smartphone proficiency, willing to share screen during sessions
- Exclusion criteria: VA employees, professional UX/web designers
- Accessibility focus: Minimum 40% of participants must use assistive technologies or have documented accessibility needs

**Number of Participants:**
- **User Interviews:** 5 participants (1-hour sessions)
- **Usability Testing:** 5 participants (45-minute task-based sessions) 
- **Total:** 10 participants across both methods
- **Compensation:** $75 per participant per session

**Timeline Preference:** 
⚪ Rush (2-3 weeks)  
✅ **Standard (4-6 weeks)**  
⚪ Extended (7+ weeks)

*Rationale: Standard timeline allows for proper recruitment of veterans with diverse accessibility needs, adequate time for analysis, and coordination with stakeholder schedules.*

**Lead Researcher:** Jordan Martinez, Senior UX Researcher

**Team/Office:** 
- **Primary Team:** VA Digital Experience Team  
- **Collaborating Office:** Office of Veterans Experience (VEO)
- **Support:** VA Housing Benefits Administration
- **Technical Partner:** VA.gov Platform Team
- **Accessibility Consultant:** Section 508 Compliance Office

## Additional Research Plan Details

**Research Questions:**
1. Where do veterans get stuck in the current application process?
2. What information or guidance is missing that causes confusion?
3. How do veterans with disabilities experience the current application flow?
4. What workarounds have veterans developed to complete applications?
5. How do veterans prefer to track application status and receive updates?

**Success Metrics:**
- Identify specific pages/steps with highest abandonment rates
- Document time-to-complete for each application section
- Measure task success rates across different user groups
- Catalog accessibility barriers and compliance gaps

**Deliverables:**
- Research plan and discussion guides
- Individual session recordings and notes
- Synthesized findings report with prioritized recommendations
- User journey maps highlighting pain points
- Executive summary with actionable next steps

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Backup participant recruitment through multiple veteran organizations
- Technical setup testing 24 hours before each session
- Alternative methods planned for participants with connectivity issues
- Privacy and PII handling procedures established per VA guidelines
